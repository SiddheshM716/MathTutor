Question 3.
Find the sum of first 28 terms of an A.P. whose nth term is 4n – 3.
Solution:
n = 28
tn = 4n – 3
t1 = 4 × 1 – 3 = 1
t2 = 4 × 2 – 3 = 5
t28 = 4 × 28 – 3
= 112 – 3 = 109
∴ a = 1, d = t2 – t1 = 5 – 1 = 4
l = 109.
Sn = \(\frac{n}{2}\) (2a+(n – 1)d)
S28 = \(\frac{28}{2}\) (2 × 1 + 27 × 4)
= 14(2 + 108)
= 14 × 110
= 1540
