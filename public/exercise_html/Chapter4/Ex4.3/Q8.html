Question 8.
In the adjacent figure, ABC is a right angled triangle with right angle at B and points D, E trisect BC. Prove that 8AE2 = 3AC2 + 5AD2.
Solution:
Since the Points D, E trisect BC.
BD = DE = CE
Let BD = DE = CE = x
BE = 2x and BC = 3x

In the right ∆ABD,
AD2 = AB2 + BD2
AD2 = AB2 + x2 ……….(1)
In the right ∆ABE,
AE2 = AB2 + 2BE2
AE2 = AB2 + 4X2 ………..(2) (BE = 2x)
In the right ∆ABC
AC2 = AB2 + BC2
AC2 = AB2 + 9x2 …………… (3) (BC = 3x)
R.H.S = 3AC2 + 5AD2
= 3[AB2 + 9x2] + 5 [AB2 + x2] [From (1) and (3)]
= 3AB2 + 27x2 + 5AB2 + 5x2
= 8AB2 + 32x2
= 8 (AB2 + 4 x2)
= 8AE2 [From (2)]
= R.H.S.
∴ 8AE2 = 3AC2 + 5AD2
