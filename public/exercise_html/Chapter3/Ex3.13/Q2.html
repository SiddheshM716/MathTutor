Question 2.
Find the value(s) of ‘k’ for which the roots of the following equations are real and equal.
(i) (5k – 6)2 + 2kx + 1 = 0
(ii) kx2 + (6k + 2)x + 16 = 0
Solution:
(5k – 6)x2 + 2kx + 1 :
a = (5k – 6), b = 2, c = 1
Δ = b2 – 4ac
⇒ (2k)2 – 4 (5k – 6)(1)
⇒ 4k2 – 20k + 24 = 0 [∵ Since the roots are real and equal)
⇒ k2 – 5k + 6 = 0
⇒ (k – 3)(k – 2) = 0
k = 3, 2

(ii) kx2 + (6k + 2)x + 16 = 0
a = k, b = (6k + 2), c = 16
Δ = b2 – 4ac [∵ the roots are real and equal)
⇒ (6k + 2)2 – 4 × k × 16 = 0
⇒ 36k2 + 24k + 4 – 64k = 0
⇒ 36k2 – 40k + 4 =0
⇒ 36k2 – 36k – 4k + 4 =0
⇒ 36k (k – 1) – 4 (k – 1) = 0
⇒ 4 (k – 1) (9k – 1) =0
⇒ k = 1 or k = \(\frac{1}{9}\)
