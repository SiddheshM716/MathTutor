Show that the points P(-1.5, 3), Q(6, -2), R(-3, 4) are collinear.

1

Identify the coordinates of the points P, Q, and R.

P(-1.5, 3), Q(6, -2), R(-3, 4).

2

Use the area formula for a triangle formed by three points:

\[

\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \{(x_1y_2 + x_2y_3 + x_3y_1) - (x_2y_1 + x_3y_2 + x_1y_3)\}

\]

3

Substitute the coordinates into the formula and calculate:

\[

\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \{(-1.5 \times -2 + 6 \times 4 + -3 \times 3) - (6 \times 3 + -3 \times -2 + -1.5 \times 4)\}

\]

= \frac{1}{2} \{(3 + 24 - 9) - (18 + 6 - 6)\} = \frac{1}{2} \{18 - 18\} = 0
