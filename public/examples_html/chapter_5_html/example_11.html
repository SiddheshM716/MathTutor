The line p passes through the points (3,−2), (12,4) and the line q passes through the points (6,−2) and (12,2). Is p parallel to q?

1

Find the slope of line p using the formula \( m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \).

The slope of line p is \( m_p = \frac{4 - (-2)}{12 - 3} = \frac{6}{9} = \frac{2}{3} \).

2

Find the slope of line q using the same formula.

The slope of line q is \( m_q = \frac{2 - (-2)}{12 - 6} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \).

3

Compare the slopes of both lines.

Since \( m_p = m_q = \frac{2}{3} \), the slopes are equal.

Yes, line p is parallel to line q because their slopes are equal.
