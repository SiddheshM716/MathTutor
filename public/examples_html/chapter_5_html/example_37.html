(i) Find the equation of the line AB, where A(0,5) and B(4,1).

(ii) Find the equation of a line perpendicular to AB and passing through the point (4,4).

(iii) Find the coordinates of the point of contact P of the tangent line AB with the circle, given that P is the intersection of the lines from (i) and (ii).

1

Find the equation of line AB using the two-point form.

The two-point form is \(\frac{y - y_1}{y_2 - y_1} = \frac{x - x_1}{x_2 - x_1}\).

Substituting A(0,5) and B(4,1):

\[

\frac{y - 5}{1 - 5} = \frac{x - 0}{4 - 0}

\]

2

Simplify the equation.

\[

\frac{y - 5}{-4} = \frac{x}{4}

\]

\[

y - 5 = -x

\]

\[

x + y - 5 = 0

\]

Final Answer for (i): The equation of line AB is \(x + y - 5 = 0\).

---

3

Find the equation of a line perpendicular to AB.

A line perpendicular to \(x + y - 5 = 0\) has the form \(x - y + k = 0\).

4

Substitute the point (4,4) to find \(k\).

\[

4 - 4 + k = 0 \Rightarrow k = 0

\]

Final Answer for (ii): The equation of the perpendicular line is \(x - y = 0\).

---

5

Find the intersection point P of the lines \(x + y - 5 = 0\) and \(x - y = 0\).

6

Solve the system of equations.

\[

x + y = 5

\]

\[

x - y = 0

\]

Adding the equations:

\[

2x = 5 \Rightarrow x = \frac{5}{2}

\]

Substituting \(x = \frac{5}{2}\) into \(x - y = 0\):

\[

\frac{5}{2} - y = 0 \Rightarrow y = \frac{5}{2}

\]

Final Answer for (iii): The coordinates of P are \(\left(\frac{5}{2}, \frac{5}{2}\right)\).
