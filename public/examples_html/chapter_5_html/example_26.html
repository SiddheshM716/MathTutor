Find the intercepts made by the line \(4x - 9y + 36 = 0\) on the coordinate axes.

1

Start with the given equation of the line:

\[ 4x - 9y + 36 = 0 \]

2

Rearrange the equation to the standard intercept form \( \frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{b} = 1 \). First, move the constant term to the other side:

\[ 4x - 9y = -36 \]

3

Divide the entire equation by \(-36\) to normalize it:

\[ \frac{x}{-9} + \frac{y}{4} = 1 \]

Comparing with the intercept form, the \(x\)-intercept is \(a = -9\) and the \(y\)-intercept is \(b = 4\).
