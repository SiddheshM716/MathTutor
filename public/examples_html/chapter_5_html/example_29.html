(a) (i) East Avenue is the straight line joining C(0,2) and B(7,2). Find the equation of East Avenue.

(ii) If point D lies vertically above C(0,2), find the equation of North Street.

(iii) The center of a circular garden M is at (7,7), and point A is at (3,10). Find the equation of Cross Road, which is perpendicular to MA and passes through A.

(b) (i) If D is a point on the Cross Road with x-coordinate 0, find the coordinates of D.

(ii) Find the coordinates of E where the Cross Road meets East Avenue.

1

For part (a)(i), use the two-point form of a line equation for points C(0,2) and B(7,2).

The two-point form is \(\frac{y-y_1}{y_2-y_1} = \frac{x-x_1}{x_2-x_1}\).

Substituting the points: \(\frac{y-2}{2-2} = \frac{x-0}{7-0}\).

Simplifying: \(\frac{y-2}{0} = \frac{x}{7}\), which gives \(y = 2\).

2

For part (a)(ii), since D lies vertically above C(0,2), its x-coordinate is 0.

Thus, North Street is the vertical line \(x = 0\).

3

For part (a)(iii), first find the slope of MA, where M is (7,7) and A is (3,10).

Slope \(m_1 = \frac{10-7}{3-7} = -\frac{3}{4}\).

Since Cross Road is perpendicular to MA, its slope \(m_2\) satisfies \(m_1m_2 = -1\).

Solving: \(-\frac{3}{4}m_2 = -1\) gives \(m_2 = \frac{4}{3}\).

Using point-slope form for Cross Road passing through A(3,10): \(y - 10 = \frac{4}{3}(x - 3)\).

Simplifying: \(3y - 30 = 4x - 12\), so \(4x - 3y + 18 = 0\).

4

For part (b)(i), substitute \(x = 0\) into the Cross Road equation \(4x - 3y + 18 = 0\).

This gives \(0 - 3k + 18 = 0\), so \(k = 6\).

Thus, D is (0,6).

5

For part (b)(ii), substitute \(y = 2\) into the Cross Road equation \(4x - 3y + 18 = 0\).

This gives \(4q - 6 + 18 = 0\), so \(4q = -12\) and \(q = -3\).

Thus, E is (-3,2).

(a)(i) \(y = 2\)

(a)(ii) \(x = 0\)

(a)(iii) \(4x - 3y + 18 = 0\)

(b)(i) D is (0,6)

(b)(ii) E is (-3,2)
