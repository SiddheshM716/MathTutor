A mobile phone is put to use when the battery power is 100%. The percent of battery power \( y \) (in decimal) remaining after using the mobile phone for \( x \) hours is assumed as \( y = -0.25x + 1 \).

(i) Find the number of hours elapsed if the battery power is 40%.

1

To find the time when the battery power is 40%, substitute \( y = 0.40 \) into the equation \( y = -0.25x + 1 \).

2

The equation becomes \( 0.40 = -0.25x + 1 \). Rearrange to solve for \( x \).

3

Subtract 1 from both sides: \( 0.40 - 1 = -0.25x \), which simplifies to \( -0.60 = -0.25x \).

Divide both sides by -0.25: \( x = \frac{-0.60}{-0.25} = 2.4 \) hours.

The number of hours elapsed is \( 2.4 \) hours.

(ii) Determine how long it takes for the battery power to reach 0%.

1

To find when the battery power is 0%, substitute \( y = 0 \) into the equation \( y = -0.25x + 1 \).

2

The equation becomes \( 0 = -0.25x + 1 \). Rearrange to solve for \( x \).

3

Subtract 1 from both sides: \( -1 = -0.25x \).

Divide both sides by -0.25: \( x = \frac{-1}{-0.25} = 4 \) hours.

The battery power reaches 0% after \( 4 \) hours.
