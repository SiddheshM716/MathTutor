Example 5.32

Show that the straight lines \(2x + 3y - 8 = 0\) and \(4x + 6y + 18 = 0\) are parallel.

Solution: Slope of the straight line \(2x + 3y - 8 = 0\) is

\[ m_1 = -\frac{\text{coefficient of } x}{\text{coefficient of } y} = -\frac{2}{3} \]

Slope of the straight line \(4x + 6y + 18 = 0\) is

\[ m_2 = -\frac{\text{coefficient of } x}{\text{coefficient of } y} = -\frac{4}{6} = -\frac{2}{3} \]

Here, \(m_1 = m_2\). Therefore, the slopes are equal. Hence, the two straight lines are parallel.
