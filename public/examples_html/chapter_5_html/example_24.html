Two buildings of different heights are located at opposite sides of each other. If a heavy rod is attached joining the terrace of the buildings from (6,10) to (14,12), find the equation of the rod joining the buildings?

1

Identify the given points as A(6,10) and B(14,12). The equation of the rod is the equation of the straight line passing through these two points.

2

Use the two-point form of the equation of a line:

\[

\frac{y - y_1}{y_2 - y_1} = \frac{x - x_1}{x_2 - x_1}

\]

3

Substitute the coordinates of points A and B into the formula:

\[

\frac{y - 10}{12 - 10} = \frac{x - 6}{14 - 6}

\]

\[

\frac{y - 10}{2} = \frac{x - 6}{8}

\]

Simplify and rearrange to get the final equation:

\[

x - 4y + 34 = 0

\]

The equation of the rod is \(x - 4y + 34 = 0\).
