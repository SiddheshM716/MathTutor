Find the equation of a straight line whose:

Slope is 5 and y-intercept is −9

Inclination is 45° and y-intercept is 11

1

Given slope \( m = 5 \) and y-intercept \( c = -9 \).

2

The equation of a straight line is given by \( y = mx + c \).

3

Substituting the values, we get \( y = 5x - 9 \).

The equation is \( 5x - y - 9 = 0 \).

Part (ii)

Step 1: Given inclination \( \theta = 45° \) and y-intercept \( c = 11 \).

Step 2: First, find the slope using \( m = \tan \theta \). Here, \( m = \tan 45° = 1 \).

Step 3: Using the line equation \( y = mx + c \), substitute \( m = 1 \) and \( c = 11 \).

The equation is \( x - y + 11 = 0 \).
