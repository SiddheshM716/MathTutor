Without using Pythagoras theorem, show that the points (1, -4), (2, -3) and (4, -7) form a right angled triangle.

1

Label the given points as A(1, -4), B(2, -3), and C(4, -7).

2

Calculate the slope of AB using the formula:

\[ \text{Slope} = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \]

\[ \text{Slope of AB} = \frac{-3 - (-4)}{2 - 1} = \frac{1}{1} = 1 \]

3

Calculate the slope of BC:

\[ \text{Slope of BC} = \frac{-7 - (-3)}{4 - 2} = \frac{-4}{2} = -2 \]

4

Calculate the slope of AC:

\[ \text{Slope of AC} = \frac{-7 - (-4)}{4 - 1} = \frac{-3}{3} = -1 \]

5

Check if the product of the slopes of AB and AC equals -1 (condition for perpendicularity):

\[ \text{Slope of AB} \times \text{Slope of AC} = 1 \times (-1) = -1 \]
