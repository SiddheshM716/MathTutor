Find the equation of a line passing through the point A(1,4) and perpendicular to the line joining points (2,5) and (4,7).

1

Identify the given points and find the slope of line BC joining points B(2,5) and C(4,7).

Slope of line BC = \(\frac{7 - 5}{4 - 2} = \frac{2}{2} = 1\).

2

Since the required line is perpendicular to BC, the product of their slopes must be -1.

Let \(m\) be the slope of the required line. Then, \(m \times 1 = -1\), so \(m = -1\).

3

Use the point-slope form of the line equation with point A(1,4) and slope \(m = -1\).

The equation is \(y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)\).

Substituting the values: \(y - 4 = -1(x - 1)\).

Simplify: \(y - 4 = -x + 1\).

Rearrange: \(x + y - 5 = 0\).

The equation of the line is \(\boxed{x + y - 5 = 0}\).
