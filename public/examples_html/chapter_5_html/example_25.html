Find the equation of a line which passes through (5,7) and makes intercepts on the axes equal in magnitude but opposite in sign.

1

Let the x-intercept be \(a\) and y-intercept be \(-a\) since the intercepts are equal in magnitude but opposite in sign.

2

The equation of the line in intercept form is \(\frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{b} = 1\). Here, \(b = -a\), so the equation becomes \(\frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{-a} = 1\).

3

Simplify the equation:

\[

\frac{x}{a} - \frac{y}{a} = 1

\]

Multiply both sides by \(a\):

\[

x - y = a

\]

4

Since the line passes through the point (5,7), substitute \(x = 5\) and \(y = 7\) into the equation:

\[

5 - 7 = a \Rightarrow a = -2

\]

The required equation of the line is \(x - y = -2\) or equivalently \(x - y + 2 = 0\).
