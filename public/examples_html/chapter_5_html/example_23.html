Example 5.23

Find the equation of a straight line passing through (5, -3) and (7, -4).

The equation of a straight line passing through the two points (x1, y1) and (x2, y2) is:

\(\frac{y - y_1}{y_2 - y_1} = \frac{x - x_1}{x_2 - x_1}\)

Substituting the points we get:

\(\frac{y + 3}{-4 + 3} = \frac{x - 5}{7 - 5}\)

\(\frac{y + 3}{-1} = \frac{x - 5}{2}\)

\(2(y + 3) = -1(x - 5)\)

\(2y + 6 = -x + 5\)

\(x + 2y + 1 = 0\)
