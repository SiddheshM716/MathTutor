Find the equation of a straight line passing through (5, -3) and (7, -4).

1

The equation of a straight line passing through two points \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_2, y_2)\) is given by:

\[

\frac{y - y_1}{y_2 - y_1} = \frac{x - x_1}{x_2 - x_1}

\]

2

Substitute the given points \((5, -3)\) and \((7, -4)\) into the formula:

\[

\frac{y - (-3)}{-4 - (-3)} = \frac{x - 5}{7 - 5}

\]

3

Simplify the equation step by step:

\[

\frac{y + 3}{-1} = \frac{x - 5}{2}

\]

\[

2(y + 3) = -1(x - 5)

\]

\[

2y + 6 = -x + 5

\]

\[

x + 2y + 1 = 0

\]

The equation of the line is \(x + 2y + 1 = 0\).
