A line makes positive intercepts on coordinate axes whose sum is 7 and it passes through (âˆ’3,8). Find its equation.

1

Let the intercepts on the x-axis and y-axis be \(a\) and \(b\) respectively. Given that \(a + b = 7\), we can express \(b\) as \(b = 7 - a\).

2

The intercept form of a line is \(\frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{b} = 1\). Substituting \(b = 7 - a\), the equation becomes \(\frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{7 - a} = 1\).

3

Since the line passes through the point \((-3, 8)\), substitute \(x = -3\) and \(y = 8\) into the equation:

\[

\frac{-3}{a} + \frac{8}{7 - a} = 1

\]

4

Multiply through by \(a(7 - a)\) to eliminate denominators:

\[

-3(7 - a) + 8a = a(7 - a)

\]

\[

-21 + 3a + 8a = 7a - a^2

\]

\[

a^2 + 4a - 21 = 0

\]

5

Solve the quadratic equation:

\[

(a - 3)(a + 7) = 0

\]

\[

a = 3 \quad \text{or} \quad a = -7

\]

6

Since \(a\) must be positive, we take \(a = 3\). Then, \(b = 7 - 3 = 4\).

The equation of the line in intercept form is \(\frac{x}{3} + \frac{y}{4} = 1\), which simplifies to \(4x + 3y - 12 = 0\).
