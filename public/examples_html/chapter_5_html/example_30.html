Find the slope of the straight line \(6x + 8y + 7 = 0\).

1

The given equation of the line is \(6x + 8y + 7 = 0\). To find the slope, we first rewrite it in the slope-intercept form \(y = mx + c\), where \(m\) is the slope.

2

The formula for converting the general form \(Ax + By + C = 0\) to slope-intercept form is \(y = -\frac{A}{B}x - \frac{C}{B}\).

3

Substituting the given values \(A = 6\), \(B = 8\), and \(C = 7\), we get:

\[

y = -\frac{6}{8}x - \frac{7}{8} = -\frac{3}{4}x - \frac{7}{8}

\]

From this, the slope \(m = -\frac{3}{4}\).

The slope of the straight line is \(-\frac{3}{4}\).
