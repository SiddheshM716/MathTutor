Example 5.9

Find the slope of a line joining the given points

(i) (-6,1) and (3,2)

Solution:

The slope = \(\frac{2 - 1}{3 - (-6)} = \frac{1}{9}\)

(ii) (-4,2) and (2,3)

Solution:

The slope = \(\frac{3 - 2}{2 - (-4)} = \frac{1}{6}\)

(iii) (14,10) and (14,-6)

Solution:

The slope = \(\frac{-6 - 10}{14 - 14} = \frac{-16}{0}\)

The slope is undefined.
