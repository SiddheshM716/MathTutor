Example 5.35

Find the equation of a straight line perpendicular to the line \( y = \frac{4}{3}x - 7 \) and passing through the point \( (7, -1) \).

Solution: The equation \( y = \frac{4}{3}x - 7 \) can be written as \( 4x - 3y - 21 = 0 \).

Equation of a straight line perpendicular to \( 4x - 3y - 21 = 0 \) is \( 3x + 4y + k = 0 \).

Since it passes through the point \( (7, -1) \),

\( 21 - 4 + k = 0 \) we get, \( k = -17 \).

Therefore, the equation of the required straight line is \( 3x + 4y - 17 = 0 \).
