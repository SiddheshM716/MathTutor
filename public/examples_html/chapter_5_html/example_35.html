Find the equation of a straight line perpendicular to the line \( y = \frac{4}{3}x - 7 \) and passing through the point \( (7, -1) \).

1

Rewrite the given line equation in standard form.

The equation \( y = \frac{4}{3}x - 7 \) can be rewritten as \( 4x - 3y - 21 = 0 \).

2

Determine the equation of the perpendicular line.

The formula for a line perpendicular to \( ax + by + c = 0 \) is \( bx - ay + k = 0 \).

Applying this, the perpendicular line to \( 4x - 3y - 21 = 0 \) is \( 3x + 4y + k = 0 \).

3

Substitute the point \( (7, -1) \) into the perpendicular line equation to find \( k \).

\( 3(7) + 4(-1) + k = 0 \)

\( 21 - 4 + k = 0 \)

\( k = -17 \).

The equation of the required straight line is \( 3x + 4y - 17 = 0 \).
