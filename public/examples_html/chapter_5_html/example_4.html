Example 5.4

If the points P(−1,−4), Q(b,c) and R(5,−1) are collinear and if 2b + c = 4, then find the values of b and c.

Since the three points P(−1,−4), Q(b,c) and R(5,−1) are collinear, the area of triangle PQR = 0.

\(\frac{1}{2} \left( (-1 \cdot c + b \cdot (-1) + 5 \cdot (-4)) - (-4 \cdot b + c \cdot 5 + (-1) \cdot (-1)) \right) = 0\)

\(\frac{1}{2} \left( (-c - b - 20) - (-4b + 5c + 1) \right) = 0\)

\(-c - b - 20 + 4b - 5c - 1 = 0\)

\(b - 2c = 7 \quad \text{(1)}\)

Also, \(2b + c = 4 \quad \text{(2)}\) (from given information)

Solving (1) and (2) we get \(b = 3\), \(c = -2\).
