If the points P(−1,−4), Q(b,c) and R(5,−1) are collinear and if 2b + c = 4, then find the values of b and c.

1

Since the points P, Q, and R are collinear, the area of triangle PQR must be zero.

2

Use the area formula for three collinear points (x₁,y₁), (x₂,y₂), (x₃,y₃):

\[

\frac{1}{2} \left( (x_1(y_2 - y_3) + x_2(y_3 - y_1) + x_3(y_1 - y_2)) \right) = 0

\]

3

Substitute the given points P(−1,−4), Q(b,c), R(5,−1) into the formula:

\[

\frac{1}{2} \left( (-1 \cdot (c - (-1)) + b \cdot (-1 - (-4)) + 5 \cdot (-4 - c) \right) = 0

\]

Simplify the equation:

\[

\frac{1}{2} \left( (-c - 1) + 3b + (-20 - 5c) \right) = 0

\]

\[

\frac{1}{2} \left( 3b - 6c - 21 \right) = 0

\]

Multiply both sides by 2:

\[

3b - 6c - 21 = 0

\]

Simplify further:

\[

b - 2c = 7 \quad \text{(1)}

\]

4

Use the given equation \(2b + c = 4\) (2) and solve the system of equations (1) and (2).

Multiply equation (1) by 2:

\[

2b - 4c = 14

\]

Subtract equation (2) from this result:

\[

(2b - 4c) - (2b + c) = 14 - 4

\]

\[

-5c = 10

\]

\[

c = -2

\]

Substitute \(c = -2\) into equation (2):

\[

2b + (-2) = 4

\]

\[

2b = 6

\]

\[

b = 3

\]

\(b = 3\), \(c = -2\).
