Prove analytically that the line segment joining the mid-points of two sides of a triangle is parallel to the third side and is equal to half of its length.

1

Let \( P(a,b) \), \( Q(c,d) \), and \( R(e, f) \) be the vertices of a triangle. Let \( S \) be the mid-point of \( PQ \) and \( T \) be the mid-point of \( PR \).

2

The coordinates of the mid-points are calculated using the mid-point formula:

\[ S = \left( \frac{a + c}{2}, \frac{b + d}{2} \right) \]

\[ T = \left( \frac{a + e}{2}, \frac{b + f}{2} \right) \]

3

The slope of \( ST \) is calculated as:

\[ \text{Slope of } ST = \frac{\frac{b + f}{2} - \frac{b + d}{2}}{\frac{a + e}{2} - \frac{a + c}{2}} = \frac{f - d}{e - c} \]

4

The slope of \( QR \) is calculated as:

\[ \text{Slope of } QR = \frac{f - d}{e - c} \]

5

Since the slopes of \( ST \) and \( QR \) are equal, \( ST \) is parallel to \( QR \).

6

The length of \( ST \) is calculated using the distance formula:

\[ ST = \sqrt{\left( \frac{a + e}{2} - \frac{a + c}{2} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{b + f}{2} - \frac{b + d}{2} \right)^2} = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{(e - c)^2 + (f - d)^2} \]

7

The length of \( QR \) is:

\[ QR = \sqrt{(e - c)^2 + (f - d)^2} \]

Therefore, \( ST \) is parallel to \( QR \) and \( ST = \frac{1}{2} QR \).
