Example 5.16

Prove analytically that the line segment joining the mid-points of two sides of a triangle is parallel to the third side and is equal to half of its length.

Solution Let P(a,b), Q(c,d) and R(e, f) be the vertices of a triangle. Let S be the mid-point of PQ and T be the mid-point of PR.

Therefore, S = \(\left( \frac{a + c}{2}, \frac{b + d}{2} \right)\) and T = \(\left( \frac{a + e}{2}, \frac{b + f}{2} \right)\).

Now, slope of ST = \(\frac{\frac{b + f}{2} - \frac{b + d}{2}}{\frac{a + e}{2} - \frac{a + c}{2}} = \frac{f - d}{e - c}\).

And slope of QR = \(\frac{f - d}{e - c}\).

Therefore, ST is parallel to QR (since their slopes are equal).

Also, ST = \(\sqrt{\left( \frac{a + e}{2} - \frac{a + c}{2} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{b + f}{2} - \frac{b + d}{2} \right)^2} = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{(e - c)^2 + (f - d)^2}\).

ST = \(\frac{1}{2}\) QR.
