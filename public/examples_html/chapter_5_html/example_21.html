1

Identify the given point and slope.

Given point: $(x_1, y_1) = (3, -4)$

Given slope: $m = 2$

2

Use the point-slope form of a line equation.

The formula is:

$$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$$

3

Substitute the given values into the formula.

$$y - (-4) = 2(x - 3)$$

Simplify the equation:

$$y + 4 = 2x - 6$$

Rearrange to standard form:

$$2x - y - 10 = 0$$

The equation of the line is $\boxed{2x - y - 10 = 0}$.
