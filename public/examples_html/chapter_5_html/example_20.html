The graph relates temperatures y (in Fahrenheit degree) to temperatures x (in Celsius degree).

(a) Find the slope and y-intercept.

(b) Write an equation of the line.

(c) What is the mean temperature of the earth in Fahrenheit degree if its mean temperature is 25° Celsius?

1

From the graph, identify the slope and y-intercept of the line.

The slope is \(\frac{9}{5}\) and the y-intercept is 32.

2

Use the slope-intercept form of a line to write the equation.

The equation of the line is \(y = \frac{9}{5}x + 32\).

3

Substitute x = 25 into the equation to find the corresponding Fahrenheit temperature.

\(y = \frac{9}{5}(25) + 32 = 45 + 32 = 77\).

The mean temperature of the earth is 77°F.
