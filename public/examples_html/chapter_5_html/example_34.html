Find the equation of a straight line which is parallel to the line \(3x - 7y = 12\) and passing through the point \((6,4)\).

1

The given line is \(3x - 7y = 12\). A line parallel to this will have the same coefficients for \(x\) and \(y\), so its equation will be of the form \(3x - 7y + k = 0\).

2

Since the line passes through the point \((6,4)\), substitute \(x = 6\) and \(y = 4\) into the equation to find \(k\).

3

Substituting the values:

\[3(6) - 7(4) + k = 0\]

\[18 - 28 + k = 0\]

\[-10 + k = 0\]

\[k = 10\]

The equation of the required line is \(3x - 7y + 10 = 0\).
