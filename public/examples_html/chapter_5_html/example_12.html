Show that the points (−2,5), (6,−1) and (2,2) are collinear.

1

Identify the given points as A(−2,5), B(6,−1), and C(2,2).

2

Calculate the slope of AB using the formula:

\[ \text{Slope} = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \]

3

Substitute the coordinates of A and B into the slope formula:

\[ \text{Slope of AB} = \frac{−1 − 5}{6 − (−2)} = \frac{−6}{8} = −\frac{3}{4} \]

4

Calculate the slope of BC using the same formula:

\[ \text{Slope of BC} = \frac{2 − (−1)}{2 − 6} = \frac{3}{−4} = −\frac{3}{4} \]

5

Compare the slopes of AB and BC. Since they are equal, the points lie on the same straight line.

The points (−2,5), (6,−1), and (2,2) are collinear.
