The line \( r \) passes through the points \( (-2, 2) \) and \( (5, 8) \), and the line \( s \) passes through the points \( (-8, 7) \) and \( (-2, 0) \). Is the line \( r \) perpendicular to \( s \)?

1

Find the slope of line \( r \) using the formula for slope between two points \( (x_1, y_1) \) and \( (x_2, y_2) \):

\[ m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \]

2

Calculate the slope of line \( r \):

\[ m_r = \frac{8 - 2}{5 - (-2)} = \frac{6}{7} \]

3

Find the slope of line \( s \) using the same formula:

\[ m_s = \frac{0 - 7}{-2 - (-8)} = \frac{-7}{6} \]

4

Check if the lines are perpendicular by verifying if the product of their slopes is \( -1 \):

\[ m_r \times m_s = \frac{6}{7} \times \frac{-7}{6} = -1 \]

Since \( m_r \times m_s = -1 \), the line \( r \) is perpendicular to line \( s \).
