Example 5.10

The line r passes through the points (−2, 2) and (5, 8) and the line s passes through the points (−8, 7) and (−2, 0). Is the line r perpendicular to s?

Solution: The slope of line r is \( m_r = \frac{8 - 2}{5 + 2} = \frac{6}{7} \).

The slope of line s is \( m_s = \frac{0 - 7}{-2 + 8} = \frac{-7}{6} \).

The product of slopes \( m_r \times m_s = \frac{6}{7} \times \frac{-7}{6} = -1 \).

That is, \( m_r \times m_s = -1 \).

Therefore, the line r is perpendicular to line s.
