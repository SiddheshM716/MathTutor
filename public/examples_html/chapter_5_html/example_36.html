Find the equation of a straight line parallel to the Y-axis and passing through the point of intersection of the lines \(4x + 5y = 13\) and \(x - 8y + 9 = 0\).

1

Identify the given lines:

\[

4x + 5y - 13 = 0 \quad \text{(1)}

\]

\[

x - 8y + 9 = 0 \quad \text{(2)}

\]

2

Find the point of intersection of the two lines using the cross-multiplication method:

\[

\frac{x}{(5)(9) - (-8)(-13)} = \frac{y}{(-13)(1) - (4)(9)} = \frac{1}{(4)(-8) - (5)(1)}

\]

\[

\frac{x}{45 - 104} = \frac{y}{-13 - 36} = \frac{1}{-32 - 5}

\]

\[

\frac{x}{-59} = \frac{y}{-49} = \frac{1}{-37}

\]

3

Solve for \(x\) and \(y\):

\[

x = \frac{59}{37}, \quad y = \frac{49}{37}

\]

4

The equation of a line parallel to the Y-axis is \(x = c\). Since it passes through \(\left(\frac{59}{37}, \frac{49}{37}\right)\), we have:

\[

c = \frac{59}{37}

\]

The equation of the line is:

\[

37x - 59 = 0

\]
