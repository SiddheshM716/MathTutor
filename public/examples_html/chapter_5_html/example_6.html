Find the area of the quadrilateral formed by the points (8,6), (5,11), (−5,12) and (−4,3).

1

Label the vertices of the quadrilateral as A(8,6), B(5,11), C(−5,12), and D(−4,3).

2

Use the shoelace formula for the area of a quadrilateral:

\[

\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \left| (x_1y_2 + x_2y_3 + x_3y_4 + x_4y_1) - (y_1x_2 + y_2x_3 + y_3x_4 + y_4x_1) \right|

\]

3

Substitute the coordinates into the formula:

\[

\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \left| (8 \times 11 + 5 \times 12 + (-5) \times 3 + (-4) \times 6) - (6 \times 5 + 11 \times (-5) + 12 \times (-4) + 3 \times 8) \right|

\]

4

Calculate the terms:

\[

= \frac{1}{2} \left| (88 + 60 - 15 - 24) - (30 - 55 - 48 + 24) \right|

\]

\[

= \frac{1}{2} \left| 109 - (-49) \right|

\]

\[

= \frac{1}{2} \times 158

\]

The area of the quadrilateral is \(79\) sq.units.
