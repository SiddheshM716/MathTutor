Solve the matrix equation:

\[

\begin{pmatrix}

2 & 1 \\

1 & 2

\end{pmatrix}

\begin{pmatrix}

x \\

y

\end{pmatrix}

=

\begin{pmatrix}

4 \\

5

\end{pmatrix}

\]

1

Perform matrix multiplication to convert the equation into a system of linear equations.

\[

\begin{pmatrix}

2x + y \\

x + 2y

\end{pmatrix}

=

\begin{pmatrix}

4 \\

5

\end{pmatrix}

\]

This gives the system:

\[

\begin{cases}

2x + y = 4 \quad \text{(1)} \\

x + 2y = 5 \quad \text{(2)}

\end{cases}

\]

2

Solve the system using elimination. Multiply equation (2) by 2 to align coefficients for elimination.

\[

2x + 4y = 10 \quad \text{(2a)}

\]

3

Subtract equation (1) from equation (2a) to eliminate \( x \).

\[

(2x + 4y) - (2x + y) = 10 - 4 \\

3y = 6 \\

y = 2

\]

4

Substitute \( y = 2 \) back into equation (1) to solve for \( x \).

\[

2x + 2 = 4 \\

2x = 2 \\

x = 1

\]

\[

x = 1, \quad y = 2

\]
