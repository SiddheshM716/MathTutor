Example 3.70

Solve the matrix equation:

\[ \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix} \]

Solution

Performing matrix multiplication:

\[ \begin{pmatrix} 2x + y \\ x + 2y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix} \]

This gives us the system of equations:

\[ \begin{cases} 2x + y = 4 & \text{(1)} \\ x + 2y = 5 & \text{(2)} \end{cases} \]

Solving the system:

Multiply equation (2) by 2:

\[ 2x + 4y = 10 \quad \text{(2a)} \]

Subtract equation (1) from (2a):

\[ (2x + 4y) - (2x + y) = 10 - 4 \\ 3y = 6 \\ y = 2 \]

Substitute y = 2 into equation (1):

\[ 2x + 2 = 4 \\ 2x = 2 \\ x = 1 \]

Solution

\[ x = 1, \quad y = 2 \]
