Find the LCM of the following:

(i) \(8x^2y^3\), \(48x^3y^2\)

(ii) \(5x^2 - 10\), \(5x^2 - 20\)

(iii) \(x^4 - 1\), \(x^2 - 2x + 1\)

(iv) \(x^3 - 27\), \((x - 3)^2\), \(x^2 - 9\)

1

For \(8x^2y^3\) and \(48x^3y^2\), find the LCM of the coefficients and the highest powers of variables.

LCM of 8 and 48 is 48.

LCM of \(x^2y^3\) and \(x^3y^2\) is \(x^3y^3\).

\(48x^3y^3\)

---

Step 1: Factorize \(5x^2 - 10\) and \(5x^2 - 20\).

\(5x^2 - 10 = 5(x^2 - 2)\)

\(5x^2 - 20 = 5(x^2 - 4) = 5(x + 2)(x - 2)\)

Step 2: The LCM is the product of the highest powers of all factors.

\(5(x + 2)(x - 2)\)

---

Step 1: Factorize \(x^4 - 1\) and \(x^2 - 2x + 1\).

\(x^4 - 1 = (x^2 + 1)(x^2 - 1) = (x^2 + 1)(x + 1)(x - 1)\)

\(x^2 - 2x + 1 = (x - 1)^2\)

Step 2: The LCM is the product of the highest powers of all factors.

\((x^2 + 1)(x + 1)(x - 1)^2\)

Step 1: Factorize \(x^3 - 27\), \((x - 3)^2\), and \(x^2 - 9\).

\(x^3 - 27 = (x - 3)(x^2 + 3x + 9)\)

\((x - 3)^2 = (x - 3)^2\)

\(x^2 - 9 = (x + 3)(x - 3)\)

Step 2: The LCM is the product of the highest powers of all factors.

\((x - 3)^2(x + 3)(x^2 + 3x + 9)\)
