Example 3.63

If \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & 9 \\ -4 & 3 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( B = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 & 4 \\ 7 & 5 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \), then find \( 2A + B \).

Solution:

Since \( A \) and \( B \) have the same order \( 2 \times 3 \), \( 2A + B \) is defined.

We have \( 2A + B = 2 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & 9 \\ -4 & 3 & -1 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 & 4 \\ 7 & 5 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 6 & 18 \\ -8 & 6 & -2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 & 4 \\ 7 & 5 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 8 & 22 \\ -1 & 11 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \).
