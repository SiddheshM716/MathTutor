If \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & 9 \\ -4 & 3 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( B = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 & 4 \\ 7 & 5 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \), then find \( 2A + B \).

1

Check if the matrices \( A \) and \( B \) have the same order. Here, both \( A \) and \( B \) are of order \( 2 \times 3 \), so \( 2A + B \) is defined.

2

Compute \( 2A \) by multiplying each element of \( A \) by 2:

\[ 2A = 2 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & 9 \\ -4 & 3 & -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 6 & 18 \\ -8 & 6 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \]

3

Add \( 2A \) and \( B \) element-wise:

\[ 2A + B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 6 & 18 \\ -8 & 6 & -2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 & 4 \\ 7 & 5 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 8 & 22 \\ -1 & 11 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \]

\( 2A + B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 8 & 22 \\ -1 & 11 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \)
