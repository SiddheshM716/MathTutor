Solve the following system of equations:

\[ 3x - y = 12 \quad \text{(1)} \]

\[ 7x - 2z = 42 \quad \text{(2)} \]

\[ 2y + 3z = 78 \quad \text{(3)} \]

1

Eliminate \( z \) from equations (2) and (3). Multiply equation (2) by 3 and equation (3) by 2 to align the coefficients of \( z \).

\[ (2) \times 3 \Rightarrow 21x - 6z = 126 \]

\[ (3) \times 2 \Rightarrow 4y + 6z = 156 \]

2

Add the resulting equations to eliminate \( z \).

\[ 21x - 6z + 4y + 6z = 126 + 156 \]

\[ 21x + 4y = 282 \quad \text{(4)} \]

3

Multiply equation (1) by 4 to align the coefficients of \( y \) with equation (4).

\[ (1) \times 4 \Rightarrow 12x - 4y = 48 \]

4

Add equation (4) and the modified equation (1) to eliminate \( y \).

\[ 21x + 4y + 12x - 4y = 282 + 48 \]

\[ 33x = 330 \]

\[ x = 10 \]

5

Substitute \( x = 10 \) into equation (1) to solve for \( y \).

\[ 3(10) - y = 12 \]

\[ 30 - y = 12 \]

\[ y = 18 \]

6

Substitute \( x = 10 \) into equation (2) to solve for \( z \).

\[ 7(10) - 2z = 42 \]

\[ 70 - 2z = 42 \]

\[ -2z = -28 \]

\[ z = 14 \]

The solution is \( x = 10 \), \( y = 18 \), \( z = 14 \).
