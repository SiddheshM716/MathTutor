A flock of swans contained \( x^2 \) members. As the clouds gathered, \( 10x \) went to a lake and one-eighth of the members flew away to a garden. The remaining three pairs played about in the water. How many swans were there in total?

1

Let the total number of swans be \( x^2 \). According to the problem, \( 10x \) swans went to a lake, and \( \frac{1}{8}x^2 \) flew away to a garden. The remaining swans are 3 pairs, which is 6 swans.

2

Formulate the equation based on the given information:

\[ x^2 - 10x - \frac{1}{8}x^2 = 6 \]

3

Simplify the equation:

\[ \frac{7}{8}x^2 - 10x - 6 = 0 \]

Multiply both sides by 8 to eliminate the fraction:

\[ 7x^2 - 80x - 48 = 0 \]

4

Solve the quadratic equation using the quadratic formula:

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

Here, \( a = 7 \), \( b = -80 \), and \( c = -48 \).

5

Substitute the values into the formula:

\[ x = \frac{80 \pm \sqrt{6400 - 4(7)(-48)}}{14} \]

\[ x = \frac{80 \pm \sqrt{6400 + 1344}}{14} \]

\[ x = \frac{80 \pm \sqrt{7744}}{14} \]

\[ x = \frac{80 \pm 88}{14} \]

6

Evaluate the two possible solutions:

\[ x = \frac{80 + 88}{14} = \frac{168}{14} = 12 \]

\[ x = \frac{80 - 88}{14} = \frac{-8}{14} = -\frac{4}{7} \]

7

Since the number of swans cannot be negative, discard \( x = -\frac{4}{7} \).

The total number of swans is \( x^2 = 12^2 = 144 \).
