If a matrix has 16 elements, what are the possible orders it can have?

1

Recall that a matrix of order \( m \times n \) has \( m \times n \) elements. To find all possible orders, we need to find all ordered pairs of natural numbers \((m, n)\) such that \( m \times n = 16 \).

2

List all the factor pairs of 16 where both factors are natural numbers.

3

The ordered pairs \((m, n)\) that satisfy \( m \times n = 16 \) are:

(1, 16), (16, 1), (2, 8), (8, 2), and (4, 4).

The possible orders of the matrix are \( 1 \times 16 \), \( 16 \times 1 \), \( 2 \times 8 \), \( 8 \times 2 \), and \( 4 \times 4 \).
