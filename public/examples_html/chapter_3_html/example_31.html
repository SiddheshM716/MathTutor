Solve \(2x^2 - x - 1 = 0\).

1

Make the coefficient of \(x^2\) equal to 1 by dividing the entire equation by 2.

\[

x^2 - \frac{1}{2}x - \frac{1}{2} = 0

\]

2

Move the constant term to the other side.

\[

x^2 - \frac{1}{2}x = \frac{1}{2}

\]

3

Complete the square by adding \(\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{16}\) to both sides.

\[

x^2 - \frac{1}{2}x + \frac{1}{16} = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{16}

\]

4

Simplify the equation.

\[

\left(x - \frac{1}{4}\right)^2 = \frac{9}{16}

\]

5

Take the square root of both sides and solve for \(x\).

\[

x - \frac{1}{4} = \pm \frac{3}{4}

\]

\[

x = \frac{1}{4} + \frac{3}{4} = 1 \quad \text{or} \quad x = \frac{1}{4} - \frac{3}{4} = -\frac{1}{2}

\]

\(x = 1\) or \(x = -\frac{1}{2}\).
