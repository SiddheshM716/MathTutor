If \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \) are the roots of \( x^2 + 7x + 10 = 0 \), find the values of:

(i) \( \alpha - \beta \)

(ii) \( \alpha^2 + \beta^2 \)

(iii) \( \alpha^3 - \beta^3 \)

(iv) \( \alpha^4 + \beta^4 \)

(v) \( \frac{\alpha}{\beta} + \frac{\beta}{\alpha} \)

(vi) \( \frac{\alpha^3}{\beta} + \frac{\beta^3}{\alpha} \)

1

Identify the coefficients and sum/product of roots.

Given the quadratic equation \( x^2 + 7x + 10 = 0 \), we have \( a = 1 \), \( b = 7 \), \( c = 10 \).

The sum of the roots \( \alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a} = -7 \).

The product of the roots \( \alpha \beta = \frac{c}{a} = 10 \).

2

Solve for \( \alpha - \beta \).

Using the formula \( \alpha - \beta = \sqrt{(\alpha + \beta)^2 - 4\alpha \beta} \).

Substitute the known values: \( \alpha - \beta = \sqrt{(-7)^2 - 4 \times 10} = \sqrt{49 - 40} = \sqrt{9} = 3 \).

Final Answer for (i): \( 3 \).

3

Solve for \( \alpha^2 + \beta^2 \).

Using the formula \( \alpha^2 + \beta^2 = (\alpha + \beta)^2 - 2\alpha \beta \).

Substitute the known values: \( \alpha^2 + \beta^2 = (-7)^2 - 2 \times 10 = 49 - 20 = 29 \).

Final Answer for (ii): \( 29 \).

4

Solve for \( \alpha^3 - \beta^3 \).

Using the formula \( \alpha^3 - \beta^3 = (\alpha - \beta)^3 + 3\alpha \beta (\alpha - \beta) \).

Substitute the known values: \( \alpha^3 - \beta^3 = (3)^3 + 3 \times 10 \times 3 = 27 + 90 = 117 \).

Final Answer for (iii): \( 117 \).

5

Solve for \( \alpha^4 + \beta^4 \).

Using the formula \( \alpha^4 + \beta^4 = (\alpha^2 + \beta^2)^2 - 2\alpha^2 \beta^2 \).

Substitute the known values: \( \alpha^4 + \beta^4 = 29^2 - 2 \times (10)^2 = 841 - 200 = 641 \).

Final Answer for (iv): \( 641 \).

6

Solve for \( \frac{\alpha}{\beta} + \frac{\beta}{\alpha} \).

Using the formula \( \frac{\alpha}{\beta} + \frac{\beta}{\alpha} = \frac{\alpha^2 + \beta^2}{\alpha \beta} \).

Substitute the known values: \( \frac{\alpha}{\beta} + \frac{\beta}{\alpha} = \frac{29}{10} \).

Final Answer for (v): \( \frac{29}{10} \).

7

Solve for \( \frac{\alpha^3}{\beta} + \frac{\beta^3}{\alpha} \).

Using the formula \( \frac{\alpha^3}{\beta} + \frac{\beta^3}{\alpha} = \frac{\alpha^4 + \beta^4}{\alpha \beta} \).

Substitute the known values: \( \frac{\alpha^3}{\beta} + \frac{\beta^3}{\alpha} = \frac{641}{10} \).

Final Answer for (vi): \( \frac{641}{10} \).
