Example 3.5

Solve:

1. \( x + 2y - z = 5 \)

2. \( x - y + z = -2 \)

3. \( -5x - 4y + z = -11 \)

Adding (1) and (2) we get:

\( 2x + y = 3 \) ...(4)

Subtracting (2) and (3):

\( 6x + 3y = 9 \)

Dividing by 3:

\( 2x + y = 3 \) ...(5)

Subtracting (4) and (5):

\( 0 = 0 \)

Here we arrive at an identity \( 0 = 0 \).

Hence the system has an infinite number of solutions.
