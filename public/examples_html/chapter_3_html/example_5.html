Solve the system of equations:

1. \( x + 2y - z = 5 \)

2. \( x - y + z = -2 \)

3. \( -5x - 4y + z = -11 \)

1

Add equations (1) and (2) to eliminate \( z \).

\[ (x + 2y - z) + (x - y + z) = 5 + (-2) \]

\[ 2x + y = 3 \quad \text{(Equation 4)} \]

2

Subtract equation (2) from equation (3) to eliminate \( z \).

\[ (-5x - 4y + z) - (x - y + z) = -11 - (-2) \]

\[ -6x - 3y = -9 \]

3

Simplify the resulting equation by dividing by \(-3\).

\[ 2x + y = 3 \quad \text{(Equation 5)} \]

4

Subtract Equation 4 from Equation 5.

\[ (2x + y) - (2x + y) = 3 - 3 \]

\[ 0 = 0 \]

The system reduces to an identity \( 0 = 0 \), indicating infinitely many solutions.
