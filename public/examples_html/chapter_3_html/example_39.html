A passenger train takes 1 hr more than an express train to travel a distance of 240 km from Chennai to Virudhachalam. The speed of the express train is more than that of the passenger train by 20 km per hour. Find the average speed of both the trains.

1

Let the average speed of the passenger train be \( x \) km/hr. Then, the average speed of the express train will be \( (x + 20) \) km/hr.

2

The time taken by the passenger train to cover 240 km is \( \frac{240}{x} \) hr, and the time taken by the express train is \( \frac{240}{x + 20} \) hr.

Given that the passenger train takes 1 hour more, we set up the equation:

\[

\frac{240}{x} - \frac{240}{x + 20} = 1

\]

3

Multiply both sides by \( x(x + 20) \) to eliminate denominators:

\[

240(x + 20) - 240x = x(x + 20)

\]

Simplify the equation:

\[

240x + 4800 - 240x = x^2 + 20x

\]

\[

4800 = x^2 + 20x

\]

Rearrange into standard quadratic form:

\[

x^2 + 20x - 4800 = 0

\]

Factor the quadratic equation:

\[

(x + 80)(x - 60) = 0

\]

Solve for \( x \):

\[

x = -80 \quad \text{or} \quad x = 60

\]

Since speed cannot be negative, we reject \( x = -80 \). Thus, \( x = 60 \).

The average speed of the passenger train is \( 60 \) km/hr, and the average speed of the express train is \( 80 \) km/hr.
