From the table, we found that as \( x \) increases, \( y \) also increases. Thus, the variation is a direct variation.

1

Identify the relationship between diameter (\( x \)) and circumference (\( y \)). The table shows a direct variation, so we use the formula \( y = kx \), where \( k \) is the constant of proportionality.

2

Calculate the constant \( k \) using the given values. For the first pair (\( x = 1 \), \( y = 3.1 \)):

\[ k = \frac{y}{x} = \frac{3.1}{1} = 3.1 \]

3

Verify the constant \( k \) with other pairs to ensure consistency. For example, for \( x = 2 \), \( y = 6.2 \):

\[ k = \frac{6.2}{2} = 3.1 \]

The same holds for all other pairs, confirming the relationship \( y = 3.1x \).

4

Use the formula \( y = 3.1x \) to find the circumference when the diameter is 6 cm:

\[ y = 3.1 \times 6 = 18.6 \]

The circumference of a circle with a diameter of 6 cm is \( \boxed{18.6 \text{ cm}} \).
