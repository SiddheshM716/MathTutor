Example 3.11

Find the GCD of \(6x^3 - 30x^2 + 60x - 48\) and \(3x^3 - 12x^2 + 21x - 18\).

Solution: Let \(f(x) = 6x^3 - 30x^2 + 60x - 48 = 6(x^3 - 5x^2 + 10x - 8)\) and \(g(x) = 3x^3 - 12x^2 + 21x - 18 = 3(x^3 - 4x^2 + 7x - 6)\).

Now, we shall find the GCD of \(x^3 - 5x^2 + 10x - 8\) and \(x^3 - 4x^2 + 7x - 6\).

\[ \begin{array}{r|l} x^3 - 5x^2 + 10x - 8 & x^3 - 4x^2 + 7x - 6 \\ \hline x^3 - 5x^2 + 10x - 8 & (-) \\ \hline x^2 - 3x + 2 & \\ \end{array} \]

\[ \begin{array}{r|l} x - 2 & x^3 - 5x^2 + 10x - 8 \\ \hline x^3 - 3x^2 + 2x & (-) \\ \hline -2x^2 + 8x - 8 & \\ -2x^2 + 6x - 4 & (-) \\ \hline 2x - 4 & \\ = 2(x - 2) & \\ \end{array} \]

\[ \begin{array}{r|l} x - 2 & x^2 - 3x + 2 \\ \hline x^2 - 2x & (-) \\ \hline -x + 2 & \\ -x + 2 & (-) \\ \hline 0 & \\ \end{array} \]

Here, we get zero as remainder.

GCD of leading coefficients 3 and 6 is 3.

Thus, GCD of \(6x^3 - 30x^2 + 60x - 48\) and \(3x^3 - 12x^2 + 21x - 18\) is \(3(x - 2)\).
