Solve the system of equations:

\[

\frac{1}{2x} + \frac{1}{4y} + \frac{1}{3z} = \frac{1}{4}

\]

\[

\frac{1}{5x} - \frac{1}{8y} + \frac{1}{5z} = \frac{2}{15}

\]

\[

\frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} = 1

\]

1

Let \(\frac{1}{x} = p\), \(\frac{1}{y} = q\), and \(\frac{1}{z} = r\). Rewrite the equations in terms of \(p\), \(q\), and \(r\):

\[

\frac{1}{2}p + \frac{1}{4}q + \frac{1}{3}r = \frac{1}{4}

\]

\[

\frac{1}{5}p - \frac{1}{8}q + \frac{1}{5}r = \frac{2}{15}

\]

\[

p + q = 1

\]

2

Simplify the equations by eliminating denominators. Multiply the first equation by 12 and the second equation by 120 to clear fractions:

\[

6p + 3q + 4r = 3 \quad (1)

\]

\[

24p - 15q + 24r = 16 \quad (2)

\]

\[

p + q = 1 \quad (3)

\]

3

Solve equation (3) for \(q\): \(q = 1 - p\). Substitute \(q = 1 - p\) into equations (1) and (2):

\[

6p + 3(1 - p) + 4r = 3 \implies 3p + 4r = 0 \quad (4)

\]

\[

24p - 15(1 - p) + 24r = 16 \implies 39p + 24r = 31 \quad (5)

\]

4

Solve equation (4) for \(p\): \(p = -\frac{4}{3}r\). Substitute \(p = -\frac{4}{3}r\) into equation (5):

\[

39\left(-\frac{4}{3}r\right) + 24r = 31 \implies -52r + 24r = 31 \implies -28r = 31 \implies r = -\frac{31}{28}

\]

5

Substitute \(r = -\frac{31}{28}\) back into equation (4) to find \(p\):

\[

3p + 4\left(-\frac{31}{28}\right) = 0 \implies 3p = \frac{31}{7} \implies p = \frac{31}{21}

\]

6

Substitute \(p = \frac{31}{21}\) into equation (3) to find \(q\):

\[

q = 1 - \frac{31}{21} = -\frac{10}{21}

\]

7

Recover \(x\), \(y\), and \(z\) from \(p\), \(q\), and \(r\):

\[

x = \frac{1}{p} = \frac{21}{31}, \quad y = \frac{1}{q} = -\frac{21}{10}, \quad z = \frac{1}{r} = -\frac{28}{31}

\]

The solution is \(x = \frac{21}{31}\), \(y = -\frac{21}{10}\), and \(z = -\frac{28}{31}\).
