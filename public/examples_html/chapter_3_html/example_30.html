Example 3.30

Solve \( x^2 - 3x - 2 = 0 \)

Solution:

\( x^2 - 3x = 2 \) (Shifting the Constant to RHS)

\( x^2 - 3x + \left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^2 = 2 + \left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^2 \) (Add \(\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^2\) [coefficient of \(x\)] to both sides)

\( \left(x - \frac{3}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{17}{4} \) (Writing the LHS as complete square)

\( x - \frac{3}{2} = \pm \frac{\sqrt{17}}{2} \) (Taking the square root on both sides)

\( x = \frac{3}{2} + \frac{\sqrt{17}}{2} \) or \( x = \frac{3}{2} - \frac{\sqrt{17}}{2} \)

Therefore, \( x = \frac{3 + \sqrt{17}}{2}, \frac{3 - \sqrt{17}}{2} \).
