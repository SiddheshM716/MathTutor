Solve \( x^2 - 3x - 2 = 0 \).

1

Shift the constant term to the right-hand side (RHS).

\( x^2 - 3x = 2 \).

2

Add the square of half the coefficient of \( x \) to both sides.

The coefficient of \( x \) is \(-3\), so half of it is \(-\frac{3}{2}\), and its square is \(\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^2\).

\( x^2 - 3x + \left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^2 = 2 + \left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^2 \).

3

Rewrite the left-hand side (LHS) as a perfect square and simplify the RHS.

\( \left(x - \frac{3}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{17}{4} \).

4

Take the square root of both sides.

\( x - \frac{3}{2} = \pm \frac{\sqrt{17}}{2} \).

5

Solve for \( x \).

\( x = \frac{3}{2} + \frac{\sqrt{17}}{2} \) or \( x = \frac{3}{2} - \frac{\sqrt{17}}{2} \).

\( x = \frac{3 + \sqrt{17}}{2}, \frac{3 - \sqrt{17}}{2} \).
