The product of Kumaran's age (in years) two years ago and his age four years from now is one more than twice his present age. What is his present age?

1

Let the present age of Kumaran be \( x \) years.

2

Two years ago, his age was \( (x - 2) \) years. Four years from now, his age will be \( (x + 4) \) years.

3

According to the problem, the product of these ages is one more than twice his present age. This gives the equation:

\[

(x - 2)(x + 4) = 1 + 2x

\]

4

Expand and simplify the equation:

\[

x^2 + 2x - 8 = 1 + 2x

\]

\[

x^2 - 9 = 0

\]

5

Factor the quadratic equation:

\[

(x - 3)(x + 3) = 0

\]

The solutions are \( x = 3 \) or \( x = -3 \). Since age cannot be negative, Kumaran's present age is \(\boxed{3 \text{ years}}\).
