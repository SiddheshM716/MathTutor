Given matrices:

\[

A = \begin{pmatrix}

5 & 4 & -2 \\

\frac{1}{2} & \frac{3}{4} & \sqrt{2} \\

1 & 9 & 4

\end{pmatrix}, \quad

B = \begin{pmatrix}

-7 & 4 & -3 \\

\frac{1}{4} & \frac{7}{2} & 3 \\

5 & -6 & 9

\end{pmatrix}

\]

Find \( 4A - 3B \).

1

Verify that the matrices \( A \) and \( B \) have the same dimensions (\( 3 \times 3 \)), so the operation \( 4A - 3B \) is defined.

2

Apply scalar multiplication to each matrix separately. The formula for scalar multiplication is \( kM \), where each element of \( M \) is multiplied by \( k \).

\[

4A = 4\begin{pmatrix}

5 & 4 & -2 \\

\frac{1}{2} & \frac{3}{4} & \sqrt{2} \\

1 & 9 & 4

\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}

20 & 16 & -8 \\

2 & 3 & 4\sqrt{2} \\

4 & 36 & 16

\end{pmatrix}

\]

\[

3B = 3\begin{pmatrix}

-7 & 4 & -3 \\

\frac{1}{4} & \frac{7}{2} & 3 \\

5 & -6 & 9

\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}

-21 & 12 & -9 \\

\frac{3}{4} & \frac{21}{2} & 9 \\

15 & -18 & 27

\end{pmatrix}

\]

3

Subtract \( 3B \) from \( 4A \) by subtracting corresponding elements.

\[

4A - 3B = \begin{pmatrix}

20 & 16 & -8 \\

2 & 3 & 4\sqrt{2} \\

4 & 36 & 16

\end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix}

-21 & 12 & -9 \\

\frac{3}{4} & \frac{21}{2} & 9 \\

15 & -18 & 27

\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}

41 & 4 & 1 \\

\frac{5}{4} & -\frac{15}{2} & 4\sqrt{2} - 9 \\

-11 & 54 & -11

\end{pmatrix}

\]

\[

4A - 3B = \begin{pmatrix}

-11 & 54 & -11

\end{pmatrix}

\]
