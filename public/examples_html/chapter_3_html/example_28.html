Solve \(x^4 - 13x^2 + 42 = 0\).

1

Let \(x^2 = a\). Substitute into the equation to simplify it:

\[

x^4 - 13x^2 + 42 = a^2 - 13a + 42.

\]

2

Factor the quadratic equation:

\[

a^2 - 13a + 42 = (a - 7)(a - 6).

\]

3

Solve for \(a\) by setting each factor to zero:

\[

(a - 7)(a - 6) = 0 \implies a = 7 \text{ or } a = 6.

\]

4

Substitute back \(a = x^2\) and solve for \(x\):

\[

x^2 = 7 \implies x = \pm \sqrt{7},

\]

\[

x^2 = 6 \implies x = \pm \sqrt{6}.

\]

The roots are \(x = \pm \sqrt{7}\) or \(x = \pm \sqrt{6}\).
