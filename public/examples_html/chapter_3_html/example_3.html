Solve the following system of linear equations in three variables:

\(3x - 2y + z = 2\) ...(1)

\(2x + 3y - z = 5\) ...(2)

\(x + y + z = 6\) ...(3)

1

Add equations (1) and (2) to eliminate \(z\):

\[

3x - 2y + z = 2

\]

\[

2x + 3y - z = 5

\]

\[

5x + y = 7 \quad \text{(4)}

\]

2

Add equations (2) and (3) to eliminate \(z\) again:

\[

2x + 3y - z = 5

\]

\[

x + y + z = 6

\]

\[

3x + 4y = 11 \quad \text{(5)}

\]

3

Solve the system of equations (4) and (5). Multiply equation (4) by 4 and subtract equation (5):

\[

4 \times (5x + y) = 4 \times 7 \implies 20x + 4y = 28

\]

\[

3x + 4y = 11

\]

\[

17x = 17 \implies x = 1

\]

4

Substitute \(x = 1\) into equation (4):

\[

5(1) + y = 7 \implies y = 2

\]

5

Substitute \(x = 1\) and \(y = 2\) into equation (3):

\[

1 + 2 + z = 6 \implies z = 3

\]

\(x = 1\), \(y = 2\), \(z = 3\).
