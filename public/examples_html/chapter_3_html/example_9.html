The sum of thrice the first number, second number and twice the third number is 5. If thrice the second number is subtracted from the sum of first number and thrice the third we get 2. If the third number is subtracted from the sum of twice the first, thrice the second, we get 1. Find the numbers.

1

Let the three numbers be \( x \), \( y \), \( z \). From the given data, we form the following equations:

\[ 3x + y + 2z = 5 \quad \text{(1)} \]

\[ x + 3z - 3y = 2 \quad \text{(2)} \]

\[ 2x + 3y - z = 1 \quad \text{(3)} \]

2

Multiply equation (1) by 1 and equation (2) by 3 to eliminate \( x \):

\[ 3x + y + 2z = 5 \]

\[ 3x - 9y + 9z = 6 \]

Subtract the second equation from the first:

\[ 10y - 7z = -1 \quad \text{(4)} \]

3

Multiply equation (1) by 2 and equation (3) by 3 to eliminate \( x \) again:

\[ 6x + 2y + 4z = 10 \]

\[ 6x + 9y - 3z = 3 \]

Subtract the second equation from the first:

\[ -7y + 7z = 7 \quad \text{(5)} \]

4

Add equations (4) and (5) to eliminate \( z \):

\[ 10y - 7z = -1 \]

\[ -7y + 7z = 7 \]

\[ 3y = 6 \]

\[ y = 2 \]

5

Substitute \( y = 2 \) into equation (5):

\[ -7(2) + 7z = 7 \]

\[ -14 + 7z = 7 \]

\[ 7z = 21 \]

\[ z = 3 \]

6

Substitute \( y = 2 \) and \( z = 3 \) into equation (1):

\[ 3x + 2 + 6 = 5 \]

\[ 3x + 8 = 5 \]

\[ 3x = -3 \]

\[ x = -1 \]

The numbers are \( x = -1 \), \( y = 2 \), \( z = 3 \).
