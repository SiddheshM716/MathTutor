If \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 5 & 0 \\ 8 & 7 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \), \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 11 & -3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \), \( C = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & -7 & 2 \\ 1 & 4 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \), compute the following:

(i) \( 3A + 2B - C \)

(ii) \( \frac{1}{5}A - \frac{3}{5}B \)

1

Compute \( 3A + 2B - C \) by first multiplying each matrix by its scalar and then performing addition/subtraction.

\( 3A = 3\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 5 & 0 \\ 8 & 7 & 6 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 & 15 & 0 \\ 24 & 21 & 18 \end{bmatrix} \)

\( 2B = 2\begin{bmatrix} 2 & 11 & -3 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 22 & -6 \\ 0 & 2 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \)

\( -C = -\begin{bmatrix} -1 & -7 & 2 \\ 1 & 4 & 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 7 & -2 \\ -1 & -4 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \)

2

Add the resulting matrices.

\( 3A + 2B - C = \begin{bmatrix} 9 & 15 & 0 \\ 24 & 21 & 18 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 22 & -6 \\ 0 & 2 & 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 7 & -2 \\ -1 & -4 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \)

3

Perform element-wise addition.

\( 3A + 2B - C = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 44 & -8 \\ 23 & 19 & 17 \end{bmatrix} \)

\( 3A + 2B - C = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 44 & -8 \\ 23 & 19 & 17 \end{bmatrix} \)
