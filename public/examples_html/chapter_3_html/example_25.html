Find the sum and product of the roots for each of the following quadratic equations:

(i) \( x^2 + 8x - 65 = 0 \)

(ii) \( 2x^2 + 5x + 7 = 0 \)

(iii) \( kx^2 - k^2x - 2k^3 = 0 \)

1

For the equation \( x^2 + 8x - 65 = 0 \), identify the coefficients \( a = 1 \), \( b = 8 \), and \( c = -65 \).

2

Use the formulas for sum and product of roots:

\[ \alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a} \quad \text{and} \quad \alpha\beta = \frac{c}{a} \]

3

Calculate the sum and product:

\[ \alpha + \beta = -\frac{8}{1} = -8 \]

\[ \alpha\beta = \frac{-65}{1} = -65 \]

Sum of roots = \(-8\), Product of roots = \(-65\).

---

Step 1: For the equation \( 2x^2 + 5x + 7 = 0 \), identify the coefficients \( a = 2 \), \( b = 5 \), and \( c = 7 \).

Step 2: Use the formulas for sum and product of roots:

\[ \alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a} \quad \text{and} \quad \alpha\beta = \frac{c}{a} \]

Step 3: Calculate the sum and product:

\[ \alpha + \beta = -\frac{5}{2} \]

\[ \alpha\beta = \frac{7}{2} \]

Sum of roots = \(-\frac{5}{2}\), Product of roots = \(\frac{7}{2}\).

---

Step 1: For the equation \( kx^2 - k^2x - 2k^3 = 0 \), identify the coefficients \( a = k \), \( b = -k^2 \), and \( c = -2k^3 \).

Step 2: Use the formulas for sum and product of roots:

\[ \alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a} \quad \text{and} \quad \alpha\beta = \frac{c}{a} \]

Step 3: Calculate the sum and product:

\[ \alpha + \beta = -\frac{-k^2}{k} = k \]

\[ \alpha\beta = \frac{-2k^3}{k} = -2k^2 \]

Sum of roots = \(k\), Product of roots = \(-2k^2\).
