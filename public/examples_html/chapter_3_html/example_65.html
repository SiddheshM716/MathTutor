Example 3.65

Find the value of \( a, b, c, d \) from the following matrix equation.

\[ \begin{bmatrix} d & 8 \\ 3 & a \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 2a \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3b & a \\ -2 & -4 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} b & 4c \\ -5 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]

First, we add the two matrices on both left, right hand sides to get

\[ \begin{bmatrix} d+3 & 8+a \\ 3b-2 & a-4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 2a+1 \\ b-5 & 4c \end{bmatrix} \]

Equating the corresponding elements of the two matrices, we have

\[ d+3=2 \Rightarrow d=-1 \] \[ 8+a=2a+1 \Rightarrow a=7 \] \[ 3b-2=b-5 \Rightarrow b=-1.5 \] \[ a-4=4c \Rightarrow c=0.75 \]

Therefore, \( a = 7 \), \( b = -1.5 \), \( c = 0.75 \), \( d = -1 \).
