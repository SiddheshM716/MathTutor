A bus is traveling at a uniform speed of 50 km/hr. Draw the distance-time graph and hence find:

the constant of variation

how far will it travel in 90 minutes?

the time required to cover a distance of 300 km from the graph.

Time taken x (in minutes)

60

120

180

240

Distance y (in km)

50

100

150

200

1

Identify the relationship between time (x) and distance (y). Since the bus travels at a uniform speed, the variation is direct and follows the form \( y = kx \).

2

Calculate the constant of variation \( k \) using the formula \( k = \frac{y}{x} \).

3

Using the given data points:

\[

k = \frac{50}{60} = \frac{100}{120} = \frac{150}{180} = \frac{200}{240} = \frac{5}{6}

\]

Thus, the relationship is \( y = \frac{5}{6}x \).

Final Answer (i): The constant of variation is \( \frac{5}{6} \).

4

To find the distance traveled in 90 minutes, substitute \( x = 90 \) into the equation \( y = \frac{5}{6}x \).

5

Calculate \( y \):

\[

y = \frac{5}{6} \times 90 = 75 \text{ km}

\]

Final Answer (ii): The bus will travel 75 km in 90 minutes.

6

To find the time required to cover 300 km, substitute \( y = 300 \) into the equation \( y = \frac{5}{6}x \).

7

Solve for \( x \):

\[

x = \frac{6}{5} \times 300 = 360 \text{ minutes (or 6 hours)}

\]

Final Answer (iii): The time required to cover 300 km is 360 minutes (or 6 hours).
