Solve the system of equations:

\[ 2x - 3y = 6 \]

\[ x + y = 1 \]

1

Label the equations for clarity.

\[ 2x - 3y = 6 \quad \text{(1)} \]

\[ x + y = 1 \quad \text{(2)} \]

2

Multiply equation (2) by 2 to align coefficients of \( x \).

\[ (2) \times 2 \Rightarrow 2x + 2y = 2 \quad \text{(2a)} \]

3

Subtract equation (2a) from equation (1) to eliminate \( x \).

\[ (2x - 3y) - (2x + 2y) = 6 - 2 \]

\[ -5y = 4 \]

\[ y = -\frac{4}{5} \]

4

Substitute \( y = -\frac{4}{5} \) into equation (2) to solve for \( x \).

\[ x + \left(-\frac{4}{5}\right) = 1 \]

\[ x = 1 + \frac{4}{5} \]

\[ x = \frac{9}{5} \]

\[ x = \frac{9}{5}, \quad y = -\frac{4}{5} \]
