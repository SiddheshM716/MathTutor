If \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \), find \( AB \) and \( BA \). Verify \( AB = BA \).

1

Check the dimensions of matrices \( A \) and \( B \). Both are \( 2 \times 2 \) matrices, so \( AB \) and \( BA \) are defined and will also be \( 2 \times 2 \) matrices.

2

Compute \( AB \) using matrix multiplication. The formula for multiplying two \( 2 \times 2 \) matrices is:

\[ AB = \begin{bmatrix} a_{11}b_{11} + a_{12}b_{21} & a_{11}b_{12} + a_{12}b_{22} \\ a_{21}b_{11} + a_{22}b_{21} & a_{21}b_{12} + a_{22}b_{22} \end{bmatrix} \]

3

Substitute the values and calculate \( AB \):

\[ AB = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \times \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (2 \cdot 2 + 1 \cdot 1) & (2 \cdot 0 + 1 \cdot 3) \\ (1 \cdot 2 + 3 \cdot 1) & (1 \cdot 0 + 3 \cdot 3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 3 \\ 5 & 9 \end{bmatrix} \]

4

Compute \( BA \) using the same matrix multiplication formula.

5

Substitute the values and calculate \( BA \):

\[ BA = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \times \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} (2 \cdot 2 + 0 \cdot 1) & (2 \cdot 1 + 0 \cdot 3) \\ (1 \cdot 2 + 3 \cdot 1) & (1 \cdot 1 + 3 \cdot 3) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 \\ 5 & 10 \end{bmatrix} \]

\( AB = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 3 \\ 5 & 9 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( BA = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 \\ 5 & 10 \end{bmatrix} \). Since \( AB \neq BA \), the matrices do not commute.
