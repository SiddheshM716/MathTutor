A ladder 17 feet long is leaning against a wall. If the ladder, vertical wall, and the floor from the bottom of the wall to the ladder form a right triangle, find the height of the wall where the top of the ladder meets if the distance between the bottom of the wall to the bottom of the ladder is 7 feet less than the height of the wall.

1

Let the height of the wall \( AB = x \) feet. The distance from the bottom of the wall to the bottom of the ladder is given as \( BC = (x - 7) \) feet.

2

In the right triangle \( ABC \), the ladder \( AC = 17 \) ft is the hypotenuse. Apply the Pythagorean theorem:

\[ AC^2 = AB^2 + BC^2 \]

3

Substitute the known values:

\[ 17^2 = x^2 + (x - 7)^2 \]

\[ 289 = x^2 + x^2 - 14x + 49 \]

\[ 2x^2 - 14x - 240 = 0 \]

4

Simplify the quadratic equation:

\[ x^2 - 7x - 120 = 0 \]

\[ (x - 15)(x + 8) = 0 \]

\[ x = 15 \text{ or } x = -8 \]

The height of the wall \( AB = 15 \) feet (rejecting \( -8 \) as height cannot be negative).
