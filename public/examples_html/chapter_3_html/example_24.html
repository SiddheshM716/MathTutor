Write down the quadratic equation in general form for which sum and product of the roots are given below.

(i) 9, 14

(ii) -7/2, 5/2

(iii) -3/5, -1/9

1

The general form of a quadratic equation when the sum and product of roots are known is:

\[ x^2 - (\text{sum of roots})x + (\text{product of roots}) = 0 \]

2

For part (i), the sum of roots is 9 and the product is 14.

\[ x^2 - 9x + 14 = 0 \]

3

For part (ii), the sum of roots is \(-\frac{7}{2}\) and the product is \(\frac{5}{2}\).

\[ x^2 - \left(-\frac{7}{2}\right)x + \frac{5}{2} = 0 \]

Multiply through by 2 to eliminate fractions:

\[ 2x^2 + 7x + 5 = 0 \]

4

For part (iii), the sum of roots is \(-\frac{3}{5}\) and the product is \(-\frac{1}{9}\).

\[ x^2 - \left(-\frac{3}{5}\right)x + \left(-\frac{1}{9}\right) = 0 \]

Multiply through by 45 (the LCM of 5 and 9) to eliminate fractions:

\[ 45x^2 + 27x - 5 = 0 \]

(i) \( x^2 - 9x + 14 = 0 \)

(ii) \( 2x^2 + 7x + 5 = 0 \)

(iii) \( 45x^2 + 27x - 5 = 0 \)
