If the difference between the roots of the equation \(x^2 - 13x + k = 0\) is 17, find \(k\).

1

Let \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) be the roots of the equation. From the quadratic equation properties, we know:

\[

\alpha + \beta = 13 \quad \text{(1)}

\]

\[

\alpha - \beta = 17 \quad \text{(2)}

\]

2

Add equations (1) and (2) to eliminate \(\beta\):

\[

2\alpha = 30

\]

3

Solve for \(\alpha\):

\[

\alpha = 15

\]

4

Substitute \(\alpha = 15\) into equation (1) to find \(\beta\):

\[

15 + \beta = 13 \implies \beta = -2

\]

5

Use the property of quadratic equations \(\alpha \beta = k\) to find \(k\):

\[

k = 15 \times (-2) = -30

\]

\(k = -30\)
