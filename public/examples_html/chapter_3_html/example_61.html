Example 3.61

Two examinations were conducted for three groups of students (Group 1, Group 2, Group 3) and their average marks for Tamil, English, Science, and Mathematics are given below as matrices A and B. Find the total marks of both examinations for all groups.

First Examination (Matrix A):

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 22 & 15 & 14 & 23 \\ 50 & 62 & 21 & 30 \\ 53 & 80 & 32 & 40 \end{pmatrix} \]

Where rows represent groups (1-3) and columns represent subjects (Tamil, English, Science, Mathematics).

Second Examination (Matrix B):

\[ B = \begin{pmatrix} 20 & 38 & 15 & 40 \\ 18 & 12 & 17 & 80 \\ 81 & 47 & 52 & 18 \end{pmatrix} \]

Solution

The total marks is the matrix sum A + B:

\[ A + B = \begin{pmatrix} 22+20 & 15+38 & 14+15 & 23+40 \\ 50+18 & 62+12 & 21+17 & 30+80 \\ 53+81 & 80+47 & 32+52 & 40+18 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 42 & 53 & 29 & 63 \\ 68 & 74 & 38 & 110 \\ 134 & 127 & 84 & 58 \end{pmatrix} \]

Interpretation:

The resulting matrix shows the combined marks across both exams:

\[ \begin{pmatrix} \text{Group 1} & 42 (\text{Tamil}) & 53 (\text{English}) & 29 (\text{Science}) & 63 (\text{Math}) \\ \text{Group 2} & 68 & 74 & 38 & 110 \\ \text{Group 3} & 134 & 127 & 84 & 58 \end{pmatrix} \]
