In an interschool athletic meet, with a total of 24 individual prizes, securing a total of 56 points, a first place secures 5 points, a second place secures 3 points, and a third place secures 1 point. Having as many third place finishers as first and second place finishers, find how many athletes finished in each place.

1

Let the number of first, second, and third place finishers be \( x \), \( y \), and \( z \) respectively.

2

Write the equations based on the given information:

\[

x + y + z = 24 \quad \text{(Total prizes)}

\]

\[

5x + 3y + z = 56 \quad \text{(Total points)}

\]

\[

x + y = z \quad \text{(Third place finishers equal first and second combined)}

\]

3

Substitute \( z = x + y \) into the first equation:

\[

x + y + (x + y) = 24 \implies 2x + 2y = 24 \implies x + y = 12 \implies z = 12

\]

4

Substitute \( z = 12 \) into the second equation:

\[

5x + 3y + 12 = 56 \implies 5x + 3y = 44

\]

5

Multiply \( x + y = 12 \) by 3:

\[

3x + 3y = 36

\]

6

Subtract this from the modified second equation:

\[

(5x + 3y) - (3x + 3y) = 44 - 36 \implies 2x = 8 \implies x = 4

\]

7

Substitute \( x = 4 \) into \( x + y = 12 \):

\[

4 + y = 12 \implies y = 8

\]

The number of finishers is:

- First place: \( 4 \)

- Second place: \( 8 \)

- Third place: \( 12 \)
