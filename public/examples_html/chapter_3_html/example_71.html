Example 3.71

If \( A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \), \( B = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \), and \( C = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \), show that \( (AB)C = A(BC) \).

Solution:

LHS = \( (AB)C \)

\( AB = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \times \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 - 1 + 2 & 1 - 3 + 6 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix} \)

\( (AB)C = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix} \times \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 - 4 & 6 + 12 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 18 \end{pmatrix} \)

RHS = \( A(BC) \)

\( BC = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \times \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 - 1 & 4 + 3 \\ 1 - 3 & 2 + 9 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 7 \\ -2 & 11 \end{pmatrix} \)

\( A(BC) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \times \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 7 \\ -2 & 11 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 + 2 - 4 & 7 - 11 + 22 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 18 \end{pmatrix} \)

From LHS and RHS, \( (AB)C = A(BC) \).
