The father’s age is six times his son’s age. Six years hence the age of father will be four times his son’s age. Find the present ages (in years) of the son and father.

1

Let the present age of the father be \( x \) years and the present age of the son be \( y \) years.

2

Given, the father’s age is six times the son’s age.

Formula: \( x = 6y \) ... (1)

3

Six years later, the father’s age will be four times the son’s age.

Formula: \( x + 6 = 4(y + 6) \) ... (2)

4

Substitute equation (1) into equation (2):

\( 6y + 6 = 4(y + 6) \)

5

Simplify the equation:

\( 6y + 6 = 4y + 24 \)

\( 6y - 4y = 24 - 6 \)

\( 2y = 18 \)

\( y = 9 \)

6

Substitute \( y = 9 \) back into equation (1):

\( x = 6 \times 9 = 54 \)

Son’s age = 9 years, Father’s age = 54 years.
