If \( \alpha, \beta \) are the roots of the equation \( 2x^2 - x - 1 = 0 \), then form the equation whose roots are:

(i) \( \frac{1}{\alpha}, \frac{1}{\beta} \)

(ii) \( \alpha^2\beta, \beta^2\alpha \)

(iii) \( 2\alpha + \beta, 2\beta + \alpha \)

1

Given equation \( 2x^2 - x - 1 = 0 \). Here, \( a = 2, b = -1, c = -1 \).

Sum of the roots \( \alpha + \beta = \frac{-b}{a} = \frac{1}{2} \).

Product of the roots \( \alpha\beta = \frac{c}{a} = -\frac{1}{2} \).

2

For part (i), the roots are \( \frac{1}{\alpha}, \frac{1}{\beta} \).

Sum of the roots \( = \frac{1}{\alpha} + \frac{1}{\beta} = \frac{\alpha + \beta}{\alpha\beta} = \frac{\frac{1}{2}}{-\frac{1}{2}} = -1 \).

Product of the roots \( = \frac{1}{\alpha} \times \frac{1}{\beta} = \frac{1}{\alpha\beta} = \frac{1}{-\frac{1}{2}} = -2 \).

The required equation is \( x^2 - (\text{Sum of the roots})x + (\text{Product of the roots}) = 0 \).

3

Calculation: \( x^2 - (-1)x - 2 = 0 \) ⇒ \( x^2 + x - 2 = 0 \).

(i) \( x^2 + x - 2 = 0 \).

Step 1: For part (ii), the roots are \( \alpha^2\beta, \beta^2\alpha \).

Sum of the roots \( = \alpha^2\beta + \beta^2\alpha = \alpha\beta(\alpha + \beta) = -\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2} = -\frac{1}{4} \).

Product of the roots \( = (\alpha^2\beta) \times (\beta^2\alpha) = \alpha^3\beta^3 = (\alpha\beta)^3 = \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)^3 = -\frac{1}{8} \).

The required equation is \( x^2 - (\text{Sum of the roots})x + (\text{Product of the roots}) = 0 \).

Step 2: Calculation: \( x^2 - \left(-\frac{1}{4}\right)x - \frac{1}{8} = 0 \) ⇒ \( 8x^2 + 2x - 1 = 0 \).

(ii) \( 8x^2 + 2x - 1 = 0 \).

Step 1: For part (iii), the roots are \( 2\alpha + \beta, 2\beta + \alpha \).

Sum of the roots \( = 2\alpha + \beta + 2\beta + \alpha = 3(\alpha + \beta) = 3 \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{2} \).

Product of the roots \( = (2\alpha + \beta)(2\beta + \alpha) = 5\alpha\beta + 2(\alpha^2 + \beta^2) \).

Step 2: Calculate \( \alpha^2 + \beta^2 = (\alpha + \beta)^2 - 2\alpha\beta = \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2 - 2 \times \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) = \frac{5}{4} \).

Product of the roots \( = 5 \times \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) + 2 \times \frac{5}{4} = 0 \).

The required equation is \( x^2 - (\text{Sum of the roots})x + (\text{Product of the roots}) = 0 \).

Step 3: Calculation: \( x^2 - \frac{3}{2}x + 0 = 0 \) ⇒ \( 2x^2 - 3x = 0 \).

(iii) \( 2x^2 - 3x = 0 \).
