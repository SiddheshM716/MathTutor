Example 3.69

Given matrices:

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -2\sqrt{2} \\ \sqrt{2} & 2 \end{pmatrix}, \quad B = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 2\sqrt{2} \\ -\sqrt{2} & 2 \end{pmatrix} \]

Show that \( A \) and \( B \) satisfy the commutative property of matrix multiplication (i.e., \( AB = BA \)).

Solution

We need to demonstrate that \( AB = BA \):

First compute \( AB \):

\[ AB = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -2\sqrt{2} \\ \sqrt{2} & 2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 2\sqrt{2} \\ -\sqrt{2} & 2 \end{pmatrix} \]

\[ = \begin{pmatrix} 2×2 + (-2\sqrt{2})×(-\sqrt{2}) & 2×2\sqrt{2} + (-2\sqrt{2})×2 \\ \sqrt{2}×2 + 2×(-\sqrt{2}) & \sqrt{2}×2\sqrt{2} + 2×2 \end{pmatrix} \]

\[ = \begin{pmatrix} 4 + 4 & 4\sqrt{2} - 4\sqrt{2} \\ 2\sqrt{2} - 2\sqrt{2} & 4 + 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 0 \\ 0 & 8 \end{pmatrix} \]

Then compute \( BA \):

\[ BA = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 2\sqrt{2} \\ -\sqrt{2} & 2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -2\sqrt{2} \\ \sqrt{2} & 2 \end{pmatrix} \]

\[ = \begin{pmatrix} 2×2 + 2\sqrt{2}×\sqrt{2} & 2×(-2\sqrt{2}) + 2\sqrt{2}×2 \\ -\sqrt{2}×2 + 2×\sqrt{2} & -\sqrt{2}×(-2\sqrt{2}) + 2×2 \end{pmatrix} \]

\[ = \begin{pmatrix} 4 + 4 & -4\sqrt{2} + 4\sqrt{2} \\ -2\sqrt{2} + 2\sqrt{2} & 4 + 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 0 \\ 0 & 8 \end{pmatrix} \]

Conclusion

Since \( AB = BA = \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 0 \\ 0 & 8 \end{pmatrix} \), the matrices \( A \) and \( B \) satisfy the commutative property of matrix multiplication.
