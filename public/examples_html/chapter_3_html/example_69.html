Given matrices:

\[

A = \begin{pmatrix}

2 & -2\sqrt{2} \\

\sqrt{2} & 2

\end{pmatrix}, \quad

B = \begin{pmatrix}

2 & 2\sqrt{2} \\

-\sqrt{2} & 2

\end{pmatrix}

\]

Show that \( A \) and \( B \) satisfy the commutative property of matrix multiplication (i.e., \( AB = BA \)).

1

Compute the product \( AB \).

\[

AB = \begin{pmatrix}

2 & -2\sqrt{2} \\

\sqrt{2} & 2

\end{pmatrix}

\begin{pmatrix}

2 & 2\sqrt{2} \\

-\sqrt{2} & 2

\end{pmatrix}

\]

2

Apply the matrix multiplication formula \( (AB)_{ij} = \sum_{k} A_{ik} B_{kj} \).

\[

AB = \begin{pmatrix}

2×2 + (-2\sqrt{2})×(-\sqrt{2}) & 2×2\sqrt{2} + (-2\sqrt{2})×2 \\

\sqrt{2}×2 + 2×(-\sqrt{2}) & \sqrt{2}×2\sqrt{2} + 2×2

\end{pmatrix}

\]

3

Simplify the calculations.

\[

AB = \begin{pmatrix}

4 + 4 & 4\sqrt{2} - 4\sqrt{2} \\

2\sqrt{2} - 2\sqrt{2} & 4 + 4

\end{pmatrix}

= \begin{pmatrix}

8 & 0 \\

0 & 8

\end{pmatrix}

\]

4

Compute the product \( BA \).

\[

BA = \begin{pmatrix}

2 & 2\sqrt{2} \\

-\sqrt{2} & 2

\end{pmatrix}

\begin{pmatrix}

2 & -2\sqrt{2} \\

\sqrt{2} & 2

\end{pmatrix}

\]

5

Apply the matrix multiplication formula again.

\[

BA = \begin{pmatrix}

2×2 + 2\sqrt{2}×\sqrt{2} & 2×(-2\sqrt{2}) + 2\sqrt{2}×2 \\

-\sqrt{2}×2 + 2×\sqrt{2} & -\sqrt{2}×(-2\sqrt{2}) + 2×2

\end{pmatrix}

\]

6

Simplify the calculations.

\[

BA = \begin{pmatrix}

4 + 4 & -4\sqrt{2} + 4\sqrt{2} \\

-2\sqrt{2} + 2\sqrt{2} & 4 + 4

\end{pmatrix}

= \begin{pmatrix}

8 & 0 \\

0 & 8

\end{pmatrix}

\]

Since \( AB = BA = \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 0 \\ 0 & 8 \end{pmatrix} \), the matrices \( A \) and \( B \) satisfy the commutative property of matrix multiplication.
