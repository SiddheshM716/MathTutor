Given matrices:

\[

A = \begin{pmatrix}

1 & 2 & 0 \\

3 & 1 & 5

\end{pmatrix}, \quad

B = \begin{pmatrix}

8 & 3 & 1 \\

2 & 4 & 1 \\

5 & 3 & 1

\end{pmatrix}

\]

Find the matrix product \( AB \).

1

Verify the dimensions of the matrices. Matrix \( A \) is \( 2 \times 3 \) and matrix \( B \) is \( 3 \times 3 \), so the product \( AB \) is defined and will be \( 2 \times 3 \).

2

Use the matrix multiplication formula. The element in the \( i \)-th row and \( j \)-th column of \( AB \) is the dot product of the \( i \)-th row of \( A \) and the \( j \)-th column of \( B \).

3

Compute each element of \( AB \):

\[

AB = \begin{pmatrix}

(1×8)+(2×2)+(0×5) & (1×3)+(2×4)+(0×3) & (1×1)+(2×1)+(0×1) \\

(3×8)+(1×2)+(5×5) & (3×3)+(1×4)+(5×3) & (3×1)+(1×1)+(5×1)

\end{pmatrix}

\]

= \begin{pmatrix}

8+4+0 & 3+8+0 & 1+2+0 \\

24+2+25 & 9+4+15 & 3+1+5

\end{pmatrix}

\]
