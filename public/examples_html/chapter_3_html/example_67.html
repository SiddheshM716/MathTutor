Example 3.67

Given matrices:

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 3 & 1 & 5 \end{pmatrix}, \quad B = \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 3 & 1 \\ 2 & 4 & 1 \\ 5 & 3 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \]

Find the matrix product \( AB \).

Solution

Matrix \( A \) is \( 2 \times 3 \) and matrix \( B \) is \( 3 \times 3 \), so the product \( AB \) is defined and will be \( 2 \times 3 \):

\[ AB = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 3 & 1 & 5 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 3 & 1 \\ 2 & 4 & 1 \\ 5 & 3 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \]

\[ = \begin{pmatrix} (1×8)+(2×2)+(0×5) & (1×3)+(2×4)+(0×3) & (1×1)+(2×1)+(0×1) \\ (3×8)+(1×2)+(5×5) & (3×3)+(1×4)+(5×3) & (3×1)+(1×1)+(5×1) \end{pmatrix} \]

\[ = \begin{pmatrix} 8+4+0 & 3+8+0 & 1+2+0 \\ 24+2+25 & 9+4+15 & 3+1+5 \end{pmatrix} \]

\[ = \begin{pmatrix} 12 & 11 & 3 \\ 51 & 28 & 9 \end{pmatrix} \]
