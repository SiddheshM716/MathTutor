A company initially started with 40 workers to complete the work by 150 days. Later, it decided to fasten up the work increasing the number of workers as shown below.

Number of workers (x)

40

50

60

75

Number of days (y)

150

120

100

80

(i) Graph the above data and identify the type of variation.

(ii) From the graph, find the number of days required to complete the work if the company decides to opt for 120 workers?

(iii) If the work has to be completed by 200 days, how many workers are required?

1

Observe the given data. As the number of workers (x) increases, the number of days (y) decreases. This indicates an inverse variation.

2

The formula for inverse variation is \( y = \frac{k}{x} \), where \( k \) is the constant of variation.

3

Calculate \( k \) using any pair from the table. For example, \( k = 40 \times 150 = 6000 \). Verify with other pairs: \( 50 \times 120 = 6000 \), \( 60 \times 100 = 6000 \), \( 75 \times 80 = 6000 \). Thus, \( xy = 6000 \).

4

Plot the points (40,150), (50,120), (60,100), (75,80) and join them to form a smooth curve (rectangular hyperbola).

5

For part (ii), substitute \( x = 120 \) into \( xy = 6000 \):

\( y = \frac{6000}{120} = 50 \).

6

For part (iii), substitute \( y = 200 \) into \( xy = 6000 \):

\( x = \frac{6000}{200} = 30 \).

(i) The variation is inverse.

(ii) 50 days are required for 120 workers.

(iii) 30 workers are required to complete the work in 200 days.
