Solve \(\frac{1}{x-1} + \frac{1}{x} = \frac{5}{2}\).

1

Let \(y = \frac{1}{x-1}\). Then, we can express \(\frac{1}{x}\) in terms of \(y\) as \(\frac{1}{x} = \frac{y}{y-1}\).

2

Substitute these into the original equation:

\[

y + \frac{y}{y-1} = \frac{5}{2}

\]

3

Multiply through by \(2(y-1)\) to eliminate denominators:

\[

2y(y-1) + 2y = 5(y-1)

\]

\[

2y^2 - 2y + 2y = 5y - 5

\]

\[

2y^2 - 5y + 5 = 0

\]

4

Solve the quadratic equation \(2y^2 - 5y + 2 = 0\):

\[

y = \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{25 - 16}}{4} = \frac{5 \pm 3}{4}

\]

\[

y = \frac{1}{2} \quad \text{or} \quad y = 2

\]

5

For \(y = \frac{1}{2}\), solve for \(x\):

\[

\frac{1}{x-1} = \frac{1}{2} \implies x - 1 = 2 \implies x = 3

\]

6

For \(y = 2\), solve for \(x\):

\[

\frac{1}{x-1} = 2 \implies x - 1 = \frac{1}{2} \implies x = \frac{3}{2}

\]

The roots are \(x = \frac{3}{2}, 3\).
