(i) Multiply \(\frac{x}{y}\) by \(\frac{2y}{3}\)

(ii) Multiply \(\frac{7}{ab^2}\) by \(\frac{a - 1}{a + x}\)

1

For part (i), multiply the numerators and denominators of the fractions \(\frac{x}{y}\) and \(\frac{2y}{3}\).

2

The multiplication formula for fractions is \(\frac{a}{b} \times \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a \times c}{b \times d}\).

3

Applying the formula, \(\frac{x}{y} \times \frac{2y}{3} = \frac{x \times 2y}{y \times 3} = \frac{2xy}{3y}\). Simplify by canceling \(y\): \(\frac{2x}{3}\).

\(\frac{2x}{3}\)

Step 1: For part (ii), multiply the numerators and denominators of the fractions \(\frac{7}{ab^2}\) and \(\frac{a - 1}{a + x}\).

Step 2: Use the same multiplication formula \(\frac{a}{b} \times \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a \times c}{b \times d}\).

Step 3: Applying the formula, \(\frac{7}{ab^2} \times \frac{a - 1}{a + x} = \frac{7 \times (a - 1)}{ab^2 \times (a + x)} = \frac{7(a - 1)}{ab^2(a + x)}\). No further simplification is possible.

\(\frac{7(a - 1)}{ab^2(a + x)}\)
