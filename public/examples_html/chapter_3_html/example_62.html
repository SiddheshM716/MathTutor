If \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & -4 & 6 \\ -3 & 2 & 9 \end{bmatrix} \), \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 4 \\ 9 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \), find \( A + B \).

1

Check the dimensions of matrices \( A \) and \( B \).

Matrix \( A \) has dimensions \( 2 \times 3 \) (2 rows and 3 columns).

Matrix \( B \) has dimensions \( 2 \times 2 \) (2 rows and 2 columns).

2

Recall that matrix addition is only possible if both matrices have the same dimensions.

3

Since \( A \) and \( B \) have different dimensions (\( 2 \times 3 \) vs \( 2 \times 2 \)), they cannot be added.

The operation \( A + B \) is not possible because the matrices have different orders.
