1

Combine the fractions by finding a common denominator. The common denominator is \((a-1)(a-2)(a-3)(a-5)\).

2

Rewrite each fraction with the common denominator:

\[

\frac{a(a-1)(a-5) + 1(a-3)(a-5) - 1(a-1)(a-2)}{(a-1)(a-2)(a-3)(a-5)}

\]

3

Expand the numerators:

\[

\frac{(a^2 - 6a + 5) + (a^2 - 8a + 15) - (a^2 - 3a + 2)}{(a-1)(a-2)(a-3)(a-5)}

\]

4

\[

\frac{a^2 - 11a + 18}{(a-1)(a-2)(a-3)(a-5)}

\]

5

Factor the numerator:

\[

\frac{(a-2)(a-9)}{(a-1)(a-2)(a-3)(a-5)}

\]

6

\frac{a-9}{(a-1)(a-3)(a-5)}
