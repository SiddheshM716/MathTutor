Nishanth is the winner in a Marathon race of 12 km distance. He ran at the uniform speed of 12 km/hr and reached the destination in 1 hour. He was followed by Aradhana, Jeyanth, Sathya and Swetha with their respective speed of 6 km/hr, 4 km/hr, 3 km/hr and 2 km/hr. And, they covered the distance in 2 hrs, 3 hrs, 4 hrs and 6 hours respectively.

Draw the speed-time graph and use it to find the time taken by Kaushik with his speed of 2.4 km/hr.

2

Observe that as speed decreases, time increases, indicating an inverse variation. The formula for inverse variation is \( y = \frac{k}{x} \), where \( k \) is the constant of variation.

3

Calculate the constant \( k \) using the given data points.

For Nishanth: \( k = 12 \times 1 = 12 \).

For Aradhana: \( k = 6 \times 2 = 12 \).

For Jeyanth: \( k = 4 \times 3 = 12 \).

For Sathya: \( k = 3 \times 4 = 12 \).

For Swetha: \( k = 2 \times 6 = 12 \).

Thus, the relationship is \( xy = 12 \).

4

Plot the points (12,1), (6,2), (4,3), (3,4), (2,6) and draw a smooth curve (Rectangular Hyperbola).

5

Using the graph, find the time taken by Kaushik when his speed is 2.4 km/hr.

From the inverse variation formula \( xy = 12 \), substitute \( x = 2.4 \):

\( 2.4 \times y = 12 \)

\( y = \frac{12}{2.4} = 5 \) hours.

Kaushik takes 5 hours to complete the race at 2.4 km/hr.
