Example 7.10

If the base area of a hemispherical solid is 1386 sq. metres, then find its total surface area?

Solution: Let \( r \) be the radius of the hemisphere.

Given that, base area \( = \pi r^2 = 1386 \) sq. m and T.S.A. of a hollow sphere, the formula for finding the surface area of a hemisphere can be used.

Therefore, T.S.A. of the hemispherical solid is \( 3 \times 1386 = 4158 \) mÂ².
