If the base area of a hemispherical solid is 1386 sq. metres, then find its total surface area?

1

Let \( r \) be the radius of the hemisphere. The base area of the hemisphere is given as \( 1386 \) sq. metres.

2

The base area of a hemisphere is a circle, so its area formula is \( \pi r^2 = 1386 \).

3

The total surface area (T.S.A.) of a solid hemisphere includes the curved surface area and the base area. The formula is \( 3\pi r^2 \), since the curved surface area is \( 2\pi r^2 \) and the base area is \( \pi r^2 \).

Given \( \pi r^2 = 1386 \), the T.S.A. is calculated as \( 3 \times 1386 = 4158 \).

The total surface area of the hemispherical solid is \( 4158 \) mÂ².
