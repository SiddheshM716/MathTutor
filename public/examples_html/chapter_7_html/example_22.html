Calculate the mass of a hollow brass sphere if the inner diameter is 14 cm and thickness is 1 mm, and whose density is 17.3 g/cm

1

Identify the given values and convert units if necessary.

Inner diameter \( d = 14 \) cm, so inner radius \( r = \frac{14}{2} = 7 \) cm.

Thickness = 1 mm = \( \frac{1}{10} \) cm = 0.1 cm.

2

Calculate the outer radius \( R \).

\( R = r + \text{thickness} = 7 + 0.1 = 7.1 \) cm.

3

Use the formula for the volume of a hollow sphere:

\[ V = \frac{4}{3}\pi (R^3 - r^3) \]

4

Substitute the values and compute the volume.

\[ V = \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times (7.1^3 - 7^3) \]

\[ V = \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times (357.911 - 343) \]

\[ V = \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 14.911 \]

\[ V = 62.48 \, \text{cm}^3 \]

5

Calculate the mass using the formula \( \text{mass} = \text{density} \times \text{volume} \).

Given density \( \rho = 17.3 \, \text{g/cm}^3 \),

\[ \text{mass} = 17.3 \times 62.48 = 1080.90 \, \text{g} \]

The total mass of the hollow brass sphere is \( 1080.90 \) grams.
