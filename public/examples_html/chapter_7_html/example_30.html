Example 7.30

A cone of height 24 cm is made up of modeling clay. A child reshapes it in the form of a cylinder of the same radius as the cone. Find the height of the cylinder.

Solution: Let \( h_1 \) and \( h_2 \) be the heights of the cone and cylinder respectively. Also, let \( r \) be the radius of the cone. Given that, height of the cone \( h_1 = 24 \) cm; radius of the cone and cylinder \( r = 6 \) cm.

Since, Volume of cylinder = Volume of cone

\[ \pi r^2 h_2 = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h_1 \]

\[ h_2 = \frac{1}{3} h_1 \]

Therefore, height of cylinder is 8 cm.
