A cone of height 24 cm is made up of modeling clay. A child reshapes it in the form of a cylinder of the same radius as the cone. Find the height of the cylinder.

1

Let \( h_1 \) be the height of the cone and \( h_2 \) be the height of the cylinder. The radius \( r \) is the same for both the cone and the cylinder.

Given:

\( h_1 = 24 \) cm

\( r = 6 \) cm (same for both shapes)

2

Since the volume of the clay remains the same, we equate the volume of the cone to the volume of the cylinder.

Formula for volume of cone:

\[ V_{\text{cone}} = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h_1 \]

Formula for volume of cylinder:

\[ V_{\text{cylinder}} = \pi r^2 h_2 \]

3

Set the volumes equal and solve for \( h_2 \):

\[ \pi r^2 h_2 = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h_1 \]

Cancel common terms (\( \pi r^2 \)):

\[ h_2 = \frac{1}{3} h_1 \]

Substitute \( h_1 = 24 \) cm:

\[ h_2 = \frac{1}{3} \times 24 = 8 \text{ cm} \]

The height of the cylinder is \( 8 \) cm.
