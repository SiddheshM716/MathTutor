Example 7.16

The volume of a cylindrical water tank is 1.078 x 106 litres. If the diameter of the tank is 7 m, find its height.

Solution: Let r and h be the radius and height of the cylinder respectively.

Given that, volume of the tank = 1.078 x 106 litres = 1078 m3 (1 litre = 0.001 m3).

Diameter = 7 m ⇒ radius = 3.5 m.

Volume of the tank = πr2h cubic units.

1078 = π(3.5)2h

Therefore, height of the tank is 28 m.
