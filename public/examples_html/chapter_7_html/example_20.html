The ratio of the volumes of two cones is 2:3. Find the ratio of their radii if the height of the second cone is double the height of the first.

1

Let \( r_1 \) and \( h_1 \) be the radius and height of the first cone, and \( r_2 \) and \( h_2 \) be the radius and height of the second cone. Given \( h_2 = 2h_1 \).

2

The volume ratio is given as \( \frac{V_1}{V_2} = \frac{2}{3} \). The formula for the volume of a cone is \( V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h \).

3

Substitute the volumes into the ratio:

\[

\frac{\frac{1}{3}\pi r_1^2 h_1}{\frac{1}{3}\pi r_2^2 h_2} = \frac{2}{3}

\]

4

Simplify the equation by canceling common terms:

\[

\frac{r_1^2 h_1}{r_2^2 h_2} = \frac{2}{3}

\]

5

Substitute \( h_2 = 2h_1 \):

\[

\frac{r_1^2 h_1}{r_2^2 (2h_1)} = \frac{2}{3}

\]

6

Simplify further:

\[

\frac{r_1^2}{2r_2^2} = \frac{2}{3}

\]

7

Solve for \( \frac{r_1^2}{r_2^2} \):

\[

\frac{r_1^2}{r_2^2} = \frac{4}{3}

\]

8

Take the square root of both sides to find the ratio of radii:

\[

\frac{r_1}{r_2} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}

\]

The ratio of their radii is \( 2 : \sqrt{3} \).
