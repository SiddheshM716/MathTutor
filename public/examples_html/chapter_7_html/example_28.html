Example 7.28

A hemispherical section is cut out from one face of a cubical block such that the diameter of the hemisphere is equal to side length of the cube. Determine the surface area of the remaining solid.

Solution Let \( r \) be the radius of the hemisphere.

Given that, diameter of the hemisphere = side of the cube = \( 2r \).

Radius of the hemisphere = \( r \).

TSA of the remaining solid = Surface area of the cubical part + C.S.A. of the hemispherical part - Area of the base of the hemispherical part

\[ = 6 \times (\text{Edge})^2 + 2\pi r^2 - \pi r^2 \]

\[ = 6 \times (2r)^2 + \pi r^2 \]

\[ = 6 \times 4r^2 + \pi r^2 \]

\[ = 24r^2 + \pi r^2 \]

Total surface area of the remaining solid = \( 24r^2 + \pi r^2 \) sq. units.
