A hemispherical section is cut out from one face of a cubical block such that the diameter of the hemisphere is equal to the side length of the cube. Determine the surface area of the remaining solid.

1

Let \( r \) be the radius of the hemisphere. Given that the diameter of the hemisphere equals the side length of the cube, we have:

\[

\text{Diameter} = 2r = \text{Side of the cube}

\]

2

The total surface area (TSA) of the remaining solid consists of:

\[

\text{TSA} = \text{Surface area of the cubical part} + \text{Curved Surface Area (C.S.A.) of the hemisphere} - \text{Area of the base of the hemisphere}

\]

3

Calculate each component:

\[

\text{Surface area of the cubical part} = 6 \times (\text{Edge})^2 = 6 \times (2r)^2 = 24r^2

\]

\[

\text{C.S.A. of the hemisphere} = 2\pi r^2

\]

\[

\text{Area of the base of the hemisphere} = \pi r^2

\]

4

Combine the components:

\[

\text{TSA} = 24r^2 + 2\pi r^2 - \pi r^2 = 24r^2 + \pi r^2

\]

The total surface area of the remaining solid is \( 24r^2 + \pi r^2 \) square units.
