Example 7.12

A sphere, a cylinder and a cone have the same height which equals their radius, with the cone and cylinder having the same height. Find the ratio of their curved surface areas.

Solution

Given: For all three shapes, height (h) = radius (r)

Curved Surface Areas:

  • Sphere: \( 4\pi r^2 \)
  • Cylinder: \( 2\pi r h = 2\pi r^2 \) (since h = r)
  • Cone: \( \pi r l \) where \( l = \sqrt{r^2 + h^2} = \sqrt{2}r \)

The ratio of curved surface areas:

\[ \text{Sphere} : \text{Cylinder} : \text{Cone} = 4\pi r^2 : 2\pi r^2 : \pi r (\sqrt{2}r) \]

Simplify by dividing by \( \pi r^2 \):

\[ = 4 : 2 : \sqrt{2} \]

Multiply by \( \sqrt{2} \) to rationalize:

\[ = 2\sqrt{2} : \sqrt{2} : 1 \]

Final Ratio

\[ 2\sqrt{2} : \sqrt{2} : 1 \]
