A sphere, a cylinder and a cone have the same height which equals their radius, with the cone and cylinder having the same height. Find the ratio of their curved surface areas.

1

Given that for all three shapes, height \( h = r \) (radius).

2

Calculate the curved surface areas:

- Sphere: \( 4\pi r^2 \)

- Cylinder: \( 2\pi r h = 2\pi r^2 \) (since \( h = r \))

- Cone: \( \pi r l \), where slant height \( l = \sqrt{r^2 + h^2} = \sqrt{2}r \)

3

Write the ratio of curved surface areas:

\[ \text{Sphere} : \text{Cylinder} : \text{Cone} = 4\pi r^2 : 2\pi r^2 : \pi r (\sqrt{2}r) \]

4

Simplify the ratio by dividing by \( \pi r^2 \):

\[ 4 : 2 : \sqrt{2} \]

5

Multiply by \( \sqrt{2} \) to rationalize:

\[ 2\sqrt{2} : \sqrt{2} : 1 \]

The ratio of curved surface areas is \( 2\sqrt{2} : \sqrt{2} : 1 \).
