Example 7.17

Find the volume of the iron used to make a hollow cylinder of height 9 cm and whose internal and external radii are 21 cm and 28 cm respectively.

Let \( r \), \( R \) and \( h \) be the internal radius, external radius and height of the hollow cylinder respectively.

Given that, \( r = 21 \) cm, \( R = 28 \) cm, \( h = 9 \) cm

Now, volume of hollow cylinder \( = \pi(R^2 - r^2)h \) cu. units

\( = \pi(28^2 - 21^2) \times 9 \)

\( = \pi(784 - 441) \times 9 = 9702 \)

Therefore, volume of iron used \( = 9702 \) cm3
