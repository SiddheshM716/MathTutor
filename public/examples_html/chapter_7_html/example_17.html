Find the volume of the iron used to make a hollow cylinder of height 9 cm and whose internal and external radii are 21 cm and 28 cm respectively.

1

Identify the given values. Let \( r \) be the internal radius, \( R \) be the external radius, and \( h \) be the height of the hollow cylinder.

Given: \( r = 21 \) cm, \( R = 28 \) cm, \( h = 9 \) cm.

2

Use the formula for the volume of a hollow cylinder:

\[ \text{Volume} = \pi (R^2 - r^2) h \]

3

Substitute the given values into the formula and calculate:

\[ \text{Volume} = \pi (28^2 - 21^2) \times 9 \]

\[ = \pi (784 - 441) \times 9 \]

\[ = \pi (343) \times 9 \]

\[ = 9702 \text{ cm}^3 \]

The volume of iron used is \( 9702 \) cm
