The internal and external radii of a hollow hemispherical shell are 3 m and 5 m respectively. Find the T.S.A. and C.S.A. of the shell.

1

Identify the given values

Internal radius \( r = 3 \, \text{m} \)

External radius \( R = 5 \, \text{m} \)

2

Calculate the Curved Surface Area (C.S.A.)

Formula: \( \text{C.S.A.} = 2\pi (R^2 + r^2) \)

Calculation:

\( 2 \times \pi \times (5^2 + 3^2) = 2 \times \pi \times (25 + 9) = 2 \times \pi \times 34 \approx 213.71 \, \text{m}^2 \)

3

Calculate the Total Surface Area (T.S.A.)

Formula: \( \text{T.S.A.} = \pi (3R^2 + r^2) \)

Calculation:

\( \pi \times (3 \times 5^2 + 3^2) = \pi \times (75 + 9) = \pi \times 84 \approx 264 \, \text{m}^2 \)

C.S.A. \( = 213.71 \, \text{m}^2 \), T.S.A. \( = 264 \, \text{m}^2 \)
