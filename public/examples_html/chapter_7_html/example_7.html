From a solid cylinder whose height is 2.4 cm and diameter 1.4 cm, a conical cavity of the same height and base is hollowed out. Find the total surface area of the remaining solid.

1

Identify the given values. The height \( h \) of both the cylinder and cone is 2.4 cm, and the diameter \( d \) is 1.4 cm. Thus, the radius \( r = \frac{d}{2} = 0.7 \) cm.

2

The total surface area of the remaining solid consists of the curved surface area (C.S.A.) of the cylinder, the C.S.A. of the cone, and the area of the bottom. The formula is:

\[ \text{Total Surface Area} = 2\pi rh + \pi rl + \pi r^2 \]

where \( l \) is the slant height of the cone.

3

Calculate the slant height \( l \) of the cone using the Pythagorean theorem:

\[ l = \sqrt{r^2 + h^2} = \sqrt{0.7^2 + 2.4^2} = \sqrt{0.49 + 5.76} = \sqrt{6.25} = 2.5 \text{ cm} \]

4

Substitute the values into the formula and simplify:

\[ \text{Total Surface Area} = \pi r (2h + l + r) \]

\[ = \frac{22}{7} \times 0.7 \times \left( (2 \times 2.4) + 2.5 + 0.7 \right) \]

\[ = 2.2 \times (4.8 + 2.5 + 0.7) \]

\[ = 2.2 \times 8.0 = 17.6 \text{ cm}^2 \]

The total surface area of the remaining solid is \( 17.6 \text{ cm}^2 \).
