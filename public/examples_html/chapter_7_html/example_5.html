The radius of a conical tent is 7 m and the height is 24 m. Calculate the length of the canvas used to make the tent if the width of the rectangular canvas is 4 m?

1

Find the slant height \( l \) of the conical tent using the Pythagorean theorem.

Given radius \( r = 7 \) m and height \( h = 24 \) m, the formula is:

\[ l = \sqrt{r^2 + h^2} \]

2

Calculate the slant height.

\[ l = \sqrt{7^2 + 24^2} = \sqrt{49 + 576} = \sqrt{625} = 25 \text{ m} \]

3

Compute the curved surface area (C.S.A.) of the conical tent using the formula:

\[ \text{C.S.A.} = \pi r l \]

\[ \text{C.S.A.} = \pi \times 7 \times 25 = 550 \text{ m}^2 \]

4

Determine the length of the canvas by dividing the C.S.A. by the given width (4 m).

\[ \text{Length} = \frac{550}{4} = 137.5 \text{ m} \]

The length of the canvas is \( 137.5 \) m.
