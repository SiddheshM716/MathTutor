(i) Compare the volumes of two cylinders A and B where:

Cylinder A: radius = 3.5 cm, height = 21 cm

Cylinder B: radius = 10.5 cm, height = 7 cm

(ii) Verify whether the cylinder with greater volume has a greater surface area.

(iii) Find the ratio of the volumes of cylinders A and B.

1

Calculate the volume of both cylinders using the formula for volume of a cylinder \( V = \pi r^2 h \).

Volume of cylinder A = \( \frac{22}{7} \times (3.5)^2 \times 21 = 808.5 \text{ cm}^3 \).

Volume of cylinder B = \( \frac{22}{7} \times (10.5)^2 \times 7 = 2425.5 \text{ cm}^3 \).

Thus, the volume of cylinder B is greater than that of cylinder A.

2

Calculate the total surface area (T.S.A.) of both cylinders using the formula \( \text{T.S.A.} = 2\pi r (h + r) \).

T.S.A. of cylinder A = \( 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 3.5 \times (21 + 3.5) = 539 \text{ cm}^2 \).

T.S.A. of cylinder B = \( 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 10.5 \times (7 + 10.5) = 1155 \text{ cm}^2 \).

This confirms that cylinder B, which has a greater volume, also has a greater surface area.

3

Compute the ratio of the volumes of cylinders A and B.

Volume ratio = \( \frac{808.5}{2425.5} = \frac{1}{3} \).

The ratio of the volumes of cylinders A and B is \( 1:3 \).
