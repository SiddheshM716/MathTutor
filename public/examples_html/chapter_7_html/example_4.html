If one litre of paint covers 10 m², how many litres of paint is required to paint the internal and external surface areas of a cylindrical tunnel whose thickness is 2 m, internal radius is 6 m and height is 25 m?

1

Given the height \( h = 25 \) m, thickness = 2 m, and internal radius \( r = 6 \) m.

2

Calculate the external radius \( R \).

Since thickness = 2 m,

\( R = r + \text{thickness} = 6 + 2 = 8 \) m.

3

Find the curved surface area (C.S.A.) of the hollow cylindrical tunnel using the formula:

\[ \text{C.S.A.} = 2\pi(R + r)h \]

Substitute the values:

\[ \text{C.S.A.} = 2\pi(8 + 6) \times 25 \]

\[ \text{C.S.A.} = 2\pi \times 14 \times 25 \]

\[ \text{C.S.A.} = 2200 \, \text{m}² \]

4

Calculate the number of litres of paint required.

Given that 1 litre covers 10 m²,

\[ \text{Number of litres} = \frac{2200}{10} = 220 \]

220 litres of paint is needed to paint the tunnel.
