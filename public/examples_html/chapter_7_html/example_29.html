A metallic sphere of radius 16 cm is melted and recast into small spheres each of radius 2 cm. How many small spheres can be obtained?

1

Let \( n \) be the number of small spheres obtained. The radius of the big metallic sphere is \( R = 16 \) cm, and the radius of each small sphere is \( r = 2 \) cm.

2

The volume of the big metallic sphere must equal the total volume of all the small spheres. The formula for the volume of a sphere is \( V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3 \).

3

Set up the equation for the volumes:

\[ n \times \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3 = \frac{4}{3} \pi R^3 \]

Substitute the given values:

\[ n \times \frac{4}{3} \pi (2)^3 = \frac{4}{3} \pi (16)^3 \]

Simplify the equation:

\[ 8n = 4096 \]

Solve for \( n \):

\[ n = \frac{4096}{8} = 512 \]

512 small spheres can be obtained.
