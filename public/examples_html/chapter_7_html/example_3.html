Example 7.3

A garden roller whose length is 3 m long and whose diameter is 2.8 m is rolled to level a garden. How much area will it cover in 8 revolutions?

Solution: Given that, diameter d = 2.8 m and height = 3 m

Radius r = 1.4 m

Area covered in one revolution = curved surface area of the cylinder

= 2πrh sq. units

= 2 × π × 1.4 × 3 = 26.4

Area covered in 1 revolution = 26.4 m²

Area covered in 8 revolutions = 8 × 26.4 = 211.2

Therefore, area covered is 211.2 m²
