A garden roller whose length is 3 m long and whose diameter is 2.8 m is rolled to level a garden. How much area will it cover in 8 revolutions?

1

Given the diameter \( d = 2.8 \, \text{m} \) and height \( h = 3 \, \text{m} \).

First, find the radius \( r \) by halving the diameter.

\[ r = \frac{d}{2} = \frac{2.8}{2} = 1.4 \, \text{m} \]

2

The area covered in one revolution is equal to the curved surface area of the cylinder.

The formula for the curved surface area of a cylinder is:

\[ \text{CSA} = 2\pi rh \]

3

Substitute the values of \( r \) and \( h \) into the formula and calculate the area for one revolution.

\[ \text{CSA} = 2 \times \pi \times 1.4 \times 3 = 26.4 \, \text{m}^2 \]

4

Multiply the area for one revolution by 8 to find the total area covered in 8 revolutions.

\[ \text{Total Area} = 8 \times 26.4 = 211.2 \, \text{m}^2 \]

The roller will cover \( 211.2 \, \text{m}^2 \) in 8 revolutions.
