Example 7.31

A right circular cylindrical container of base radius 6 cm and height 15 cm is full of ice cream. The ice cream is to be filled in cones of height 9 cm and base radius 3 cm, having a hemispherical cap. Find the number of cones needed to empty the container.

Solution:

Let \( h \) and \( r \) be the height and radius of the cylinder respectively.

Given that, \( h = 15 \) cm, \( r = 6 \) cm.

Volume of the container \( V = \pi r^2 h \) cubic units.

\[ V = \pi \times 6 \times 6 \times 15 \]

Let \( r_1 = 3 \) cm, \( h_1 = 9 \) cm be the radius and height of the cone.

Also, \( r_1 = 3 \) cm is the radius of the hemispherical cap.

Volume of one ice cream cone = (Volume of the cone + Volume of the hemispherical cap)

\[ = \frac{1}{3} \pi r_1^2 h_1 + \frac{2}{3} \pi r_1^3 \]

\[ = \frac{1}{3} \pi \times 3 \times 3 \times 9 + \frac{2}{3} \pi \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 \]

\[ = \pi \times 27 + \pi \times 18 \]

\[ = 45 \pi \]

Number of cones = \(\frac{\text{Volume of the cylinder}}{\text{Volume of one ice cream cone}}\)

\[ \text{Number of ice cream cones needed} = \frac{\pi \times 6 \times 6 \times 15}{45 \pi} = 12 \]

Thus, 12 ice cream cones are required to empty the cylindrical container.
