A right circular cylindrical container of base radius 6 cm and height 15 cm is full of ice cream. The ice cream is to be filled in cones of height 9 cm and base radius 3 cm, having a hemispherical cap. Find the number of cones needed to empty the container.

1

Identify the dimensions of the cylinder and cones.

Given:

- Cylinder radius \( r = 6 \) cm, height \( h = 15 \) cm.

- Cone radius \( r_1 = 3 \) cm, height \( h_1 = 9 \) cm.

- Hemispherical cap radius \( r_1 = 3 \) cm (same as cone base).

2

Calculate the volume of the cylindrical container.

Formula: \( V_{\text{cylinder}} = \pi r^2 h \).

\[ V_{\text{cylinder}} = \pi \times 6^2 \times 15 = \pi \times 36 \times 15 = 540\pi \, \text{cm}^3. \]

3

Calculate the volume of one ice cream cone (cone + hemisphere).

Formula for cone volume: \( V_{\text{cone}} = \frac{1}{3} \pi r_1^2 h_1 \).

Formula for hemisphere volume: \( V_{\text{hemisphere}} = \frac{2}{3} \pi r_1^3 \).

\[ V_{\text{ice cream cone}} = \frac{1}{3} \pi \times 3^2 \times 9 + \frac{2}{3} \pi \times 3^3 \]

\[ = \frac{1}{3} \pi \times 9 \times 9 + \frac{2}{3} \pi \times 27 \]

\[ = 27\pi + 18\pi = 45\pi \, \text{cm}^3. \]

4

Compute the number of cones needed.

\[ \text{Number of cones} = \frac{V_{\text{cylinder}}}{V_{\text{ice cream cone}}} = \frac{540\pi}{45\pi} = 12. \]

12 cones are needed to empty the container.
