A funnel consists of a frustum of a cone attached to a cylindrical portion 12 cm long attached at the bottom. If the total height be 20 cm, diameter of the cylindrical portion be 12 cm and the diameter of the top of the funnel be 24 cm. Find the outer surface area of the funnel.

1

Identify the given dimensions and required quantities.

Let \( R \), \( r \) be the top and bottom radii of the frustum. Let \( h_1 \), \( h_2 \) be the heights of the frustum and cylinder respectively.

Given:

\( R = 12 \) cm (since diameter of top = 24 cm),

\( r = 6 \) cm (since diameter of cylindrical portion = 12 cm),

\( h_2 = 12 \) cm (height of cylindrical portion).

2

Calculate the height of the frustum \( h_1 \).

Total height = 20 cm, so \( h_1 = 20 - 12 = 8 \) cm.

3

Find the slant height \( l \) of the frustum using the formula:

\( l = \sqrt{(R - r)^2 + h_1^2} \).

Substituting values:

\( l = \sqrt{(12 - 6)^2 + 8^2} = \sqrt{36 + 64} = 10 \) cm.

4

Compute the outer surface area using the formula:

Outer surface area \( = 2\pi r h_2 + \pi (R + r) l \).

Substitute the values:

\( = \pi [2 \times 6 \times 12 + (12 + 6) \times 10] \).

\( = \pi [144 + 180] \).

\( = \pi \times 324 \).

Using \( \pi = \frac{22}{7} \):

\( = \frac{22}{7} \times 324 = 1018.28 \) cm².

The outer surface area of the funnel is \( 1018.28 \) cm².
