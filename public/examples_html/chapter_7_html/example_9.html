The radius of a spherical balloon increases from 12 cm to 16 cm as air is being pumped into it. Find the ratio of the surface area of the balloons in the two cases.

1

Identify the given radii of the balloons.

Let \( r_1 = 12 \) cm and \( r_2 = 16 \) cm.

2

Recall the formula for the curved surface area (C.S.A.) of a sphere.

The formula is \( 4\pi r^2 \).

3

Calculate the ratio of the surface areas.

\[

\frac{4\pi r_1^2}{4\pi r_2^2} = \frac{r_1^2}{r_2^2} = \frac{12^2}{16^2} = \frac{144}{256} = \frac{9}{16}

\]

The ratio of the surface areas is \( \frac{9}{16} \) or 9:16.
