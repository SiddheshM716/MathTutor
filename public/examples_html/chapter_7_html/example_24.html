A toy is in the shape of a cylinder surmounted by a hemisphere. The height of the toy is 25 cm. Find the total surface area of the toy if its common diameter is 12 cm.

1

Identify the given values and required quantities.

Given: Diameter \( d = 12 \) cm, total height of the toy = 25 cm.

We need to find the total surface area (T.S.A.) of the toy.

2

Calculate the radius and height of the cylindrical portion.

Radius \( r = \frac{d}{2} = \frac{12}{2} = 6 \) cm.

Height of the cylindrical portion \( h = 25 - 6 = 19 \) cm (since the hemisphere has radius 6 cm).

3

Write the formula for the total surface area of the toy.

T.S.A. = Curved Surface Area (C.S.A.) of the cylinder + C.S.A. of the hemisphere + Base Area of the cylinder.

T.S.A. = \( 2\pi rh + 2\pi r^2 + \pi r^2 \).

Simplify the expression:

T.S.A. = \( \pi r (2h + 3r) \).

4

Substitute the values and compute the result.

T.S.A. = \( \pi \times 6 \times (2 \times 19 + 3 \times 6) \).

T.S.A. = \( \pi \times 6 \times (38 + 18) \).

T.S.A. = \( \pi \times 6 \times 56 = 1056 \) cm².

The total surface area of the toy is \( 1056 \) cm².
