The slant height of a frustum of a cone is 5 cm and the radii of its ends are 4 cm and 1 cm. Find its curved surface area.

1

Identify the given values. Let \( l \), \( R \), and \( r \) be the slant height, top radius, and bottom radius of the frustum. Here, \( l = 5 \) cm, \( R = 4 \) cm, \( r = 1 \) cm.

2

Use the formula for the curved surface area (C.S.A.) of a frustum of a cone:

\[ \text{C.S.A.} = \pi (R + r) l \]

3

Substitute the given values into the formula and calculate:

\[ \text{C.S.A.} = \frac{22}{7} \times (4 + 1) \times 5 \]

\[ \text{C.S.A.} = \frac{22}{7} \times 5 \times 5 \]

\[ \text{C.S.A.} = \frac{550}{7} \]

The curved surface area is \( 78.57 \) cmÂ².
