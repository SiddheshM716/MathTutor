The volume of a solid hemisphere is 29106 cm

3

. Another hemisphere whose volume is two-third of the above is carved out. Find the radius of the new hemisphere.

1

Given the volume of the original hemisphere is 29106 cm

3

. The volume of the new hemisphere is two-thirds of the original hemisphere.

2

Calculate the volume of the new hemisphere using the formula:

\[ \text{Volume of new hemisphere} = \frac{2}{3} \times \text{Volume of original hemisphere} \]

3

Substitute the given volume:

\[ \text{Volume of new hemisphere} = \frac{2}{3} \times 29106 = 19404 \, \text{cm}^3 \]

4

Use the formula for the volume of a hemisphere:

\[ \frac{2}{3} \pi r^3 = 19404 \]

5

Solve for \( r^3 \):

\[ r^3 = 19404 \times \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{7}{22} \]

\[ r^3 = 9261 \]

6

Take the cube root to find \( r \):

\[ r = \sqrt[3]{9261} = 21 \, \text{cm} \]

The radius of the new hemisphere is \( 21 \, \text{cm} \).
