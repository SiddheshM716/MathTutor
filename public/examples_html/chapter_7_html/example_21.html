Example 7.21

The volume of a solid hemisphere is 29106 cm3. Another hemisphere whose volume is two-third of the above is carved out. Find the radius of the new hemisphere.

Solution: Let r be the radius of the hemisphere.

Given that, volume of the hemisphere = 29106 cm3.

Now, volume of new hemisphere = 2⁄3 (Volume of original hemisphere)

= 2⁄3 × 29106

Volume of new hemisphere = 19404 cm3

2⁄3 × 2⁄3πr3 = 19404

r3 = 19404 × 3⁄2 × 7⁄22

r3 = 9261

r = 21 cm

Therefore, r = 21 cm
