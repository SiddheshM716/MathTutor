Example 7.25

A jewel box is in the shape of a cuboid of dimensions 30 cm × 15 cm × 10 cm surmounted by a half part of a cylinder. Find the volume of the box.

Solution:

Let \( l \), \( b \), and \( h \) be the length, breadth, and height of the cuboid. Also, let \( r \) and \( h \) be the radius and height of the cylinder.

Now, Volume of the box = Volume of the cuboid + (Volume of cylinder)

\[ = (l \times b \times h) + \frac{1}{2} (\pi r^2 h) \text{ cu. units} \]

\[ = (30 \times 15 \times 10) + \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{22}{7} \times \left( \frac{15}{2} \right)^2 \times 30 \right) \]

\[ = 4500 + 2651.79 = 7151.79 \]

Therefore, Volume of the box = 7151.79 cm3.
