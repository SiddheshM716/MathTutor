A jewel box is in the shape of a cuboid of dimensions 30 cm × 15 cm × 10 cm surmounted by a half part of a cylinder. Find the volume of the box.

1

Identify the dimensions of the cuboid and the cylinder.

Given cuboid dimensions: length \( l = 30 \) cm, breadth \( b = 15 \) cm, height \( h = 10 \) cm.

For the cylinder: radius \( r = \frac{15}{2} \) cm (since the cylinder is placed on the 15 cm side), height \( h = 30 \) cm (same as the length of the cuboid).

2

Write the formula for the total volume of the box.

Total Volume = Volume of the cuboid + \(\frac{1}{2}\) (Volume of the cylinder).

3

Calculate the volume of the cuboid.

Volume of cuboid = \( l \times b \times h = 30 \times 15 \times 10 = 4500 \) cm³.

4

Calculate the volume of the cylinder (half part).

Volume of cylinder = \( \pi r^2 h = \frac{22}{7} \times \left( \frac{15}{2} \right)^2 \times 30 \).

Half volume of cylinder = \( \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 56.25 \times 30 \approx 2651.79 \) cm³.

5

Add both volumes to get the total volume.

Total Volume = \( 4500 + 2651.79 = 7151.79 \) cm³.

The volume of the box is \( 7151.79 \) cm³.
