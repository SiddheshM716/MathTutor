Example 7.23

If the radii of the circular ends of a frustum which is 45 cm high are 28 cm and 7 cm, find the volume of the frustum.

Solution: Let h, r and R be the height, top and bottom radii of the frustum.

Given that, h = 45 cm, R = 28 cm, r = 7 cm

Volume = \(\frac{1}{3} \pi (R^2 + Rr + r^2) h\) cu. units

\(= \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times (28^2 + 28 \times 7 + 7^2) \times 45\)

\(= \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 1029 \times 45 = 48510\)

Therefore, volume of the frustum is 48510 cm3.
