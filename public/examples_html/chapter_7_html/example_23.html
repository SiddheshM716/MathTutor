If the radii of the circular ends of a frustum which is 45 cm high are 28 cm and 7 cm, find the volume of the frustum.

1

Identify the given values.

Height (h) = 45 cm, Bottom radius (R) = 28 cm, Top radius (r) = 7 cm.

2

Use the formula for the volume of a frustum.

Volume = \(\frac{1}{3} \pi (R^2 + Rr + r^2) h\) cubic units.

3

Substitute the values and calculate.

Volume = \(\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times (28^2 + 28 \times 7 + 7^2) \times 45\).

= \(\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times (784 + 196 + 49) \times 45\).

= \(\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 1029 \times 45\).

= 48510.

The volume of the frustum is 48510 cm
