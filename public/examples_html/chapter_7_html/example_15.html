Find the volume of a cylinder whose height is 2 m and whose base area is 250 m².

1

Identify the given values. The height \( h \) of the cylinder is 2 m, and the base area is 250 m².

2

Recall the formula for the volume of a cylinder. The volume \( V \) is given by:

\[ V = \pi r^2 h \]

where \( r \) is the radius and \( h \) is the height.

3

Since the base area \( \pi r^2 \) is already given as 250 m², we can simplify the volume formula to:

\[ V = \text{base area} \times h \]

4

Substitute the given values into the formula:

\[ V = 250 \times 2 = 500 \, \text{m}^3 \]

The volume of the cylinder is \( 500 \, \text{m}^3 \).
