If \( f(a) = 3a - 2 \), \( g(x) = 2x + k \) and if \( f \circ g = g \circ f \), then find the value of \( k \).

1

Write down the given functions \( f(x) \) and \( g(x) \).

\( f(x) = 3x - 2 \), \( g(x) = 2x + k \)

2

Compute \( f \circ g(x) \) by substituting \( g(x) \) into \( f(x) \).

\( f \circ g(x) = f(g(x)) = f(2x + k) = 3(2x + k) - 2 = 6x + 3k - 2 \)

3

Compute \( g \circ f(x) \) by substituting \( f(x) \) into \( g(x) \).

\( g \circ f(x) = g(f(x)) = g(3x - 2) = 2(3x - 2) + k = 6x - 4 + k \)

4

Set \( f \circ g(x) \) equal to \( g \circ f(x) \) and solve for \( k \).

\( 6x + 3k - 2 = 6x - 4 + k \)

Simplify the equation:

\( 3k - k = -4 + 2 \)

\( 2k = -2 \)

\( k = -1 \)
