Find \( x \) if \( g \circ f \circ f(x) = f \circ g \circ g(x) \), given \( f(x) = 3x + 1 \) and \( g(x) = x + 3 \).

1

Compute \( g \circ f \circ f(x) \).

First, find \( f \circ f(x) \):

\[ f \circ f(x) = f(f(x)) = f(3x + 1) = 3(3x + 1) + 1 = 9x + 4 \]

Next, apply \( g \) to the result:

\[ g \circ f \circ f(x) = g(9x + 4) = (9x + 4) + 3 = 9x + 7 \]

2

Compute \( f \circ g \circ g(x) \).

First, find \( g \circ g(x) \):

\[ g \circ g(x) = g(g(x)) = g(x + 3) = (x + 3) + 3 = x + 6 \]

Next, apply \( f \) to the result:

\[ f \circ g \circ g(x) = f(x + 6) = 3(x + 6) + 1 = 3x + 19 \]

3

Set the two expressions equal and solve for \( x \).

\[ 9x + 7 = 3x + 19 \]

\[ 9x - 3x = 19 - 7 \]

\[ 6x = 12 \]

\[ x = 2 \]

\( x = 2 \)
