Let \( f \) be a function \( f : \mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{N} \) defined by \( f(x) = 3x + 2, x \in \mathbb{N} \).

i) Find the images of 1, 2, 3.

ii) Find the pre-images of 29, 53.

iii) Identify the type of function.

1

Find the images of 1, 2, 3 using the function \( f(x) = 3x + 2 \).

For \( x = 1 \), \( f(1) = 3(1) + 2 = 5 \).

For \( x = 2 \), \( f(2) = 3(2) + 2 = 8 \).

For \( x = 3 \), \( f(3) = 3(3) + 2 = 11 \).

Final Answer (i): The images of 1, 2, 3 are 5, 8, 11 respectively.

2

Find the pre-images of 29 and 53 by solving \( f(x) = 29 \) and \( f(x) = 53 \).

For \( f(x) = 29 \), \( 3x + 2 = 29 \).

Subtract 2 from both sides: \( 3x = 27 \).

Divide by 3: \( x = 9 \).

For \( f(x) = 53 \), \( 3x + 2 = 53 \).

Subtract 2 from both sides: \( 3x = 51 \).

Divide by 3: \( x = 17 \).

Final Answer (ii): The pre-images of 29 and 53 are 9 and 17 respectively.

3

Identify the type of function.

Since different elements of \( \mathbb{N} \) have different images, \( f \) is one-one.

The range of \( f = \{5, 8, 11, 14, 17, \dots\} \) is a proper subset of \( \mathbb{N} \), so \( f \) is not onto (it is into).

Final Answer (iii): The function \( f \) is one-one and into.
