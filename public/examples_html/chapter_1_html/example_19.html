Find \( f \circ g \) and \( g \circ f \) when \( f(x) = 2x + 1 \) and \( g(x) = x^2 - 2 \).

1

Given the functions \( f(x) = 2x + 1 \) and \( g(x) = x^2 - 2 \), we need to find the compositions \( f \circ g \) and \( g \circ f \).

2

To find \( f \circ g(x) \), use the formula \( f \circ g(x) = f(g(x)) \).

Substitute \( g(x) \) into \( f \):

\( f(g(x)) = f(x^2 - 2) = 2(x^2 - 2) + 1 \).

3

Simplify the expression:

\( 2(x^2 - 2) + 1 = 2x^2 - 4 + 1 = 2x^2 - 3 \).

4

To find \( g \circ f(x) \), use the formula \( g \circ f(x) = g(f(x)) \).

Substitute \( f(x) \) into \( g \):

\( g(f(x)) = g(2x + 1) = (2x + 1)^2 - 2 \).

5

Expand and simplify the expression:

\( (2x + 1)^2 - 2 = 4x^2 + 4x + 1 - 2 = 4x^2 + 4x - 1 \).

\( f \circ g(x) = 2x^2 - 3 \), \( g \circ f(x) = 4x^2 + 4x - 1 \).

Thus, \( f \circ g \neq g \circ f \).
