Let \( A = \{x \in \mathbb{N} \mid 1 < x < 4\} = \{2, 3\} \), \( B = \{x \in \mathbb{W} \mid 0 < x < 2\} = \{0, 1\} \), \( C = \{x \in \mathbb{N} \mid x < 3\} = \{1, 2\} \).

Then verify that:

(i) \( A \times (B \cup C) = (A \times B) \cup (A \times C) \)

(ii) \( A \times (B \cap C) = (A \times B) \cap (A \times C) \)

1

Verify part (i) \( A \times (B \cup C) = (A \times B) \cup (A \times C) \).

First, compute \( B \cup C \):

\( B \cup C = \{0, 1\} \cup \{1, 2\} = \{0, 1, 2\} \).

Now compute \( A \times (B \cup C) \):

\( A \times (B \cup C) = \{2, 3\} \times \{0, 1, 2\} = \{(2, 0), (2, 1), (2, 2), (3, 0), (3, 1), (3, 2)\} \) — (1).

Next, compute \( A \times B \) and \( A \times C \):

\( A \times B = \{2, 3\} \times \{0, 1\} = \{(2, 0), (2, 1), (3, 0), (3, 1)\} \).

\( A \times C = \{2, 3\} \times \{1, 2\} = \{(2, 1), (2, 2), (3, 1), (3, 2)\} \).

Now compute \( (A \times B) \cup (A \times C) \):

\( (A \times B) \cup (A \times C) = \{(2, 0), (2, 1), (3, 0), (3, 1)\} \cup \{(2, 1), (2, 2), (3, 1), (3, 2)\} \).

This simplifies to \( \{(2, 0), (2, 1), (2, 2), (3, 0), (3, 1), (3, 2)\} \) — (2).

From (1) and (2), \( A \times (B \cup C) = (A \times B) \cup (A \times C) \) is verified.

2

Verify part (ii) \( A \times (B \cap C) = (A \times B) \cap (A \times C) \).

First, compute \( B \cap C \):

\( B \cap C = \{0, 1\} \cap \{1, 2\} = \{1\} \).

Now compute \( A \times (B \cap C) \):

\( A \times (B \cap C) = \{2, 3\} \times \{1\} = \{(2, 1), (3, 1)\} \) — (3).

Next, compute \( A \times B \) and \( A \times C \) (already computed in part (i)):

\( A \times B = \{(2, 0), (2, 1), (3, 0), (3, 1)\} \).

\( A \times C = \{(2, 1), (2, 2), (3, 1), (3, 2)\} \).

Now compute \( (A \times B) \cap (A \times C) \):

\( (A \times B) \cap (A \times C) = \{(2, 0), (2, 1), (3, 0), (3, 1)\} \cap \{(2, 1), (2, 2), (3, 1), (3, 2)\} \).

This simplifies to \( \{(2, 1), (3, 1)\} \) — (4).

From (3) and (4), \( A \times (B \cap C) = (A \times B) \cap (A \times C) \) is verified.

Both (i) and (ii) are verified as true.
