If \( A = \{1, 3, 5\} \) and \( B = \{2, 3\} \), then:

(i) Find \( A \times B \) and \( B \times A \).

(ii) Is \( A \times B = B \times A \)? If not, why?

(iii) Show that \( n(A \times B) = n(B \times A) = n(A) \times n(B) \).

1

Compute \( A \times B \) (Cartesian product of A and B).

\[ A \times B = \{1, 3, 5\} \times \{2, 3\} = \{(1, 2), (1, 3), (3, 2), (3, 3), (5, 2), (5, 3)\} \]

2

Compute \( B \times A \) (Cartesian product of B and A).

\[ B \times A = \{2, 3\} \times \{1, 3, 5\} = \{(2, 1), (2, 3), (2, 5), (3, 1), (3, 3), (3, 5)\} \]

3

Compare \( A \times B \) and \( B \times A \).

The ordered pairs \((1, 2)\) and \((2, 1)\) are different, and similarly for others. Hence, \( A \times B \neq B \times A \).

4

Verify the cardinality relationship.

Given \( n(A) = 3 \) and \( n(B) = 2 \), compute:

\[ n(A \times B) = 6 \]

\[ n(B \times A) = 6 \]

\[ n(A) \times n(B) = 3 \times 2 = 6 \]

(i) \( A \times B = \{(1, 2), (1, 3), (3, 2), (3, 3), (5, 2), (5, 3)\} \), \( B \times A = \{(2, 1), (2, 3), (2, 5), (3, 1), (3, 3), (3, 5)\} \)

(ii) No, \( A \times B \neq B \times A \) because the ordered pairs are different.

(iii) \( n(A \times B) = n(B \times A) = n(A) \times n(B) = 6 \).
