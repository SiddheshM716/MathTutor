A relation \( f: X \to Y \) is defined by \( f(x) = x^3 - 2 \) where \( X = \{-2, -1, 0, 3\} \) and \( Y = \mathbb{R} \).

(i) List the elements of \( f \).

(ii) Is \( f \) a function?

1

Evaluate \( f(x) = x^3 - 2 \) for each element in the domain \( X = \{-2, -1, 0, 3\} \).

2

Calculate the image of each element:

\( f(-2) = (-2)^3 - 2 = -8 - 2 = -10 \)

\( f(-1) = (-1)^3 - 2 = -1 - 2 = -3 \)

\( f(0) = (0)^3 - 2 = 0 - 2 = -2 \)

\( f(3) = (3)^3 - 2 = 27 - 2 = 25 \)

3

List the ordered pairs of the relation \( f \):

\( f = \{(-2, -10), (-1, -3), (0, -2), (3, 25)\} \)

(i) The elements of \( f \) are \( \{(-2, -10), (-1, -3), (0, -2), (3, 25)\} \).

(ii) Yes, \( f \) is a function because each element in the domain has a unique image.
