Represent the function \( f(x) = 2x^2 - 5x + 3 \) as a composition of two functions.

1

Identify the inner function \( f_1(x) \) that can be composed with another function to form \( f(x) \). Here, we choose \( f_1(x) = 2x^2 - 5x + 3 \).

2

Identify the outer function \( f_2(x) \) such that \( f(x) = f_2(f_1(x)) \). For this case, we select \( f_2(x) = \sqrt{x} \).

3

Verify the composition by substituting \( f_1(x) \) into \( f_2(x) \):

\[ f(x) = f_2(f_1(x)) = \sqrt{2x^2 - 5x + 3} \]

The function \( f(x) = 2x^2 - 5x + 3 \) can be represented as the composition \( f(x) = f_2(f_1(x)) \), where \( f_1(x) = 2x^2 - 5x + 3 \) and \( f_2(x) = \sqrt{x} \).
