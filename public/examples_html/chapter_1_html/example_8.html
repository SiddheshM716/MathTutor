If \( X = \{-5, 1, 3, 4\} \) and \( Y = \{a, 0, c\} \), then which of the following relations are functions from \( X \) to \( Y \)?

(i) \( R = \{(-5, a), (1, 0), (3, c)\} \)

(ii) \( R = \{(-5, a), (1, a), (3, c), (4, c)\} \)

(iii) \( R = \{(-5, a), (1, a), (3, 0), (4, c), (1, 5)\} \)

1

Check if every element in \( X \) has exactly one image in \( Y \) for each relation.

2

For relation (i), \( R = \{(-5, a), (1, 0), (3, c)\} \), the element \( 4 \in X \) does not have an image in \( Y \).

3

For relation (ii), \( R = \{(-5, a), (1, a), (3, c), (4, c)\} \), each element in \( X \) has exactly one image in \( Y \).

4

For relation (iii), \( R = \{(-5, a), (1, a), (3, 0), (4, c), (1, 5)\} \), the element \( 1 \in X \) has two images \( a \in Y \) and \( 5 \in Y \).

Only relation (ii) is a function from \( X \) to \( Y \).
