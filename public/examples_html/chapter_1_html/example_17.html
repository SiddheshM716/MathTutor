Example 1.17

Let \( f \) be a function from \( \mathbb{R} \) to \( \mathbb{R} \) defined by \( f(x) = 3x - 5 \). Find the values of \( a \) and \( b \) given that \( (a, 4) \) and \( (1, b) \) belong to \( f \).

Solution:

\( f(x) = 3x - 5 \) can be written as \( f = \{(x, 3x - 5) \mid x \in \mathbb{R}\} \).

\( (a, 4) \) means the image of \( a \) is 4, i.e., \( f(a) = 4 \).

\( 3a - 5 = 4 \Rightarrow a = 3 \).

\( (1, b) \) means the image of 1 is \( b \), i.e., \( f(1) = b \).

\( 3(1) - 5 = b \Rightarrow b = -2 \).
