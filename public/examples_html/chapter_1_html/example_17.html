Let \( f \) be a function from \( \mathbb{R} \) to \( \mathbb{R} \) defined by \( f(x) = 3x - 5 \). Find the values of \( a \) and \( b \) given that \( (a, 4) \) and \( (1, b) \) belong to \( f \).

1

The function \( f(x) = 3x - 5 \) can be written as \( f = \{(x, 3x - 5) \mid x \in \mathbb{R}\} \).

2

The point \( (a, 4) \) means \( f(a) = 4 \). Using the function definition \( f(a) = 3a - 5 \).

3

Solve for \( a \):

\[ 3a - 5 = 4 \]

\[ 3a = 9 \]

\[ a = 3 \]

4

The point \( (1, b) \) means \( f(1) = b \). Using the function definition \( f(1) = 3(1) - 5 \).

5

Solve for \( b \):

\[ 3(1) - 5 = b \]

\[ b = -2 \]

\( a = 3 \) and \( b = -2 \).
