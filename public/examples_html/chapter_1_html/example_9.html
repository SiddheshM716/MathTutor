Given \( f(z) = 2z - z^2 \), find:

(i) \( f(1) \)

(ii) \( f(z+1) \)

(iii) \( f(z) + f(1) \)

1

To find \( f(1) \), substitute \( z = 1 \) into the function \( f(z) = 2z - z^2 \).

2

Apply the substitution and simplify:

\[ f(1) = 2(1) - (1)^2 \]

3

Calculate the result:

\[ f(1) = 2 - 1 = 1 \]

\( f(1) = \boxed{1} \)

---

Step 1: To find \( f(z+1) \), substitute \( z = z+1 \) into the function \( f(z) = 2z - z^2 \).

Step 2: Expand and simplify:

\[ f(z+1) = 2(z+1) - (z+1)^2 \]

\[ f(z+1) = 2z + 2 - (z^2 + 2z + 1) \]

Step 3: Combine like terms:

\[ f(z+1) = -z^2 + 1 \]

\( f(z+1) = \boxed{-z^2 + 1} \)

---

Step 1: To find \( f(z) + f(1) \), add the expressions for \( f(z) \) and \( f(1) \).

Step 2: Substitute the known values:

\[ f(z) + f(1) = (2z - z^2) + 1 \]

Step 3: Simplify the expression:

\[ f(z) + f(1) = -z^2 + 2z + 1 \]

\( f(z) + f(1) = \boxed{-z^2 + 2z + 1} \)
