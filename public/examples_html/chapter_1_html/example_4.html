Let \( A = \{3,4,7,8\} \) and \( B = \{1,7,10\} \). Which of the following sets are relations from \( A \) to \( B \)?

(i) \( R_1 = \{(3,7), (4,7), (7,10), (8,)\} \)

(ii) \( R_2 = \{(3,1), (4,12)\} \)

(iii) \( R_3 = \{(3,7), (4,10), (7,7), (7,8), (8,11), (8,7), (8,10)\} \)

1

First, compute the Cartesian product \( A \times B \), which contains all possible ordered pairs where the first element is from \( A \) and the second element is from \( B \).

\[ A \times B = \{(3,1), (3,7), (3,10), (4,1), (4,7), (4,10), (7,1), (7,7), (7,10), (8,1), (8,7), (8,10)\} \]

2

For a set to be a relation from \( A \) to \( B \), all its elements must be subsets of \( A \times B \).

3

Analyze each relation:

(i) \( R_1 = \{(3,7), (4,7), (7,10), (8,)\} \)

The pair \( (8,) \) is incomplete and invalid. However, the other pairs \( (3,7), (4,7), (7,10) \) are all in \( A \times B \). Since the invalid pair disqualifies \( R_1 \), it is not a valid relation.

(ii) \( R_2 = \{(3,1), (4,12)\} \)

The pair \( (4,12) \) is not in \( A \times B \) because \( 12 \notin B \). Thus, \( R_2 \) is not a relation from \( A \) to \( B \).

(iii) \( R_3 = \{(3,7), (4,10), (7,7), (7,8), (8,11), (8,7), (8,10)\} \)

The pairs \( (7,8) \) and \( (8,11) \) are not in \( A \times B \) because \( 8 \notin B \) and \( 11 \notin B \). Thus, \( R_3 \) is not a relation from \( A \) to \( B \).

None of the given sets \( R_1, R_2, R_3 \) are valid relations from \( A \) to \( B \).
