If the function \( f: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \) is defined by

\[

f(x) =

\begin{cases}

2x + 7, & x < -2 \\

x^2 - 2, & -2 \leq x \leq 3 \\

3x - 2, & x > 3

\end{cases}

\]

then find the values of

(i) \( f(4) \)

(ii) \( f(-2) \)

(iii) \( f(4) + 2f(1) \)

(iv) \( \frac{f(1) - 3f(4)}{f(-3)} \)

1

For part (i), determine which interval \( x = 4 \) falls into. Since \( 4 > 3 \), we use the third case of the function.

2

The formula for this interval is \( f(x) = 3x - 2 \).

3

Calculate \( f(4) = 3(4) - 2 = 12 - 2 = 10 \).

\( f(4) = 10 \)

---

Step 1: For part (ii), determine which interval \( x = -2 \) falls into. Since \( -2 \leq -2 \leq 3 \), we use the second case of the function.

Step 2: The formula for this interval is \( f(x) = x^2 - 2 \).

Step 3: Calculate \( f(-2) = (-2)^2 - 2 = 4 - 2 = 2 \).

\( f(-2) = 2 \)

---

Step 1: For part (iii), we already know \( f(4) = 10 \). Now find \( f(1) \). Since \( -2 \leq 1 \leq 3 \), we use the second case.

Step 2: The formula for this interval is \( f(x) = x^2 - 2 \).

Step 3: Calculate \( f(1) = (1)^2 - 2 = 1 - 2 = -1 \).

Step 4: Compute \( f(4) + 2f(1) = 10 + 2(-1) = 10 - 2 = 8 \).

\( f(4) + 2f(1) = 8 \)

---

Step 1: For part (iv), we know \( f(1) = -1 \) and \( f(4) = 10 \). Now find \( f(-3) \). Since \( -3 < -2 \), we use the first case.

Step 2: The formula for this interval is \( f(x) = 2x + 7 \).

Step 3: Calculate \( f(-3) = 2(-3) + 7 = -6 + 7 = 1 \).

Step 4: Compute \( \frac{f(1) - 3f(4)}{f(-3)} = \frac{-1 - 3(10)}{1} = \frac{-1 - 30}{1} = -31 \).

\( \frac{f(1) - 3f(4)}{f(-3)} = -31 \)
