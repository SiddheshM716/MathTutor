Example 1.18

If the function \( f: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \) is defined by

\[ f(x) = \begin{cases} 2x + 7, & x < -2 \\ x^2 - 2, & -2 \leq x \leq 3 \\ 3x - 2, & x > 3 \end{cases} \]

then find the values of

(i) \( f(4) \) (ii) \( f(-2) \) (iii) \( f(4) + 2f(1) \) (iv) \( \frac{f(1) - 3f(4)}{f(-3)} \)

Solution:

(i) First, we see that \( x = 4 \) lies in the third interval.

\( f(x) = 3x - 2; f(4) = 3(4) - 2 = 10 \)

(ii) \( x = -2 \) lies in the second interval.

\( f(x) = x^2 - 2; f(-2) = (-2)^2 - 2 = 2 \)

(iii) From (i), \( f(4) = 10 \).

To find \( f(1) \), first we see that \( x = 1 \) lies in the second interval.

\( f(x) = x^2 - 2; f(1) = 1^2 - 2 = -1 \)

\( f(4) + 2f(1) = 10 + 2(-1) = 8 \)

(iv) We know that \( f(1) = -1 \) and \( f(4) = 10 \).

For finding \( f(-3) \), we see that \( x = -3 \), lies in the first interval.

\( f(x) = 2x + 7; \) thus, \( f(-3) = 2(-3) + 7 = 1 \)

Hence, \( \frac{f(1) - 3f(4)}{f(-3)} = \frac{-1 - 3(10)}{1} = -31 \).
