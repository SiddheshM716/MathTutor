In the Fig.4.39, AD is the bisector of ∠A. If BD = 4cm, DC = 3cm and AB = 6cm, find AC.

1

In ΔABC, AD is the bisector of ∠A. We can use the Angle Bisector Theorem to solve for AC.

2

The Angle Bisector Theorem states: \(\frac{BD}{DC} = \frac{AB}{AC}\)

3

Substitute the given values into the theorem:

\(\frac{4}{3} = \frac{6}{AC}\)

4

Cross-multiply to solve for AC:

\(4 \times AC = 6 \times 3\)

5

Simplify the equation:

\(4AC = 18\)

6

Divide both sides by 4 to find AC:

\(AC = \frac{18}{4} = 4.5\)

\(AC = 4.5\) cm
