In ΔABC, points D, E, F lie on BC, CA, AB respectively. Suppose AB, AC, and BC have lengths 13, 14, and 15 respectively. If \(\frac{AF}{FB} = \frac{2}{5}\) and \(\frac{CE}{EA} = \frac{1}{8}\), find BD and DC.

1

Given AB = 13, AC = 14, and BC = 15. Let BD = x and DC = y.

2

Apply Ceva’s theorem:

\[

\frac{BD}{DC} \cdot \frac{CE}{EA} \cdot \frac{AF}{FB} = 1

\]

3

Substitute the given ratios \(\frac{AF}{FB} = \frac{2}{5}\) and \(\frac{CE}{EA} = \frac{1}{8}\) into Ceva’s theorem:

\[

\frac{x}{y} \cdot \frac{1}{8} \cdot \frac{2}{5} = 1

\]

4

Simplify the equation:

\[

\frac{x}{y} \cdot \frac{2}{40} = 1 \implies \frac{x}{y} = 20 \implies x = 20y

\]

5

Since BC = BD + DC = 15, we have:

\[

x + y = 15

\]

6

Substitute x = 20y into the equation x + y = 15:

\[

20y + y = 15 \implies 21y = 15 \implies y = \frac{5}{7}

\]

7

Substitute y = \(\frac{5}{7}\) back into x = 20y:

\[

x = 20 \cdot \frac{5}{7} = \frac{100}{7}

\]

BD = \(\frac{100}{7}\), DC = \(\frac{5}{7}\).
