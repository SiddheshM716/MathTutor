Example 4.33

In ΔABC, points D, E, F lie on BC, CA, AB respectively. Suppose AB, AC, and BC have lengths 13, 14, and 15 respectively. If \(\frac{AF}{FB} = \frac{2}{5}\) and \(\frac{CE}{EA} = \frac{1}{8}\), find BD and DC.

Solution: Given that AB = 13, AC = 14, and BC = 15.

Let BD = x and DC = y.

Using Ceva’s theorem, we have:

\[ \frac{BD}{DC} \cdot \frac{CE}{EA} \cdot \frac{AF}{FB} = 1 \quad \text{(1)} \]

Substitute the values of \(\frac{AF}{FB}\) and \(\frac{CE}{EA}\) in (1):

\[ \frac{x}{y} \cdot \frac{1}{8} \cdot \frac{2}{5} = 1 \]

Simplifying, we get:

\[ \frac{x}{y} \cdot \frac{2}{40} = 1 \implies \frac{x}{y} = 20 \implies x = 20y \quad \text{(2)} \]

Since BC = BD + DC = 15, we have:

\[ x + y = 15 \quad \text{(3)} \]

From (2), using x = 20y in (3), we get:

\[ 20y + y = 15 \implies 21y = 15 \implies y = \frac{15}{21} = \frac{5}{7} \]

Substitute y = \(\frac{5}{7}\) in (3), we get:

\[ x = 20 \cdot \frac{5}{7} = \frac{100}{7} \]

Hence, BD = \(\frac{100}{7}\), DC = \(\frac{5}{7}\).
