Construct a triangle similar to a given triangle PQR with its sides equal to \(\frac{3}{4}\) of the corresponding sides of the triangle PQR (scale factor \(\frac{3}{4} < 1\)).

1

Construct a triangle PQR with any measurement.

2

Draw a ray QX making an acute angle with QR on the side opposite to vertex P.

3

Locate 4 points \(Q_1, Q_2, Q_3, Q_4\) on QX so that \(QQ_1 = Q_1Q_2 = Q_2Q_3 = Q_3Q_4\).

4

Join \(Q_4\) to R and draw a line through \(Q_3\) parallel to \(Q_4R\), intersecting the extended line segment QR at \(R'\).

5

Draw a line through \(R'\) parallel to RP intersecting the extended line segment QP at \(P'\).

\(\triangle P'QR'\) is the required triangle, with each side \(\frac{3}{4}\) of the corresponding sides of \(\triangle PQR\).
