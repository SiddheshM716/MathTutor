Draw a circle of diameter 6 cm from a point P, which is 8 cm away from its centre. Draw the two tangents PA and PB to the circle and measure their lengths.

1

Given the diameter of the circle is 6 cm, calculate the radius.

The radius \( r = \frac{d}{2} = \frac{6}{2} = 3 \) cm.

2

Verify the length of the tangents using the right triangle OAP.

The formula for the length of a tangent from an external point is \( PA = \sqrt{OP^2 - OA^2} \), where \( OP \) is the distance from the point to the center and \( OA \) is the radius.

3

Substitute the known values into the formula.

\( PA = \sqrt{8^2 - 3^2} = \sqrt{64 - 9} = \sqrt{55} \approx 7.4 \) cm.

The lengths of the tangents PA and PB are both approximately 7.4 cm.
