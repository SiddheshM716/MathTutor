In △ABC, if DE || BC, AD=2, DB=x-2, AE=x+2 and EC=1 then find the lengths of the sides AB and AC.

1

Since DE is parallel to BC in △ABC, we can apply Thales' theorem, which states that the ratio of corresponding sides is equal.

2

According to Thales' theorem, \(\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}\).

3

Substitute the given values into the equation:

\[

\frac{2}{x-2} = \frac{x+2}{1}

\]

4

Simplify the equation:

\[

2 \times 1 = (x - 2)(x + 2)

\]

\[

2 = x^2 - 4

\]

5

Solve for \(x\):

\[

x^2 = 6 \implies x = \sqrt{6}

\]

6

Calculate AB and AC using \(x = \sqrt{6}\):

\[

AB = AD + DB = 2 + (\sqrt{6} - 2) = \sqrt{6}

\]

\[

AC = AE + EC = (\sqrt{6} + 2) + 1 = \sqrt{6} + 3

\]

AB = \(\sqrt{6}\), AC = \(\sqrt{6} + 3\).
