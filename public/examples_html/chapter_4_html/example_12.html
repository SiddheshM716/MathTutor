Example 4.12

In △ABC, if DE || BC, AD=2, DB=x-2, AE=x+2 and EC=2-1 then find the lengths of the sides AB and AC.

Solution: In △ABC we have DE || BC.

By Thales theorem, we have \(\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}\).

\(\frac{2}{x-2} = \frac{x+2}{2-1}\)

Hence, \(2 \times 1 = (x - 2)(x + 2)\)

So, \(2 = x^2 - 4\)

Therefore, \(x^2 = 6\) and \(x = \sqrt{6}\).

When \(x = \sqrt{6}\), AD=2, DB=\(\sqrt{6}-2\), AE=\(\sqrt{6}+2\), EC=1.

Hence, AB=AD+DB=2+\(\sqrt{6}-2\)=\(\sqrt{6}\), AC=AE+EC=\(\sqrt{6}+2\)+1=\(\sqrt{6}+3\).

Therefore, AB=\(\sqrt{6}\), AC=\(\sqrt{6}+3\).
