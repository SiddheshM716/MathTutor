Example 4.16

In the Fig. 4.40, AD is the bisector of ∠BAC, if AB = 10 cm, AC = 14 cm and BC = 6 cm. Find BD and DC.

Solution: Let BD = x cm, then DC = (6 - x) cm.

AD is the bisector of ∠A.

By Angle Bisector Theorem:

\(\frac{AB}{AC} = \frac{BD}{DC}\)

\(\frac{10}{14} = \frac{x}{6 - x}\)

\(\frac{5}{7} = \frac{x}{6 - x}\)

\(5(6 - x) = 7x\)

\(30 - 5x = 7x\)

\(12x = 30\)

\(x = 2.5\) cm

Therefore, BD = 2.5 cm, DC = 6 - 2.5 = 3.5 cm.
