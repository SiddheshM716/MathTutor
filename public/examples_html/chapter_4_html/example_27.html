In Fig. 4.65, Î”ABC is circumscribing a circle. Find the length of BC.

1

Identify the lengths of the tangents from points A and B.

AN = AM = 3 cm (Tangents drawn from the same external point are equal).

BN = BL = 4 cm (Tangents drawn from the same external point are equal).

2

Calculate the length of the remaining tangent segment from point C.

Given AC = 9 cm and AM = 3 cm, we find CM using:

\[ CM = AC - AM \]

\[ CM = 9 - 3 = 6 \, \text{cm} \]

Since CL = CM (Tangents from the same external point), CL = 6 cm.

3

Compute the total length of BC by adding the segments BL and CL.

\[ BC = BL + CL \]

\[ BC = 4 + 6 = 10 \, \text{cm} \]

The length of BC is \(\boxed{10 \, \text{cm}}\).
