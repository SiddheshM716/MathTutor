Show that in a triangle, the medians are concurrent.

1

Recall that medians are line segments joining each vertex to the midpoint of the corresponding opposite sides. Let D, E, F be the midpoints of sides BC, CA, and AB respectively.

2

Since D is the midpoint of BC, BD = DC. Therefore, the ratio \(\frac{BD}{DC} = 1\).

3

Similarly, since E is the midpoint of CA, CE = EA. Thus, the ratio \(\frac{CE}{EA} = 1\).

4

Also, since F is the midpoint of AB, AF = FB. Hence, the ratio \(\frac{AF}{FB} = 1\).

5

Multiply the three ratios obtained in Steps 2, 3, and 4:

\[

\frac{BD}{DC} \cdot \frac{CE}{EA} \cdot \frac{AF}{FB} = 1 \cdot 1 \cdot 1 = 1

\]

By Cevaâ€™s theorem, if the product of the ratios is 1, the cevians (in this case, the medians) are concurrent.

The medians of a triangle are concurrent.
