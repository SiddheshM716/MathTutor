In Fig.4.21, QA and PB are perpendiculars to AB. If AO = 10 cm, BO = 6 cm and PB = 9 cm. Find AQ.

1

Identify the similar triangles. In ΔAOQ and ΔBOP, ∠OAQ = ∠OBP = 90° (both are perpendicular to AB) and ∠AOQ = ∠BOP (vertically opposite angles).

2

By the AA Criterion of similarity, ΔAOQ ~ ΔBOP. Therefore, the corresponding sides are proportional.

3

Set up the proportion using the corresponding sides: \(\frac{AQ}{BO} = \frac{AO}{BP}\).

Substitute the given values: \(\frac{AQ}{6} = \frac{10}{9}\).

4

Solve for AQ: \(AQ = \frac{10 \times 6}{9} = \frac{60}{9} = 6.\overline{6} \text{ cm}\).

\(AQ = 6.\overline{6} \text{ cm}\)
