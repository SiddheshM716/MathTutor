Example 4.25

PQ is a chord of length 8 cm to a circle of radius 5 cm. The tangents at P and Q intersect at a point T. Find the length of the tangent TP.

Solution: Let TR = y. Since OT is perpendicular bisector of PQ, PR = QR = 4 cm.

In ΔORP, OP² = OR² + PR²

OR² = OP² − PR²

OR² = 5² − 4² = 25 − 16 = 9 ⇒ OR = 3 cm

OT = OR + RT = 3 + y ... (1)

In ΔPRT, TP² = TR² + PR² ... (2)

In ΔOPT, OT² = TP² + OP²

OT² = (TR² + PR²) + OP² (substitute for TP² from (2))

(3 + y)² = y² + 4² + 5² (substitute for OT from (1))

9 + 6y + y² = y² + 16 + 25

6y = 41 − 9 ⇒ y = 16/3

From (2), TP² = TR² + PR²

TP² = (16/3)² + 4² = 256/9 + 16 = 400/9 ⇒ TP = 20/3 cm
