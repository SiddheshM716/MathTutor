PQ is a chord of length 8 cm to a circle of radius 5 cm. The tangents at P and Q intersect at a point T. Find the length of the tangent TP.

1

Let TR = y. Since OT is perpendicular bisector of PQ, PR = QR = 4 cm (half of chord length).

2

In right triangle ORP, apply Pythagoras theorem: OP² = OR² + PR².

3

Calculate OR:

OR² = OP² − PR² = 5² − 4² = 25 − 16 = 9 ⇒ OR = 3 cm.

4

Express OT in terms of y:

OT = OR + RT = 3 + y.

5

In right triangle PRT, apply Pythagoras theorem:

TP² = TR² + PR².

6

In right triangle OPT, apply Pythagoras theorem:

OT² = TP² + OP² ⇒ (3 + y)² = y² + 4² + 5².

7

Expand and solve for y:

9 + 6y + y² = y² + 16 + 25 ⇒ 6y = 32 ⇒ y = 16/3.

8

9

TP² = (16/3)² + 4² = 256/9 + 16 = 400/9 ⇒ TP = 20/3 cm.

The length of tangent TP is \(\frac{20}{3}\) cm.
