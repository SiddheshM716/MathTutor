An insect 8 m away initially from the foot of a lamp post which is 6 m tall, crawls towards it moving through a distance. If its distance from the top of the lamp post is equal to the distance it has moved, how far is the insect away from the foot of the lamp post?

1

Identify the given values and variables.

The initial distance between the insect and the foot of the lamp post (BD) is 8 m. The height of the lamp post (AB) is 6 m. Let the distance moved by the insect be \( x \) m, and let the new position of the insect be C.

2

Define the relationships.

The distance from the top of the lamp post to the insect (AC) is equal to the distance moved by the insect (CD), so \( AC = CD = z \). The remaining distance from the insect to the foot of the lamp post is \( BC = BD - CD = 8 - z \).

3

Apply the Pythagorean theorem to triangle ABC.

Since \( \triangle ABC \) is a right-angled triangle at B, we have:

\[ AC^2 = AB^2 + BC^2 \]

Substitute the known values:

\[ z^2 = 6^2 + (8 - z)^2 \]

4

Expand and simplify the equation.

\[ z^2 = 36 + 64 - 16z + z^2 \]

Cancel \( z^2 \) from both sides:

\[ 0 = 100 - 16z \]

Solve for \( z \):

\[ 16z = 100 \]

\[ z = 6.25 \]

5

Calculate the remaining distance BC.

\[ BC = 8 - z = 8 - 6.25 = 1.75 \]

The insect is \( 1.75 \) m away from the foot of the lamp post.
