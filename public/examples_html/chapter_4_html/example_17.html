Example 4.17

Construct a ΔPQR in which PQ = 8 cm, ∠R = 60° and the median RG from R to PQ is 5.8 cm. Find the length of the altitude from R to PQ.

Solution:

Construction:

Step 1: Draw a line segment PQ = 8 cm.

Step 2: At P, draw PE such that ∠QPE = 60°.

Step 3: At P, draw PF such that ∠EPF = 90°.

Step 4: Draw the perpendicular bisector to PQ, which intersects PF at O and PQ at G.

Step 5: With O as centre and OP as radius, draw a circle.

Step 6: From G, mark arcs of radius 5.8 cm on the circle. Mark them as R and S.

Step 7: Join PR and RQ. Then ΔPQR is the required triangle.

Step 8: From R, draw a line RN perpendicular to PQ. PQ meets RN at M.

Step 9: The length of the altitude is RM = 3.8 cm.
