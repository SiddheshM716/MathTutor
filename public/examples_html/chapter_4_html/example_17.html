Construct a ΔPQR in which PQ = 8 cm, ∠R = 60° and the median RG from R to PQ is 5.8 cm. Find the length of the altitude from R to PQ.

1

Draw a line segment PQ = 8 cm.

2

At point P, construct angle ∠QPE = 60° using a protractor.

3

At point P, construct angle ∠EPF = 90° perpendicular to PE.

4

Draw the perpendicular bisector of PQ, intersecting PF at point O and PQ at point G.

5

Using O as the center and OP as the radius, draw a circle.

6

From point G, mark arcs with radius 5.8 cm on the circle, labeling the intersection points as R and S.

7

Join points PR and RQ to form the required triangle ΔPQR.

8

From point R, draw a perpendicular line RN to PQ, meeting PQ at point M.

9

Measure the length of RM, which is the altitude from R to PQ.

The length of the altitude RM is 3.8 cm.
