What length of ladder is needed to reach a height of 7 ft along the wall when the base of the ladder is 4 ft from the wall? Round off your answer to the next tenth place.

1

Let \( x \) be the length of the ladder. The given measurements are \( BC = 4 \) ft (distance from the wall) and \( AC = 7 \) ft (height on the wall).

2

Apply the Pythagorean theorem: \( AB^2 = AC^2 + BC^2 \).

3

Substitute the values and calculate:

\[

x^2 = 7^2 + 4^2 \Rightarrow x^2 = 49 + 16 \Rightarrow x^2 = 65 \Rightarrow x = \sqrt{65}

\]

4

Estimate \( \sqrt{65} \). Since \( 8^2 = 64 < 65 < 65.61 = 8.1^2 \), \( \sqrt{65} \) is between 8 and 8.1.

The length of the ladder is approximately \( \boxed{8.1} \) ft.
