Example 4.23

An aeroplane after take off from an airport and flies due north at a speed of 1000 km/hr. At the same time, another aeroplane takes off from the same airport and flies due west at a speed of 1200 km/hr. How far apart will be the two planes after 1.5 hours?

Solution: Let the first aeroplane starts from O and goes up to A towards north, where OA = 1000 × 1.5 km = 1500 km.

Let the second aeroplane starts from O at the same time and goes up to B towards west, where OB = 1200 × 1.5 = 1800 km.

The required distance to be found is BA. In right angled triangle AOB, AB² = OA² + OB².

AB² = (1500)² + (1800)² = 100² (15² + 18²) = 100² × 549 = 100² × 9 × 61.

AB = 100 × 3 × √61 = 300√61 km.
