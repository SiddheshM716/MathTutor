An aeroplane after take off from an airport and flies due north at a speed of 1000 km/hr. At the same time, another aeroplane takes off from the same airport and flies due west at a speed of 1200 km/hr. How far apart will be the two planes after 1.5 hours?

1

Calculate the distance covered by the first aeroplane (northbound).

Distance = Speed × Time = 1000 km/hr × 1.5 hr = 1500 km.

2

Calculate the distance covered by the second aeroplane (westbound).

Distance = Speed × Time = 1200 km/hr × 1.5 hr = 1800 km.

3

Use the Pythagorean theorem to find the distance between the two planes.

Formula: \( AB^2 = OA^2 + OB^2 \), where \( OA = 1500 \) km and \( OB = 1800 \) km.

Calculation:

\( AB^2 = (1500)^2 + (1800)^2 = 2,250,000 + 3,240,000 = 5,490,000 \).

\( AB = \sqrt{5,490,000} = \sqrt{100^2 \times 549} = 100 \times \sqrt{549} \).

Simplify \( \sqrt{549} \):

\( \sqrt{549} = \sqrt{9 \times 61} = 3\sqrt{61} \).

The distance between the two planes is \( 300\sqrt{61} \) km.
