If radii of two concentric circles are 4 cm and 5 cm then find the length of the chord of one circle which is a tangent to the other circle.

1

Given the radii of the two concentric circles, OA = 4 cm (radius of the smaller circle) and OB = 5 cm (radius of the larger circle). The chord BC of the larger circle is tangent to the smaller circle at point A, so OA ‚ä• BC.

2

Apply the Pythagorean theorem to the right triangle OAB:

\[ OB^2 = OA^2 + AB^2 \]

3

Substitute the known values and solve for AB:

\[ 5^2 = 4^2 + AB^2 \]

\[ 25 = 16 + AB^2 \]

\[ AB^2 = 9 \]

\[ AB = 3 \text{ cm} \]

Since BC is the chord and A is the midpoint (as OA is perpendicular to BC), the total length of BC is twice AB:

\[ BC = 2 \times AB = 2 \times 3 = 6 \text{ cm} \]

The length of the chord is \(\boxed{6 \text{ cm}}\).
