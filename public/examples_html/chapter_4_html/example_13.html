D and E are respectively the points on the sides AB and AC of a Î”ABC such that AB = 5.6 cm, AD = 1.4 cm, AC = 7.2 cm, and AE = 1.8 cm. Show that DE || BC.

1

Given AB = 5.6 cm, AD = 1.4 cm, AC = 7.2 cm, and AE = 1.8 cm. First, find the lengths of DB and EC.

2

Calculate DB using the formula DB = AB - AD.

DB = 5.6 cm - 1.4 cm = 4.2 cm.

3

Calculate EC using the formula EC = AC - AE.

EC = 7.2 cm - 1.8 cm = 5.4 cm.

4

Compute the ratios \(\frac{AD}{DB}\) and \(\frac{AE}{EC}\).

\(\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{1.4}{4.2} = \frac{1}{3}\).

\(\frac{AE}{EC} = \frac{1.8}{5.4} = \frac{1}{3}\).

5

Since \(\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}\), by the converse of the Basic Proportionality Theorem, DE is parallel to BC.

DE || BC.
