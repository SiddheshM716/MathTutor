Show that ΔPST ~ ΔPQR in the following cases:

In ΔPST and ΔPQR, \(\frac{PS}{PQ} = \frac{2}{2+1} = \frac{2}{3}\), \(\frac{PT}{PR} = \frac{4}{4+2} = \frac{2}{3}\).

In ΔPST and ΔPQR, \(\frac{PS}{PQ} = \frac{2}{2+3} = \frac{2}{5}\), \(\frac{PT}{PR} = \frac{2}{2+3} = \frac{2}{5}\).

1

For part (1), verify the given ratios \(\frac{PS}{PQ} = \frac{2}{3}\) and \(\frac{PT}{PR} = \frac{2}{3}\).

2

Note that ∠P is common to both ΔPST and ΔPQR.

3

Since \(\frac{PS}{PQ} = \frac{PT}{PR}\) and ∠P is common, by the SAS similarity criterion, ΔPST ~ ΔPQR.

ΔPST ~ ΔPQR (proved for part 1).

Step 1: For part (2), verify the given ratios \(\frac{PS}{PQ} = \frac{2}{5}\) and \(\frac{PT}{PR} = \frac{2}{5}\).

Step 2: Note that ∠P is common to both ΔPST and ΔPQR.

Step 3: Since \(\frac{PS}{PQ} = \frac{PT}{PR}\) and ∠P is common, by the SAS similarity criterion, ΔPST ~ ΔPQR.

ΔPST ~ ΔPQR (proved for part 2).
