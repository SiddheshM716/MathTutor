Example 4.1

  1. Show that ΔPST ~ ΔPQR

    In ΔPST and ΔPQR,

    \(\frac{PS}{PQ} = \frac{2}{2+1} = \frac{2}{3}\), \(\frac{PT}{PR} = \frac{4}{4+2} = \frac{2}{3}\)

    Thus, \(\frac{PS}{PQ} = \frac{PT}{PR}\) and ∠P is common.

    Therefore, by SAS similarity, ΔPST ~ ΔPQR.

  2. In ΔPST and ΔPQR,

    \(\frac{PS}{PQ} = \frac{2}{2+3} = \frac{2}{5}\), \(\frac{PT}{PR} = \frac{2}{2+3} = \frac{2}{5}\)

    Thus, \(\frac{PS}{PQ} = \frac{PT}{PR}\) and ∠P is common.

    Therefore, by SAS similarity, ΔPST ~ ΔPQR.
