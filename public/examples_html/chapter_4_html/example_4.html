Example 4.4

A boy of height 90 cm is walking away from the base of a lamp post at a speed of 1.2 m/sec. If the lamppost is 3.6 m above the ground, find the length of his shadow cast after 4 seconds.

Solution: Given, speed = 1.2 m/s, time = 4 seconds

Distance = speed × time = 1.2 × 4 = 4.8 m

Let x be the length of the shadow after 4 seconds.

Since, ΔABE ~ ΔCDB, \(\frac{BE}{DB} = \frac{AE}{CD}\) gives \(\frac{4.8 + x}{x} = \frac{3.6}{0.9}\) (since 90 cm = 0.9 m)

\(\frac{4.8 + x}{x} = 4\) gives \(4.8 + x = 4x\) so, \(3x = 4.8\) and \(x = 1.6\) m

The length of his shadow DE = 1.6 m
