A boy of height 90 cm is walking away from the base of a lamp post at a speed of 1.2 m/sec. If the lamppost is 3.6 m above the ground, find the length of his shadow cast after 4 seconds.

1

Calculate the distance the boy walks in 4 seconds.

Given speed = 1.2 m/s and time = 4 seconds.

2

Use the formula Distance = Speed × Time.

Distance = 1.2 × 4 = 4.8 m.

3

Let \( x \) be the length of the shadow.

Since triangles \( \Delta ABE \) and \( \Delta CDB \) are similar, the ratio of corresponding sides is equal.

4

Set up the proportion \( \frac{BE}{DB} = \frac{AE}{CD} \).

Substitute the given values: \( \frac{4.8 + x}{x} = \frac{3.6}{0.9} \).

5

Simplify the equation.

\( \frac{4.8 + x}{x} = 4 \) leads to \( 4.8 + x = 4x \).

6

Solve for \( x \).

\( 3x = 4.8 \) gives \( x = 1.6 \) m.

The length of his shadow is \( \boxed{1.6 \text{ m}} \).
