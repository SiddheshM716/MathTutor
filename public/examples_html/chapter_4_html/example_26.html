In Fig.4.64, O is the centre of a circle. PQ is a chord and the tangent PR at P makes an angle of 50° with PQ. Find ∠POQ.

1

Determine ∠OPQ using the property that the angle between a tangent and radius is 90°.

Given ∠RPQ = 50°, we have:

\[

∠OPQ = 90° - 50° = 40°

\]

2

Recognize that OP = OQ (both are radii of the circle), so △OPQ is an isosceles triangle.

Thus, the base angles are equal:

\[

∠OPQ = ∠OQP = 40°

\]

3

Use the angle sum property of a triangle to find ∠POQ.

\[

∠POQ = 180° - ∠OPQ - ∠OQP = 180° - 40° - 40° = 100°

\]

\[

∠POQ = 100°

\]
