Draw a triangle ABC of base BC = 8 cm, ∠A = 60° and the bisector of ∠A meets BC at D such that BD = 6 cm.

1

Draw a line segment BC = 8 cm.

2

At point B, construct angle ∠CBE = 60° using a protractor.

3

At point B, construct angle ∠EBF = 90° such that BF is perpendicular to BE.

4

Draw the perpendicular bisector of BC, which intersects BF at point O and BC at point G.

5

Using O as the center and OB as the radius, draw a circle.

6

From point B, mark an arc of 6 cm on BC to locate point D.

7

The perpendicular bisector intersects the circle at point I. Join points I and D.

8

Extend ID to meet the circle at point A. Finally, join points A to B and A to C.

ΔABC is the required triangle with BC = 8 cm, ∠A = 60°, and BD = 6 cm.
