Example 4.8

If ΔABC is similar to ΔDEF such that BC = 3 cm, EF = 4 cm and area of ΔABC = 54 cm². Find the area of ΔDEF.

Since the ratio of area of two similar triangles is equal to the ratio of the squares of any two corresponding sides, we have

\(\frac{\text{Area}(\Delta ABC)}{\text{Area}(\Delta DEF)} = \frac{BC^2}{EF^2}\)

\(\frac{54}{\text{Area}(\Delta DEF)} = \frac{3^2}{4^2}\)

\(\text{Area}(\Delta DEF) = \frac{54 \times 16}{9} = 96 \text{ cm}^2\)
