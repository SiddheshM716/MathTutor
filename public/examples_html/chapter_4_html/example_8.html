If ΔABC is similar to ΔDEF such that BC = 3 cm, EF = 4 cm and area of ΔABC = 54 cm². Find the area of ΔDEF.

1

Since ΔABC and ΔDEF are similar, the ratio of their areas is equal to the ratio of the squares of their corresponding sides.

2

The formula for the ratio of areas of similar triangles is:

\[

\frac{\text{Area}(\Delta ABC)}{\text{Area}(\Delta DEF)} = \frac{BC^2}{EF^2}

\]

3

Substitute the given values into the formula:

\[

\frac{54}{\text{Area}(\Delta DEF)} = \frac{3^2}{4^2} = \frac{9}{16}

\]

4

Solve for \(\text{Area}(\Delta DEF)\):

\[

\text{Area}(\Delta DEF) = \frac{54 \times 16}{9} = 96 \text{ cm}^2

\]

The area of ΔDEF is \(\boxed{96 \text{ cm}^2}\).
