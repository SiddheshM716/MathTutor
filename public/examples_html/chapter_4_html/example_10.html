Construct a triangle similar to a given triangle \( PQR \) with its sides equal to \( \frac{3}{5} \) of the corresponding sides of the triangle \( PQR \) (scale factor \( = \frac{3}{5} < 1 \)).

1

Draw the given triangle \( \triangle PQR \) with any arbitrary measurements.

2

From vertex \( Q \), draw a ray \( QX \) making an acute angle with \( QR \) on the side opposite to vertex \( P \).

3

On the ray \( QX \), mark 5 equal segments \( QQ_1 = Q_1Q_2 = Q_2Q_3 = Q_3Q_4 = Q_4Q_5 \). (Here, 5 is the denominator of the scale factor \( \frac{3}{5} \)).

4

Join \( Q_5 \) to \( R \) and construct a line through \( Q_3 \) (since 3 is the numerator of the scale factor) parallel to \( Q_5R \), intersecting \( QR \) at \( R' \).

5

Draw a line through \( R' \) parallel to \( RP \), intersecting \( QP \) at \( P' \).

The resulting triangle \( \triangle P'QR' \) is the required triangle, with each side \( \frac{3}{5} \) of the corresponding sides of \( \triangle PQR \).
