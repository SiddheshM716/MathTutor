Find the length of the tangent drawn from a point whose distance from the centre of a circle is 5 cm and radius of the circle is 3 cm.

1

Given the distance from the point to the center of the circle \( OP = 5 \) cm and the radius \( r = 3 \) cm.

2

The tangent \( PT \) is perpendicular to the radius \( OT \) at the point of contact. Using the Pythagoras theorem in right-angled \( \Delta OTP \):

\[ OP^2 = OT^2 + PT^2 \]

3

Substitute the known values:

\[ 5^2 = 3^2 + PT^2 \]

\[ 25 = 9 + PT^2 \]

\[ PT^2 = 25 - 9 = 16 \]

\[ PT = \sqrt{16} = 4 \text{ cm} \]

The length of the tangent \( PT \) is \( 4 \) cm.
