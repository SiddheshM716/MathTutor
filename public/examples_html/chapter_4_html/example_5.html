In ΔCAB and ΔCED, ∠C is common, ∠A = ∠CED. Therefore, ΔCAB ~ ΔCED (By AA similarity). Hence, \(\frac{CA}{CE} = \frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{CB}{CD}\). Given: DE = 8, CD = 12.

1

Since ΔCAB ~ ΔCED by AA similarity, the corresponding sides are proportional.

2

The proportionality of corresponding sides is given by \(\frac{CA}{CE} = \frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{CB}{CD}\).

3

Given DE = 8 and CD = 12, we can write the ratio \(\frac{CB}{CD} = \frac{CB}{12}\).

The ratio \(\frac{CB}{CD} = \frac{CB}{12}\) is derived from the similarity of the triangles.
