The perimeters of two similar triangles ABC and PQR are respectively 36 cm and 24 cm. If PQ = 10 cm, find AB.

1

The ratio of the corresponding sides of similar triangles is equal to the ratio of their perimeters.

2

Since ΔABC ~ ΔPQR, the ratio of their corresponding sides is given by:

\[

\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{BC}{QR} = \frac{AC}{PR} = \frac{\text{Perimeter of } \Delta ABC}{\text{Perimeter of } \Delta PQR} = \frac{36}{24}

\]

3

Substitute the given value of PQ = 10 cm into the ratio:

\[

\frac{AB}{10} = \frac{36}{24}

\]

4

Solve for AB:

\[

AB = \frac{36 \times 10}{24} = 15 \text{ cm}

\]

AB = 15 cm
