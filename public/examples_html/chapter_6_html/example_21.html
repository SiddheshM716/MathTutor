Two ships are sailing in the sea on either sides of a lighthouse. The angle of elevation of the top of the lighthouse as observed from the ships are 30° and 45° respectively. If the lighthouse is 200 m high, find the distance between the two ships. (√3 = 1.732)

1

Let AB be the lighthouse (height = 200 m). Let C and D be the positions of the two ships, with angles of elevation ∠ACB = 30° and ∠ADB = 45°.

2

For ship C, use the tangent formula in right triangle BAC:

\[ \tan 30° = \frac{AB}{AC} \]

3

Substitute the known values:

\[ \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{200}{AC} \]

\[ AC = 200\sqrt{3} \]

4

For ship D, use the tangent formula in right triangle BAD:

\[ \tan 45° = \frac{AB}{AD} \]

5

Substitute the known values:

\[ 1 = \frac{200}{AD} \]

\[ AD = 200 \]

6

The total distance between the two ships (CD) is the sum of AC and AD:

\[ CD = AC + AD = 200\sqrt{3} + 200 \]

\[ CD = 200(\sqrt{3} + 1) \]

\[ CD = 200(1.732 + 1) = 200 \times 2.732 = 546.4 \]

The distance between the two ships is \( 546.4 \) m.
