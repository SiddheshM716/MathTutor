From a window (h metres high above the ground) of a house in a street, the angles of elevation and depression of the top and the foot of another house on the opposite side of the street are θ and φ, respectively. Show that the height of the opposite house is \( h(1 + \frac{\cotθ}{\cotφ}) \).

1

Let W be the point on the window where the angles of elevation and depression are measured. Let PQ be the house on the opposite side. Then WA is the width of the street. The height of the window is \( h \) metres, which is equal to AQ.

2

Let PA = \( x \) metres. In the right-angled triangle \( \Delta PAW \), we use the tangent formula for angle θ:

\[ \tanθ = \frac{AP}{AW} \]

\[ \tanθ = \frac{x}{AW} \]

\[ AW = \frac{x}{\tanθ} \]

\[ AW = x \cotθ \quad \text{(1)} \]

3

In the right-angled triangle \( \Delta QAW \), we use the tangent formula for angle φ:

\[ \tanφ = \frac{AQ}{AW} \]

\[ \tanφ = \frac{h}{AW} \]

\[ AW = h \cotφ \quad \text{(2)} \]

4

Equate (1) and (2):

\[ x \cotθ = h \cotφ \]

\[ x = \frac{h \cotφ}{\cotθ} \]

5

The total height of the opposite house is the sum of PA and AQ:

\[ \text{Height} = x + h = \frac{h \cotφ}{\cotθ} + h \]

\[ \text{Height} = h \left(1 + \frac{\cotθ}{\cotφ}\right) \]

The height of the opposite house is \( h \left(1 + \frac{\cotθ}{\cotφ}\right) \).
