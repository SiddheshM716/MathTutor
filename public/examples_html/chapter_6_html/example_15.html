Show that:

\[

\frac{1 + \cot^2 A}{1 - \cot A} = \frac{1 + \tan^2 A}{1 - \tan A}

\]

1

Start with the left-hand side (LHS) and express everything in terms of \(\tan A\).

\[

\cot A = \frac{1}{\tan A}

\]

2

Substitute \(\cot A\) in the LHS.

\[

\text{LHS} = \frac{1 + \left(\frac{1}{\tan A}\right)^2}{1 - \frac{1}{\tan A}} = \frac{1 + \frac{1}{\tan^2 A}}{\frac{\tan A - 1}{\tan A}}

\]

3

Simplify the numerator and denominator.

\[

\text{LHS} = \frac{\frac{\tan^2 A + 1}{\tan^2 A}}{\frac{\tan A - 1}{\tan A}} = \frac{\tan^2 A + 1}{\tan^2 A} \cdot \frac{\tan A}{\tan A - 1} = \frac{\tan^2 A + 1}{\tan A (\tan A - 1)}

\]

4

Recognize that \(\tan^2 A + 1 = \sec^2 A\) and rewrite the expression.

\[

\text{LHS} = \frac{\sec^2 A}{\tan A (\tan A - 1)}

\]

5

Now, consider the right-hand side (RHS).

\[

\text{RHS} = \frac{1 + \tan^2 A}{1 - \tan A} = \frac{\sec^2 A}{1 - \tan A}

\]

6

Compare LHS and RHS.

\[

\frac{\sec^2 A}{\tan A (\tan A - 1)} = \frac{\sec^2 A}{1 - \tan A}

\]

Multiply numerator and denominator of LHS by \(-1\):

\[

\frac{\sec^2 A}{-\tan A (1 - \tan A)} = \frac{\sec^2 A}{1 - \tan A}

\]

Thus, LHS = RHS.

\[

\frac{1 + \cot^2 A}{1 - \cot A} = \frac{1 + \tan^2 A}{1 - \tan A}

\]
