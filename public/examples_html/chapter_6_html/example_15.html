Example 6.15

Show that:

\[ \frac{1 + \cot^2 A}{1 - \cot A} = \frac{1 + \tan^2 A}{1 - \tan A} \]

Solution:

LHS = RHS

\[ \frac{1 + \tan^2 A}{\tan A} = \frac{1 - \tan A}{\tan A} \]

\[ \frac{1 + \tan^2 A}{\tan A} = \frac{1 - \tan A}{\tan A} \]

From (1) and (2),

\[ \frac{1 + \tan^2 A}{1 - \tan A} = \frac{1 + \cot^2 A}{1 - \cot A} \]

\[ \frac{(1 + \cot A + \tan A)(\sin A - \cos A)}{\sec^2 A - \csc^2 A} \]

\[ \frac{(1 + \cot A + \tan A)(\sin A - \cos A)}{\sec^2 A - \csc^2 A} \]

\[ \frac{1 + \frac{\cos A}{\sin A} + \frac{\sin A}{\cos A}}{(\sec A - \csc A)(\sec^2 A + \sec A \csc A + \csc^2 A)} \]
