As shown in the figure, two trees are standing on flat ground. The angle of elevation of the top of both the trees from a point X on the ground is 40°. If the horizontal distance between X and the smaller tree is 8 m and the distance of the top of the two trees is 20 m, calculate:

(i) the distance between the point X and the top of the smaller tree.

(ii) the horizontal distance between the two trees.

(cos 40° = 0.7660)

1

Let AB be the height of the bigger tree and CD be the height of the smaller tree. X is the point on the ground.

2

For part (i), in the right-angled triangle XCD, we use the cosine formula:

\[ \cos 40° = \frac{\text{XD}}{\text{CD}} \]

3

Rearranging the formula to find XD:

\[ \text{XD} = \frac{\text{CD}}{\cos 40°} = \frac{8}{0.7660} = 10.44 \text{ m} \]

Final Answer (i): The distance between X and the top of the smaller tree is \( 10.44 \) m.

4

For part (ii), in the right-angled triangle XAB, we again use the cosine formula:

\[ \cos 40° = \frac{\text{AX}}{\text{AB}} \]

5

The total distance from X to the top of the bigger tree (AB) is the sum of the distance between the tops of the two trees (20 m) and the distance from X to the top of the smaller tree (10.44 m):

\[ \text{AB} = 20 + 10.44 = 30.44 \text{ m} \]

6

Now, calculate AX:

\[ \text{AX} = \text{AB} \cdot \cos 40° = 30.44 \times 0.7660 = 23.382 \text{ m} \]

7

The horizontal distance between the two trees is the difference between AX and CX (which is 8 m):

\[ \text{AC} = \text{AX} - \text{CX} = 23.382 - 8 = 15.382 \text{ m} \]

Final Answer (ii): The horizontal distance between the two trees is \( 15.38 \) m (rounded to two decimal places).
