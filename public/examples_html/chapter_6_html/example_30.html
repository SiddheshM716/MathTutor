A man is watching a boat speeding away from the top of a tower. The boat makes an angle of depression of 60° with the man’s eye when at a distance of 200 m from the tower. After 10 seconds, the angle of depression becomes 45°. What is the approximate speed of the boat (in km/hr), assuming that it is sailing in still water? (√3 = 1.732)

1

Let AB be the tower, and C and D be the initial and final positions of the boat. The angle of depression from A to C is 60°, and after 10 seconds, it becomes 45° at point D. The horizontal distance BC is 200 m.

2

In right triangle ABC, use the tangent formula for angle 60°:

\[ \tan 60° = \frac{AB}{BC} \]

3

Substitute the known values:

\[ \sqrt{3} = \frac{AB}{200} \]

\[ AB = 200\sqrt{3} \, \text{m} \]

4

In right triangle ABD, use the tangent formula for angle 45°:

\[ \tan 45° = \frac{AB}{BD} \]

5

Substitute the value of AB:

\[ 1 = \frac{200\sqrt{3}}{BD} \]

\[ BD = 200\sqrt{3} \, \text{m} \]

6

Calculate the distance CD traveled by the boat:

\[ CD = BD - BC \]

\[ CD = 200\sqrt{3} - 200 \]

\[ CD = 200(\sqrt{3} - 1) \approx 146.4 \, \text{m} \]

7

Compute the speed of the boat in m/s:

\[ \text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{146.4}{10} = 14.64 \, \text{m/s} \]

8

Convert the speed to km/hr:

\[ 14.64 \, \text{m/s} \times \frac{3600}{1000} = 52.704 \, \text{km/hr} \]

The approximate speed of the boat is \( 52.704 \, \text{km/hr} \).
