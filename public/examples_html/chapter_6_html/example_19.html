A tower stands vertically on the ground. From a point on the ground, which is 48 m away from the foot of the tower, the angle of elevation of the top of the tower is 30°. Find the height of the tower.

1

Let PQ be the height of the tower, where PQ = h. The distance between the tower and the point is QR = 48 m. In the right-angled △PQR, ∠PRQ = 30°.

2

The tangent of an angle in a right-angled triangle is the ratio of the opposite side to the adjacent side. The formula is:

\[ \tan \theta = \frac{\text{opposite side}}{\text{adjacent side}} \]

3

Applying the formula to △PQR:

\[ \tan 30° = \frac{PQ}{QR} = \frac{h}{48} \]

\[ \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{h}{48} \]

\[ h = \frac{48}{\sqrt{3}} = 16\sqrt{3} \]

The height of the tower is \( 16\sqrt{3} \) m.
