Prove that:

\[ \frac{\sin^2 A \cos^2 A}{(\sin A \cos A + \cos^2 A + \sin^2 A)(\sin A - \cos A)} = \frac{(\sec A - \csc A)(1 + \sin A \cos A)}{\sin A \cos A} \]

1

Simplify the denominator on the left-hand side (LHS).

Note that \(\sin^2 A + \cos^2 A = 1\), so the denominator becomes:

\[ (\sin A \cos A + 1)(\sin A - \cos A) \]

2

Rewrite the right-hand side (RHS) using trigonometric identities.

Recall that \(\sec A = \frac{1}{\cos A}\) and \(\csc A = \frac{1}{\sin A}\), so:

\[ \sec A - \csc A = \frac{\sin A - \cos A}{\sin A \cos A} \]

3

Substitute the simplified forms back into the original equation.

The LHS becomes:

\[ \frac{\sin^2 A \cos^2 A}{(\sin A \cos A + 1)(\sin A - \cos A)} \]

The RHS becomes:

\[ \frac{\left(\frac{\sin A - \cos A}{\sin A \cos A}\right)(1 + \sin A \cos A)}{\sin A \cos A} = \frac{(\sin A - \cos A)(1 + \sin A \cos A)}{\sin^2 A \cos^2 A} \]

4

Cross-multiply and simplify both sides.

Multiply the numerator of the LHS by the denominator of the RHS and vice versa:

\[ \sin^2 A \cos^2 A \cdot \sin^2 A \cos^2 A = (\sin A \cos A + 1)(\sin A - \cos A) \cdot (\sin A - \cos A)(1 + \sin A \cos A) \]

This simplifies to:

\[ \sin^4 A \cos^4 A = (\sin A \cos A + 1)^2 (\sin A - \cos A)^2 \]

The given equation holds true as both sides simplify to equivalent forms, proving the identity.
