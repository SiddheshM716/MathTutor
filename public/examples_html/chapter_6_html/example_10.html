Prove that \(\frac{\sin A}{1 + \cos A} + \frac{\sin A}{1 - \cos A} = 2 \csc A\).

1

Combine the two fractions by finding a common denominator.

\[

\frac{\sin A}{1 + \cos A} + \frac{\sin A}{1 - \cos A} = \frac{\sin A (1 - \cos A) + \sin A (1 + \cos A)}{(1 + \cos A)(1 - \cos A)}

\]

2

Expand the numerator and simplify the denominator.

\[

= \frac{\sin A - \sin A \cos A + \sin A + \sin A \cos A}{1 - \cos^2 A}

\]

3

Simplify the numerator and use the Pythagorean identity \(1 - \cos^2 A = \sin^2 A\).

\[

= \frac{2 \sin A}{\sin^2 A} = \frac{2}{\sin A} = 2 \csc A

\]

\(\frac{\sin A}{1 + \cos A} + \frac{\sin A}{1 - \cos A} = 2 \csc A\)
