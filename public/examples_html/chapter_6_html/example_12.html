Example 6.12

Prove that:

\[ \tan^2 A - \tan^2 B = \frac{\sin^2 A - \sin^2 B}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B} \]

Proof

Starting with the left-hand side (LHS):

\[ \tan^2 A - \tan^2 B = \frac{\sin^2 A}{\cos^2 A} - \frac{\sin^2 B}{\cos^2 B} \]

Combine the fractions:

\[ = \frac{\sin^2 A \cos^2 B - \sin^2 B \cos^2 A}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B} \]

Express \(\cos^2\) terms using \(\sin^2\):

\[ = \frac{\sin^2 A (1 - \sin^2 B) - \sin^2 B (1 - \sin^2 A)}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B} \]

Expand the numerator:

\[ = \frac{\sin^2 A - \sin^2 A \sin^2 B - \sin^2 B + \sin^2 A \sin^2 B}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B} \]

Simplify by canceling terms:

\[ = \frac{\sin^2 A - \sin^2 B}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B} \]

Which equals the right-hand side (RHS). Therefore, the identity is proved.
