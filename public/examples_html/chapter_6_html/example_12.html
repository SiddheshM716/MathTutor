Prove that:

\[

\tan^2 A - \tan^2 B = \frac{\sin^2 A - \sin^2 B}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]

1

Start with the left-hand side (LHS) and express \(\tan^2 A\) and \(\tan^2 B\) in terms of \(\sin\) and \(\cos\):

\[

\tan^2 A - \tan^2 B = \frac{\sin^2 A}{\cos^2 A} - \frac{\sin^2 B}{\cos^2 B}

\]

2

Combine the fractions into a single fraction:

\[

= \frac{\sin^2 A \cos^2 B - \sin^2 B \cos^2 A}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]

3

Use the Pythagorean identity \(\cos^2 x = 1 - \sin^2 x\) to rewrite the \(\cos^2\) terms:

\[

= \frac{\sin^2 A (1 - \sin^2 B) - \sin^2 B (1 - \sin^2 A)}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]

4

Expand the numerator:

\[

= \frac{\sin^2 A - \sin^2 A \sin^2 B - \sin^2 B + \sin^2 A \sin^2 B}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]

5

Simplify the numerator by canceling the \(\sin^2 A \sin^2 B\) terms:

\[

= \frac{\sin^2 A - \sin^2 B}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]

The simplified form matches the right-hand side (RHS), proving the identity:

\[

\tan^2 A - \tan^2 B = \frac{\sin^2 A - \sin^2 B}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]
