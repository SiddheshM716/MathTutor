Example 6.28

From the top of a tower 50 m high, the angles of depression of the top and bottom of a tree are observed to be 30° and 45° respectively. Find the height of the tree.

Solution: The height of the tower AB = 50 m. Let the height of the tree CD = y and BD = z.

From the diagram, ∠AC = 30° = ∠ACM and ∠AD = 45° = ∠ADB.

In the right angled △ABD,

tan 45° = AB / BD

In the right angled △AMC,

tan 30° = AM / CM

1 / √3 = AM / z

AM = z / √3

z = 50 / √3 = 28.87 m.

Therefore, height of the tree = CD = MB = AB - AM = 50 - 28.87 = 21.13 m.
