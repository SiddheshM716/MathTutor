From the top of a tower 50 m high, the angles of depression of the top and bottom of a tree are observed to be 30° and 45° respectively. Find the height of the tree.

1

Let the height of the tower AB = 50 m. Let the height of the tree CD = y and the distance BD = z.

2

In right-angled △ABD, we use the tangent formula for angle 45°:

\[ \tan 45° = \frac{AB}{BD} \]

3

Since \(\tan 45° = 1\), we get:

\[ 1 = \frac{50}{z} \implies z = 50 \, \text{m} \]

4

In right-angled △AMC, we use the tangent formula for angle 30°:

\[ \tan 30° = \frac{AM}{CM} \]

5

Since \(\tan 30° = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\) and CM = BD = z = 50 m, we get:

\[ \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{AM}{50} \implies AM = \frac{50}{\sqrt{3}} \approx 28.87 \, \text{m} \]

6

The height of the tree CD is the difference between the tower height and AM:

\[ CD = AB - AM = 50 - 28.87 = 21.13 \, \text{m} \]

The height of the tree is \(\boxed{21.13 \, \text{m}}\).
