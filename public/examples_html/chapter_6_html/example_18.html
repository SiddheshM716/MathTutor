Calculate ∠BAC in the given triangles. (tan 38.7° = 0.8011, tan 69.4° = 2.6604)

(i) In the right-angled ΔABC [see Fig. 6.12(a)]

tan θ = opposite side / adjacent side = 4 / 5

(ii) In the right-angled ΔABC [see Fig. 6.12(b)]

tan θ = opposite side / adjacent side = 8 / 3

1

For part (i), we are given the ratio of the opposite side to the adjacent side as 4/5.

2

The formula for tangent of an angle θ in a right-angled triangle is:

\[ \tan θ = \frac{\text{opposite side}}{\text{adjacent side}} \]

3

Here, \(\tan θ = \frac{4}{5} = 0.8\). Comparing with the given value \(\tan 38.7° = 0.8011\), we conclude:

\[ ∠BAC = 38.7° \]

For part (i), \(∠BAC = 38.7°\).

Step 1: For part (ii), we are given the ratio of the opposite side to the adjacent side as 8/3.

Step 2: The formula for tangent of an angle θ in a right-angled triangle is:

\[ \tan θ = \frac{\text{opposite side}}{\text{adjacent side}} \]

Step 3: Here, \(\tan θ = \frac{8}{3} ≈ 2.6667\). Comparing with the given value \(\tan 69.4° = 2.6604\), we conclude:

\[ ∠BAC = 69.4° \]

For part (ii), \(∠BAC = 69.4°\).
