As observed from the top of a 60 m high lighthouse from the sea level, the angles of depression of two ships are 28° and 45°. If one ship is exactly behind the other on the same side of the lighthouse, find the distance between the two ships. (tan 28° = 0.5317)

1

Let the observer on the lighthouse CD be at D. The height of the lighthouse CD is 60 m. From the diagram, the angles of depression are ∠XDA = 28° = ∠DAC and ∠XDB = 45° = ∠DBC.

2

In the right-angled ΔDCB, we use the formula tan θ = opposite/adjacent. Here, θ = 45°.

3

tan 45° = CD/BC ⇒ 1 = 60/BC ⇒ BC = 60 m.

4

In the right-angled ΔDCA, we again use tan θ = opposite/adjacent. Here, θ = 28° and tan 28° = 0.5317.

5

tan 28° = CD/AC ⇒ 0.5317 = 60/AC ⇒ AC = 60/0.5317 = 112.85 m.

The distance between the two ships AB = AC − BC = 112.85 m − 60 m = 52.85 m.
