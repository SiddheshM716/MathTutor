An aeroplane sets off from G on a bearing of 24° towards H, a point 250 km away. At H it changes course and heads towards J deviates further by 55° and a distance of 180 km away.

How far is H to the North of G?

How far is H to the East of G?

How far is J to the North of H?

How far is J to the East of H?

Given: $\sin 24° = 0.4067$, $\sin 11° = 0.1908$, $\cos 24° = 0.9135$, $\cos 11° = 0.9816$

1

Find the distance of H to the North of G.

Using the formula $\cos \theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$ in right-angled triangle GOH:

$\cos 24° = \frac{OG}{GH}$

$0.9135 = \frac{OG}{250}$

$OG = 250 \times 0.9135 = 228.38 \text{ km}$

The distance of H to the North of G is $\boxed{228.38 \text{ km}}$.

Step 2: Find the distance of H to the East of G.

Using the formula $\sin \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$ in right-angled triangle GOH:

$\sin 24° = \frac{OH}{GH}$

$0.4067 = \frac{OH}{250}$

$OH = 250 \times 0.4067 = 101.68 \text{ km}$

The distance of H to the East of G is $\boxed{101.68 \text{ km}}$.

Step 3: Find the distance of J to the North of H.

Using the formula $\sin \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$ in right-angled triangle HIJ:

$\sin 11° = \frac{IJ}{HJ}$

$0.1908 = \frac{IJ}{180}$

$IJ = 180 \times 0.1908 = 34.34 \text{ km}$

The distance of J to the North of H is $\boxed{34.34 \text{ km}}$.

Step 4: Find the distance of J to the East of H.

Using the formula $\cos \theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$ in right-angled triangle HIJ:

$\cos 11° = \frac{HI}{HJ}$

$0.9816 = \frac{HI}{180}$

$HI = 180 \times 0.9816 = 176.69 \text{ km}$

The distance of J to the East of H is $\boxed{176.69 \text{ km}}$.
