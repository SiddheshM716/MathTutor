From a point on the ground, the angles of elevation of the bottom and top of a tower fixed at the top of a 30 m high building are 45° and 60° respectively. Find the height of the tower. (√3 = 1.732)

1

Let AC be the height of the tower and AB be the height of the building. Given AB = 30 m, and let AC = h metres.

2

In the right-angled ΔCBP, where ∠CPB = 60°, we use the tangent formula:

\[ \tan 60° = \frac{BC}{BP} \]

\[ \sqrt{3} = \frac{30 + h}{BP} \]

3

In the right-angled ΔABP, where ∠APB = 45°, we again use the tangent formula:

\[ \tan 45° = \frac{AB}{BP} \]

\[ 1 = \frac{30}{BP} \]

\[ BP = 30 \]

4

Substitute BP = 30 into the first equation:

\[ \sqrt{3} = \frac{30 + h}{30} \]

\[ 30 + h = 30\sqrt{3} \]

\[ h = 30\sqrt{3} - 30 \]

\[ h = 30(\sqrt{3} - 1) \]

5

Substitute √3 = 1.732 and calculate:

\[ h = 30(1.732 - 1) \]

\[ h = 30(0.732) \]

\[ h = 21.96 \]

The height of the tower is \( 21.96 \) m.
