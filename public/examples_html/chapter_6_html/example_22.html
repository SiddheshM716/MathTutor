Example 6.22

From a point on the ground, the angles of elevation of the bottom and top of a tower fixed at the top of a 30 m high building are 45° and 60° respectively. Find the height of the tower. (√3 = 1.732)

Let AC be the height of the tower. Let AB be the height of the building. Then, AC = h metres, AB = 30 m.

In the right angled ΔCBP, ∠CPB = 60°

tan 60° = (BC) / (BP) ⇒ √3 = (30 + h) / BP

In the right angled ΔABP, ∠APB = 45°

tan 45° = (AB) / (BP) ⇒ 1 = 30 / BP ⇒ BP = 30

Substituting BP = 30 in the first equation, we get √3 = (30 + h) / 30

h = 30(√3 - 1) = 30(1.732 - 1) = 30(0.732) = 21.96

Hence, the height of the tower is 21.96 m.
