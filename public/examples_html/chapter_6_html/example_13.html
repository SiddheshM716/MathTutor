Prove that:

\[

\tan^2 A - \tan^2 B = \frac{\sin^2 A - \sin^2 B}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]

1

Start with the left-hand side (LHS) of the equation:

\[

\tan^2 A - \tan^2 B

\]

2

Express \(\tan^2 A\) and \(\tan^2 B\) in terms of sine and cosine:

\[

\tan^2 A = \frac{\sin^2 A}{\cos^2 A}, \quad \tan^2 B = \frac{\sin^2 B}{\cos^2 B}

\]

3

Substitute these into the LHS:

\[

\frac{\sin^2 A}{\cos^2 A} - \frac{\sin^2 B}{\cos^2 B}

\]

4

Combine the fractions over a common denominator:

\[

\frac{\sin^2 A \cos^2 B - \sin^2 B \cos^2 A}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]

5

Use the Pythagorean identity \(\cos^2 x = 1 - \sin^2 x\) to rewrite \(\cos^2 A\) and \(\cos^2 B\):

\[

\frac{\sin^2 A (1 - \sin^2 B) - \sin^2 B (1 - \sin^2 A)}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]

6

Expand the numerator:

\[

\frac{\sin^2 A - \sin^2 A \sin^2 B - \sin^2 B + \sin^2 A \sin^2 B}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]

7

Simplify the numerator by canceling terms:

\[

\frac{\sin^2 A - \sin^2 B}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]

The right-hand side (RHS) matches the simplified form of the LHS, proving the identity:

\[

\tan^2 A - \tan^2 B = \frac{\sin^2 A - \sin^2 B}{\cos^2 A \cos^2 B}

\]
