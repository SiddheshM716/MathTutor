A pole 5 m high is fixed on the top of a tower. The angle of elevation of the top of the pole observed from a point ‘A’ on the ground is 60° and the angle of depression to the point ‘A’ from the top of the tower is 45°. Find the height of the tower. ($\sqrt{3} = 1.732$)

1

Let BC be the height of the tower and CD be the height of the pole. Let ‘A’ be the point of observation. Let BC = x and AB = y.

2

From the diagram, $\angle BAD = 60°$ and $\angle XCA = 45° = \angle BAC$.

In the right-angled $\triangle ABC$, use the formula $\tan 45° = \frac{BC}{AB}$.

3

$\tan 45° = \frac{x}{y} \Rightarrow 1 = \frac{x}{y} \Rightarrow y = x$ (1).

In the right-angled $\triangle ABD$, use the formula $\tan 60° = \frac{BD}{AB} = \frac{BC + CD}{AB}$.

$\sqrt{3} = \frac{x + 5}{y}$.

Substitute $y = x$ from (1): $\sqrt{3}x = x + 5$.

Rearrange: $x(\sqrt{3} - 1) = 5 \Rightarrow x = \frac{5}{\sqrt{3} - 1}$.

Rationalize the denominator: $x = \frac{5(\sqrt{3} + 1)}{(\sqrt{3} - 1)(\sqrt{3} + 1)} = \frac{5(1.732 + 1)}{2} = 6.83$.

The height of the tower is $\boxed{6.83 \text{ m}}$.
