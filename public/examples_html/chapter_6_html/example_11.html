If $\cscθ + \cotθ = P$, then prove that $\cosθ = \frac{P² - 1}{P² + 1}$.

1

Given $\cscθ + \cotθ = P$ (Equation 1).

2

Recall the identity $\csc²θ - \cot²θ = 1$.

3

Rewrite the identity as $(\cscθ - \cotθ)(\cscθ + \cotθ) = 1$.

4

Substitute Equation 1 into the identity to get $\cscθ - \cotθ = \frac{1}{P}$ (Equation 2).

5

Add Equation 1 and Equation 2 to get $2\cscθ = P + \frac{1}{P}$ (Equation 3).

6

Subtract Equation 2 from Equation 1 to get $2\cotθ = P - \frac{1}{P}$ (Equation 4).

7

Divide Equation 4 by Equation 3 to get $\frac{\cotθ}{\cscθ} = \frac{P - \frac{1}{P}}{P + \frac{1}{P}}$.

8

Simplify the right side to $\frac{P² - 1}{P² + 1}$ and note that $\frac{\cotθ}{\cscθ} = \cosθ$.

$\cosθ = \frac{P² - 1}{P² + 1}$.
