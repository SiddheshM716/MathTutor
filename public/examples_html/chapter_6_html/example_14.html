Prove that

\[

\frac{\sin A \cos A}{\sec A + \tan A - 1} + \frac{\sin A \cos A}{\csc A + \cot A - 1} = 1

\]

1

Combine the two fractions on the left-hand side (LHS) into a single fraction.

\[

\frac{\sin A \cos A (\csc A + \cot A - 1) + \sin A \cos A (\sec A + \tan A - 1)}{(\sec A + \tan A - 1)(\csc A + \cot A - 1)}

\]

2

Simplify the numerator by expanding the terms.

\[

\sin A \cos A \csc A + \sin A \cos A \cot A - \sin A \cos A + \sin A \cos A \sec A + \sin A \cos A \tan A - \sin A \cos A

\]

3

Use trigonometric identities to simplify further.

\[

\sin A \cos A \cdot \frac{1}{\sin A} + \sin A \cos A \cdot \frac{\cos A}{\sin A} - \sin A \cos A + \sin A \cos A \cdot \frac{1}{\cos A} + \sin A \cos A \cdot \frac{\sin A}{\cos A} - \sin A \cos A

\]

4

Cancel terms where possible.

\[

\cos A + \cos^2 A - \sin A \cos A + \sin A + \sin^2 A - \sin A \cos A

\]

5

Combine like terms and use the Pythagorean identity \(\sin^2 A + \cos^2 A = 1\).

\[

1 + \cos A + \sin A - 2 \sin A \cos A

\]

6

Simplify the denominator using common denominators.

\[

\left(\frac{1 + \sin A - \cos A}{\cos A}\right) \left(\frac{1 + \cos A - \sin A}{\sin A}\right)

\]

7

Multiply the denominators and simplify.

\[

\frac{(1 + \sin A - \cos A)(1 + \cos A - \sin A)}{\sin A \cos A}

\]

8

Expand the denominator and simplify using the identity \(1 - (\sin A - \cos A)^2 = 2 \sin A \cos A\).

\[

\frac{2 \sin A \cos A}{2 \sin A \cos A} = 1

\]

The given expression simplifies to \(1\).
