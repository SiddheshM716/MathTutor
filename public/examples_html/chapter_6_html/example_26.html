A player sitting on the top of a tower of height 20 m observes the angle of depression of a ball lying on the ground as 60°. Find the distance between the foot of the tower and the ball. (√3 = 1.732)

1

Let BC be the height of the tower and A be the position of the ball lying on the ground. Given:

- BC = 20 m

- ∠CAB = 60°

- Let AB = x metres (distance between foot of tower and ball).

2

In the right-angled ΔABC, we use the tangent formula:

\[ \tan 60° = \frac{\text{opposite side}}{\text{adjacent side}} = \frac{BC}{AB} \]

3

Substitute the known values:

\[ \sqrt{3} = \frac{20}{x} \]

4

Solve for x:

\[ x = \frac{20}{\sqrt{3}} \]

5

Rationalize the denominator:

\[ x = \frac{20 \times \sqrt{3}}{3} \]

6

Substitute √3 = 1.732 and calculate:

\[ x = \frac{20 \times 1.732}{3} = \frac{34.64}{3} = 11.55 \, \text{m} \]

The distance between the foot of the tower and the ball is \(\boxed{11.55 \, \text{m}}\).
