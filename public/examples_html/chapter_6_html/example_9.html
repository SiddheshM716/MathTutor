Prove that \((\csc \theta - \sin \theta) (\sec \theta - \cos \theta) (\tan \theta + \cot \theta) = 1\)

1

Rewrite each trigonometric function in terms of sine and cosine.

\[

(\csc \theta - \sin \theta) = \left( \frac{1}{\sin \theta} - \sin \theta \right) = \frac{1 - \sin^2 \theta}{\sin \theta}

\]

\[

(\sec \theta - \cos \theta) = \left( \frac{1}{\cos \theta} - \cos \theta \right) = \frac{1 - \cos^2 \theta}{\cos \theta}

\]

\[

(\tan \theta + \cot \theta) = \left( \frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta} + \frac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta} \right) = \frac{\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta}{\sin \theta \cos \theta}

\]

2

Substitute the simplified forms back into the original expression.

\[

\left( \frac{1 - \sin^2 \theta}{\sin \theta} \right) \left( \frac{1 - \cos^2 \theta}{\cos \theta} \right) \left( \frac{\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta}{\sin \theta \cos \theta} \right)

\]

3

Simplify the expression using trigonometric identities.

\[

1 - \sin^2 \theta = \cos^2 \theta \quad \text{and} \quad 1 - \cos^2 \theta = \sin^2 \theta \quad \text{and} \quad \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1

\]

\[

= \left( \frac{\cos^2 \theta}{\sin \theta} \right) \left( \frac{\sin^2 \theta}{\cos \theta} \right) \left( \frac{1}{\sin \theta \cos \theta} \right)

\]

4

Multiply the terms and simplify.

\[

= \frac{\cos^2 \theta \sin^2 \theta \cdot 1}{\sin^2 \theta \cos^2 \theta} = 1

\]
