Example 6.2

Prove that \(\frac{1 - \cos A}{1 + \cos A} = \left(\frac{\sin A}{1 + \cos A}\right)^2\)

Solution:

Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate of \(1 + \cos A\):

\[ \frac{1 - \cos A}{1 + \cos A} \cdot \frac{1 - \cos A}{1 - \cos A} = \frac{(1 - \cos A)^2}{(1 + \cos A)(1 - \cos A)} \]

Simplify the denominator:

\[ \frac{(1 - \cos A)^2}{1 - \cos^2 A} = \frac{(1 - \cos A)^2}{\sin^2 A} \]

Simplify the expression:

\[ \left(\frac{1 - \cos A}{\sin A}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{\sin A}{1 + \cos A}\right)^2 \]
