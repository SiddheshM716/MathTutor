Prove that \(\frac{1 + \cos \theta}{1 - \cos \theta} = \csc \theta + \cot \theta\)

1

Multiply the numerator and denominator by the conjugate of \(1 - \cos \theta\), which is \(1 + \cos \theta\).

\[

\frac{1 + \cos \theta}{1 - \cos \theta} = \left(\frac{1 + \cos \theta}{1 - \cos \theta}\right) \times \left(\frac{1 + \cos \theta}{1 + \cos \theta}\right)

\]

2

Simplify the denominator using the identity \(\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1\).

\[

\frac{(1 + \cos \theta)^2}{1 - \cos^2 \theta} = \frac{1 + 2\cos \theta + \cos^2 \theta}{\sin^2 \theta}

\]

3

Rewrite the expression in terms of \(\csc \theta\) and \(\cot \theta\).

\[

\frac{1}{\sin^2 \theta} + \frac{2\cos \theta}{\sin^2 \theta} + \frac{\cos^2 \theta}{\sin^2 \theta} = \csc^2 \theta + 2\csc \theta \cot \theta + \cot^2 \theta

\]

4

Recognize the expression as a perfect square.

\[

(\csc \theta + \cot \theta)^2

\]

Taking the square root of both sides (and considering the positive root for the given identity), we have:

\[

\frac{1 + \cos \theta}{1 - \cos \theta} = \csc \theta + \cot \theta

\]
