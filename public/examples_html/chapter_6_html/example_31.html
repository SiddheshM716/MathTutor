From the top of a 12 m high building, the angle of elevation of the top of a cable tower is 60° and the angle of depression of its foot is 30°. Determine the height of the tower.

1

Let OA be the building with height 12 m, and PP' be the cable tower. O is the observation point on top of the building.

2

Draw OM perpendicular to PP'. The angle of elevation of P (top of tower) is ∠MOP = 60°, and the angle of depression of P' (bottom of tower) is ∠MOP' = 30°.

3

In right triangle OMP, use the tangent formula for angle 60°:

\[ \frac{MP}{OM} = \tan 60° \]

\[ \frac{h - 12}{OM} = \sqrt{3} \]

\[ OM = \frac{h - 12}{\sqrt{3}} \quad (1) \]

4

In right triangle OMP', use the tangent formula for angle 30°:

\[ \frac{MP'}{OM} = \tan 30° \]

\[ \frac{12}{OM} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \]

\[ OM = 12\sqrt{3} \quad (2) \]

5

Equate equations (1) and (2):

\[ \frac{h - 12}{\sqrt{3}} = 12\sqrt{3} \]

\[ h - 12 = 12\sqrt{3} \times \sqrt{3} \]

\[ h - 12 = 36 \]

\[ h = 48 \]

The height of the cable tower is \(\boxed{48 \text{ m}}\).
