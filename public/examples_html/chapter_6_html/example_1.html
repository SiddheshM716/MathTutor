Prove that \(\tan^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta = \tan^2 \theta \sin^2 \theta\).

1

Start with the left-hand side (LHS) of the equation: \(\tan^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta\).

2

Express \(\tan^2 \theta\) in terms of \(\sin \theta\) and \(\cos \theta\):

\[

\tan^2 \theta = \frac{\sin^2 \theta}{\cos^2 \theta}

\]

3

Substitute this into the LHS:

\[

\tan^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta = \frac{\sin^2 \theta}{\cos^2 \theta} - \sin^2 \theta

\]

4

Factor out \(\tan^2 \theta\):

\[

= \tan^2 \theta (1 - \cos^2 \theta)

\]

5

Use the identity \(1 - \cos^2 \theta = \sin^2 \theta\):

\[

= \tan^2 \theta \sin^2 \theta

\]

\(\tan^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta = \tan^2 \theta \sin^2 \theta\) is proven.
