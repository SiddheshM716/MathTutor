In a class of 50 students, 28 opted for NCC, 30 opted for NSS and 18 opted both NCC and NSS. One of the students is selected at random. Find the probability that:

(i) The student opted for NCC but not NSS.

(ii) The student opted for NSS but not NCC.

(iii) The student opted for exactly one of them.

1

Identify the total number of students and the given data.

Total number of students \( n(S) = 50 \).

Let \( A \) be the event of students opting for NCC, and \( B \) be the event of students opting for NSS.

Given: \( n(A) = 28 \), \( n(B) = 30 \), \( n(A \cap B) = 18 \).

2

Calculate the probabilities of events \( A \), \( B \), and \( A \cap B \).

\( P(A) = \frac{n(A)}{n(S)} = \frac{28}{50} \).

\( P(B) = \frac{n(B)}{n(S)} = \frac{30}{50} \).

\( P(A \cap B) = \frac{n(A \cap B)}{n(S)} = \frac{18}{50} \).

3

Find the probability that the student opted for NCC but not NSS.

Use the formula \( P(A \cap B') = P(A) - P(A \cap B) \).

Calculation: \( P(A \cap B') = \frac{28}{50} - \frac{18}{50} = \frac{10}{50} = \frac{1}{5} \).

Final Answer (i): \( \frac{1}{5} \).

4

Find the probability that the student opted for NSS but not NCC.

Use the formula \( P(A' \cap B) = P(B) - P(A \cap B) \).

Calculation: \( P(A' \cap B) = \frac{30}{50} - \frac{18}{50} = \frac{12}{50} = \frac{6}{25} \).

Final Answer (ii): \( \frac{6}{25} \).

5

Find the probability that the student opted for exactly one of them.

Use the formula \( P(\text{exactly one}) = P(A \cap B') + P(A' \cap B) \).

Calculation: \( \frac{10}{50} + \frac{12}{50} = \frac{22}{50} = \frac{11}{25} \).

Final Answer (iii): \( \frac{11}{25} \).
