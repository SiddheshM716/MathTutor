The amount of rainfall (in cm) for 6 days: 17.8, 19.2, 16.3, 12.5, 12.8, 11.4. Find the standard deviation.

1

Arrange the data in ascending order: 11.4, 12.5, 12.8, 16.3, 17.8, 19.2.

2

Calculate the mean using the formula:

\[

\text{Mean} = \frac{\sum x_i}{n}

\]

\[

\text{Mean} = \frac{11.4 + 12.5 + 12.8 + 16.3 + 17.8 + 19.2}{6} = \frac{90}{6} = 15 \text{ cm}

\]

3

Compute deviations from the mean and their squares:

\[

\begin{array}{|c|c|c|}

\hline

\text{Rainfall (xᵢ)} & \text{Deviation (dᵢ = xᵢ - 15)} & \text{dᵢ²} \\

\hline

11.4 & -3.6 & 12.96 \\

12.5 & -2.5 & 6.25 \\

12.8 & -2.2 & 4.84 \\

16.3 & 1.3 & 1.69 \\

17.8 & 2.8 & 7.84 \\

19.2 & 4.2 & 17.64 \\

\hline

\text{Total} & & \Sigma dᵢ² = 51.22 \\

\hline

\end{array}

\]

4

Calculate the standard deviation using the formula:

\[

\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\Sigma d_i^2}{n}}

\]

\[

\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{51.22}{6}} = \sqrt{8.53} \approx 2.9 \text{ cm}

\]

The standard deviation is \( \boxed{2.9 \text{ cm}} \).
