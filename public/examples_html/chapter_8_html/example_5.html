Example 8.5

The amount of rainfall (in cm) for 6 days: 17.8, 19.2, 16.3, 12.5, 12.8, 11.4. Find the standard deviation.

Solution

Data in ascending order: 11.4, 12.5, 12.8, 16.3, 17.8, 19.2

Step 1: Calculate Mean

\[ \text{Mean} = \frac{11.4 + 12.5 + 12.8 + 16.3 + 17.8 + 19.2}{6} = \frac{90}{6} = 15 \text{ cm} \]

Step 2: Calculate Deviations and Squares

Rainfall (xᵢ) Deviation (dᵢ = xᵢ - 15) dᵢ²
11.4-3.612.96
12.5-2.56.25
12.8-2.24.84
16.31.31.69
17.82.87.84
19.24.217.64
Total Σdᵢ² = 51.22

Step 3: Calculate Standard Deviation

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\Sigma d_i^2}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{51.22}{6}} = \sqrt{8.53} \approx 2.9 \text{ cm} \]

Alternative: Assumed Mean Method

When the mean is not integer, use an assumed mean (A):

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\Sigma d_i^2}{n} - \left(\frac{\Sigma d_i}{n}\right)^2} \]

Where \( d_i = x_i - A \) and A is typically the middle value.
