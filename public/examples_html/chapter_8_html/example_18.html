A bag contains 5 blue balls and 4 green balls. A ball is drawn at random from the bag. Find the probability that the ball drawn is (i) blue (ii) not blue.

1

Calculate the total number of possible outcomes.

Total balls = blue balls + green balls = 5 + 4 = 9.

Thus, \( n(S) = 9 \).

2

For part (i), find the probability of drawing a blue ball.

Let \( A \) be the event of drawing a blue ball.

Number of favorable outcomes \( n(A) = 5 \).

Probability formula: \( P(A) = \frac{n(A)}{n(S)} \).

3

Substitute the values into the formula.

\( P(A) = \frac{5}{9} \).

4

For part (ii), find the probability of not drawing a blue ball.

The probability of the complement event is \( P(\overline{A}) = 1 - P(A) \).

Substitute \( P(A) = \frac{5}{9} \).

\( P(\overline{A}) = 1 - \frac{5}{9} = \frac{4}{9} \).

(i) \( \frac{5}{9} \)

(ii) \( \frac{4}{9} \)
