Example 8.11

48 students reported their weekly TV watching hours. Find the standard deviation.

Given Data:

Calculations:

\[ \text{Mean } \bar{x} = \frac{\sum x_i f_i}{N} = \frac{432}{48} = 9 \text{ hours} \]

\[ \text{Standard deviation } \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum f_i d_i^2}{N}} = \sqrt{\frac{124}{48}} = \sqrt{2.58} \approx 1.6 \text{ hours} \]

Alternative: Assumed Mean Method

For data with frequencies:

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum f_i d_i^2}{N} - \left(\frac{\sum f_i d_i}{N}\right)^2} \]

Where \( d_i = x_i - A \) and A is the assumed mean.
