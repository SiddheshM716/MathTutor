Example 8.16

The following table gives the values of mean and variance of heights and weights of the 10th standard students of a school.

Height Weight
Mean: 155 cm Mean: 46.50 kg
Variance: 72.25 cm² Variance: 28.09 kg²

Which is more varying than the other?

Solution: For comparing two data, first we have to find their coefficient of variations

Mean x̄₁ = 155 cm, variance σ₁² = 72.25 cm²

Therefore standard deviation σ₁ = 8.5 cm

Coefficient of variation CV₁ = (σ₁/x̄₁) × 100%

CV₁ = (8.5/155) × 100% = 5.48% (for heights)

Mean x̄₂ = 46.50 kg, Variance σ₂² = 28.09 kg²

Standard deviation σ₂ = 5.3 kg

Coefficient of variation CV₂ = (σ₂/x̄₂) × 100%

CV₂ = (5.3/46.50) × 100% = 11.40% (for weights)

CV₁ = 5.48% and CV₂ = 11.40%

Height is more consistent.
