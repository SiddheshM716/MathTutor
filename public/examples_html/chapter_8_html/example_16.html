The following table gives the values of mean and variance of heights and weights of the 10th standard students of a school.

Height

Weight

Mean: 155 cm

Mean: 46.50 kg

Variance: 72.25 cm²

Variance: 28.09 kg²

Which is more varying than the other?

1

To compare variation, we calculate the coefficient of variation (CV) for both height and weight.

2

For height, given mean $\bar{x}_1 = 155$ cm and variance $\sigma_1^2 = 72.25$ cm².

First, find standard deviation: $\sigma_1 = \sqrt{72.25} = 8.5$ cm.

Coefficient of variation formula: $CV_1 = \left(\frac{\sigma_1}{\bar{x}_1}\right) \times 100\%$.

3

Calculate $CV_1$:

$CV_1 = \left(\frac{8.5}{155}\right) \times 100\% = 5.48\%$.

4

For weight, given mean $\bar{x}_2 = 46.50$ kg and variance $\sigma_2^2 = 28.09$ kg².

First, find standard deviation: $\sigma_2 = \sqrt{28.09} = 5.3$ kg.

Coefficient of variation formula: $CV_2 = \left(\frac{\sigma_2}{\bar{x}_2}\right) \times 100\%$.

5

Calculate $CV_2$:

$CV_2 = \left(\frac{5.3}{46.50}\right) \times 100\% = 11.40\%$.

Since $CV_1 = 5.48\%$ (height) is less than $CV_2 = 11.40\%$ (weight), height is more consistent (less varying) than weight.
