1

Calculate the mid values, deviations, and squared deviations for each class interval. Assume mean \( A = 35 \) and class width \( c = 10 \).

2

Use the standard deviation formula for grouped data:

\[

\sigma = c \times \sqrt{ \frac{\Sigma f_i u_i^2}{N} - \left( \frac{\Sigma f_i u_i}{N} \right)^2 }

\]

where \( N = 71 \), \( \Sigma f_i u_i = -30 \), and \( \Sigma f_i u_i^2 = 210 \).

3

Substitute the values into the formula:

\[

\sigma = 10 \times \sqrt{ \frac{210}{71} - \left( \frac{-30}{71} \right)^2 }

\]

\[

\sigma = 10 \times \sqrt{ 2.9577 - 0.1786 }

\]

\[

\sigma = 10 \times \sqrt{2.7791}

\]

\[

\sigma \approx 10 \times 1.667

\]

The standard deviation is approximately \( 16.67 \).
