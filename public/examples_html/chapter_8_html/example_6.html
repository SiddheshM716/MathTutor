Example 8.6

The marks scored by 10 students: 25, 29, 30, 33, 35, 37, 38, 40, 44, 48. Find the standard deviation.

Solution

Mean = 35.9 (not integer), so we use assumed mean method with A = 35, n = 10

Total Σdᵢ = 9 Σdᵢ² = 453

Standard Deviation Calculation:

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\Sigma d_i^2}{n} - \left(\frac{\Sigma d_i}{n}\right)^2} = \sqrt{\frac{453}{10} - \left(\frac{9}{10}\right)^2} \]

\[ = \sqrt{45.3 - 0.81} = \sqrt{44.49} \approx 6.67 \]

Alternative: Step Deviation Method

When data values are large, use:

\[ \sigma = c \times \sqrt{\frac{\Sigma d_i^2}{n} - \left(\frac{\Sigma d_i}{n}\right)^2} \]

Where \( d_i = \frac{x_i - A}{c} \) and c is a common factor of deviations.
