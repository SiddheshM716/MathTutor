What is the probability that a leap year selected at random will contain 53 Saturdays?

1

A leap year has 366 days, which means it consists of 52 full weeks (364 days) plus 2 extra days. The 52 Saturdays are already included in the 52 full weeks.

2

The sample space S consists of all possible combinations for the remaining 2 days:

S = {Sun-Mon, Mon-Tue, Tue-Wed, Wed-Thu, Thu-Fri, Fri-Sat, Sat-Sun}

The number of outcomes in the sample space is n(S) = 7.

3

Let A be the event of getting a 53rd Saturday. This occurs when the extra days include a Saturday:

A = {Fri-Sat, Sat-Sun}

The number of favorable outcomes is n(A) = 2.

The probability is calculated using the formula:

P(A) = n(A)/n(S) = 2/7

The probability is \(\boxed{\dfrac{2}{7}}\).
