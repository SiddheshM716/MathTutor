If \( P(A) = 0.37 \), \( P(B) = 0.42 \), \( P(A \cap B) = 0.09 \) then find \( P(A \cup B) \).

1

Identify the given probabilities:

\( P(A) = 0.37 \), \( P(B) = 0.42 \), \( P(A \cap B) = 0.09 \).

2

Use the formula for the probability of the union of two events:

\( P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) \).

3

Substitute the given values into the formula and calculate:

\( P(A \cup B) = 0.37 + 0.42 - 0.09 = 0.7 \).

\( P(A \cup B) = 0.7 \).
