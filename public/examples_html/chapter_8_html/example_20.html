Two coins are tossed together. What is the probability of getting different faces on the coins?

1

Determine the sample space when two coins are tossed together.

The sample space \( S = \{HH, HT, TH, TT\} \) and the number of outcomes \( n(S) = 4 \).

2

Define the event \( A \) as getting different faces on the coins.

The favorable outcomes for \( A \) are \( \{HT, TH\} \), so \( n(A) = 2 \).

3

Calculate the probability using the formula:

\[ P(A) = \frac{n(A)}{n(S)} \]

Substitute the values:

\[ P(A) = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2} \]

The probability of getting different faces on the coins is \( \frac{1}{2} \).
