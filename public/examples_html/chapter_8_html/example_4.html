2

We use the standard deviation formula for a population:

\[

\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\Sigma x_i^2}{n} - \left(\frac{\Sigma x_i}{n}\right)^2}

\]

3

Substitute the values and calculate:

\[

\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{623}{7} - \left(\frac{63}{7}\right)^2} = \sqrt{89 - 81} = \sqrt{8} \approx 2.83

\]

The standard deviation is approximately \( 2.83 \).
