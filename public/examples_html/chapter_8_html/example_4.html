Example 8.4

The number of televisions sold each day of a week are: 13, 8, 4, 9, 7, 12, 10. Find the standard deviation.

Total Σxᵢ = 63 Σxᵢ² = 623

Using the standard deviation formula:

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\Sigma x_i^2}{n} - \left(\frac{\Sigma x_i}{n}\right)^2} \]

\[ = \sqrt{\frac{623}{7} - \left(\frac{63}{7}\right)^2} = \sqrt{89 - 81} = \sqrt{8} \approx 2.83 \]

Alternative Mean Method

Standard deviation can also be calculated as:

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\Sigma (x_i - \overline{x})^2}{n}} \]

Where \( \overline{x} = \frac{63}{7} = 9 \) is the mean, and \( d_i = x_i - \overline{x} \) are the deviations.
