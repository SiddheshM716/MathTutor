Two dice are rolled. Find the probability that the sum of outcomes is

(i) equal to 4

(ii) greater than 10

(iii) less than 13

The sample space when rolling two dice is:

\[ S = \{ (1,1),(1,2),...,(6,6) \}; \quad n(S) = 36 \]

1

For part (i), identify the event A where the sum equals 4.

The favorable outcomes are \( A = \{(1,3),(2,2),(3,1)\} \), so \( n(A) = 3 \).

2

Use the probability formula \( P(A) = \frac{n(A)}{n(S)} \).

3

Calculate \( P(A) = \frac{3}{36} = \frac{1}{12} \).

\( \boxed{\dfrac{1}{12}} \)

Step 1: For part (ii), identify the event B where the sum is greater than 10.

The favorable outcomes are \( B = \{(5,6),(6,5),(6,6)\} \), so \( n(B) = 3 \).

Step 2: Use the probability formula \( P(B) = \frac{n(B)}{n(S)} \).

Step 3: Calculate \( P(B) = \frac{3}{36} = \frac{1}{12} \).

\( \boxed{\dfrac{1}{12}} \)

Step 1: For part (iii), identify the event C where the sum is less than 13.

Since all outcomes satisfy this, \( C = S \) and \( n(C) = 36 \).

Step 2: Use the probability formula \( P(C) = \frac{n(C)}{n(S)} \).

Step 3: Calculate \( P(C) = \frac{36}{36} = 1 \).

\( \boxed{1} \)
