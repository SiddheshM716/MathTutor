The standard deviation formula using the assumed mean method is:

\[

\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum f_i d_i^2}{N} - \left(\frac{\sum f_i d_i}{N}\right)^2}

\]

where \( N = 29 \) (total students).

3

Substitute the values into the formula:

\[

\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{240}{29} - \left(\frac{4}{29}\right)^2} = \sqrt{\frac{240 \times 29 - 16}{29 \times 29}} = \sqrt{\frac{6944}{841}} \approx 2.87

\]

The standard deviation of the test marks is \( \boxed{2.87} \).
