The amount that the children have spent for purchasing some eatables in one day trip of a school are 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40. Using step deviation method, find the standard deviation of the amount they have spent.

1

Note that all observations are divisible by 5, so we can use the step deviation method. Let the assumed mean A = 20 and n = 8.

2

Create a table to calculate the deviations (d = x - A), step deviations (v = d/5), and their squares (v²).

3

From the table, we find Σv = 4 and Σv² = 44.

4

The formula for standard deviation using step deviation method is:

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\Sigma v^2}{n} - \left(\frac{\Sigma v}{n}\right)^2} \times h \]

where h = 5 (the common divisor).

5

Substitute the values into the formula:

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{44}{8} - \left(\frac{4}{8}\right)^2} \times 5 \]

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{5.5 - 0.25} \times 5 \]

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{5.25} \times 5 \]

\[ \sigma \approx 2.29 \times 5 \]

The standard deviation is approximately 11.45.
