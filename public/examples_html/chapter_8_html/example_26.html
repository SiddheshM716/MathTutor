A flower is selected at random from a basket containing 80 yellow, 70 red, and 50 white flowers. Find the probability of selecting a yellow or red flower?

1

Calculate the total number of flowers in the basket.

Total flowers \( n(S) = 80 + 70 + 50 = 200 \).

2

Identify the number of yellow and red flowers.

Number of yellow flowers \( n(Y) = 80 \).

Number of red flowers \( n(R) = 70 \).

3

Since yellow and red flowers are mutually exclusive, use the formula for the probability of mutually exclusive events.

Formula: \( P(Y \cup R) = P(Y) + P(R) \).

4

Calculate the probability of selecting a yellow or red flower.

\( P(Y \cup R) = \frac{80}{200} + \frac{70}{200} = \frac{150}{200} = \frac{3}{4} \).

The probability of selecting a yellow or red flower is \( \frac{3}{4} \).
