If A and B are two events such that P(A) = \(\frac{1}{4}\), P(B) = \(\frac{1}{2}\) and P(A and B) = \(\frac{1}{8}\), find (i) P(A or B) (ii) P(not A and not B).

1

To find P(A or B), we use the formula for the probability of the union of two events.

2

The formula is \(P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)\).

3

Substituting the given values:

\(P(A \cup B) = \frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{8} = \frac{2}{8} + \frac{4}{8} - \frac{1}{8} = \frac{5}{8}\).

(i) \(P(A \text{ or } B) = \frac{5}{8}\).

Step 1: To find P(not A and not B), we use the complement of the union of A and B.

Step 2: The formula is \(P(\overline{A} \cap \overline{B}) = 1 - P(A \cup B)\).

Step 3: Substituting the previously calculated value:

\(P(\overline{A} \cap \overline{B}) = 1 - \frac{5}{8} = \frac{3}{8}\).

(ii) \(P(\text{not A and not B}) = \frac{3}{8}\).
