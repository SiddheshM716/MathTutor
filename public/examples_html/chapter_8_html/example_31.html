A and B are two candidates seeking admission to IIT. The probability that A gets selected is 0.5 and the probability that both A and B get selected is 0.3. Prove that the probability of B being selected is at most 0.8.

1

Given probabilities are \( P(A) = 0.5 \) and \( P(A \cap B) = 0.3 \).

2

We know that the probability of the union of two events satisfies \( P(A \cup B) \leq 1 \). The formula for the union is:

\[ P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) \]

3

Substitute the given values into the inequality:

\[ 0.5 + P(B) - 0.3 \leq 1 \]

\[ 0.2 + P(B) \leq 1 \]

\[ P(B) \leq 1 - 0.2 \]

\[ P(B) \leq 0.8 \]

The probability of B being selected is at most \( 0.8 \).
