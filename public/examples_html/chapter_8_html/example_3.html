The range of a set of data is 13.67 and the largest value is 70.08. Find the smallest value.

1

Identify the given values.

Range \( R = 13.67 \)

Largest value \( L = 70.08 \)

2

Recall the formula for range.

Range \( R = L - S \), where \( S \) is the smallest value.

3

Substitute the known values into the formula and solve for \( S \).

\( 13.67 = 70.08 - S \)

\( S = 70.08 - 13.67 \)

\( S = 56.41 \)

The smallest value is \( 56.41 \).
