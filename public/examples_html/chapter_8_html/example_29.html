In an apartment, in selecting a house from door numbers 1 to 100 randomly, find the probability of getting the door number of the house to be an even number or a perfect square number or a perfect cube number.

1

Determine the total number of houses and define the events.

Total number of houses \( n(S) = 100 \).

Let \( A \) be the event of getting an even door number: \( A = \{2, 4, 6, \dots, 100\} \), \( n(A) = 50 \).

Let \( B \) be the event of getting a perfect square door number: \( B = \{1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100\} \), \( n(B) = 10 \).

Let \( C \) be the event of getting a perfect cube door number: \( C = \{1, 8, 27, 64\} \), \( n(C) = 4 \).

2

Calculate individual probabilities.

\( P(A) = \frac{n(A)}{n(S)} = \frac{50}{100} \).

\( P(B) = \frac{n(B)}{n(S)} = \frac{10}{100} \).

\( P(C) = \frac{n(C)}{n(S)} = \frac{4}{100} \).

3

Calculate intersection probabilities.

\( P(A \cap B) = \frac{5}{100} \) (even perfect squares: 4, 16, 36, 64, 100).

\( P(B \cap C) = \frac{2}{100} \) (perfect squares and cubes: 1, 64).

\( P(A \cap C) = \frac{2}{100} \) (even perfect cubes: 8, 64).

\( P(A \cap B \cap C) = \frac{1}{100} \) (even perfect square and cube: 64).

4

Apply the inclusion-exclusion principle formula.

\[ P(A \cup B \cup C) = P(A) + P(B) + P(C) - P(A \cap B) - P(B \cap C) - P(A \cap C) + P(A \cap B \cap C) \]

\[ = \frac{50}{100} + \frac{10}{100} + \frac{4}{100} - \frac{5}{100} - \frac{2}{100} - \frac{2}{100} + \frac{1}{100} \]

\[ = \frac{56}{100} = \frac{14}{25} \].

\( \frac{14}{25} \).
