The mean and standard deviation of 15 observations are found to be 10 and 5 respectively. On rechecking it was found that one of the observation with value 8 was incorrect. Calculate the correct mean and standard deviation if the correct observation value was 23?

1

Given n = 15, \(\bar{x} = 10\), \(\sigma = 5\). First, find the original sum of observations.

\(\sum x = n \times \bar{x} = 15 \times 10 = 150\)

2

The incorrect observation is 8, and the correct value is 23. Calculate the corrected sum.

Correct total = \(150 - 8 + 23 = 165\)

3

Compute the corrected mean.

Correct mean \(\bar{x} = \frac{165}{15} = 11\)

4

Use the standard deviation formula to find the original sum of squares.

Standard deviation formula: \(\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum x^2}{n} - \left(\frac{\sum x}{n}\right)^2}\)

Given \(\sigma = 5\), we have:

\(5 = \sqrt{\frac{\sum x^2}{15} - (10)^2}\)

Squaring both sides:

\(25 = \frac{\sum x^2}{15} - 100\)

Solving for \(\sum x^2\):

\(\sum x^2 = 1875\)

5

Correct the sum of squares by replacing the incorrect observation.

Correct \(\sum x^2 = 1875 - 8^2 + 23^2 = 1875 - 64 + 529 = 2340\)

6

Calculate the corrected standard deviation.

Correct \(\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{2340}{15} - (11)^2} = \sqrt{156 - 121} = \sqrt{35} \approx 5.92\)
