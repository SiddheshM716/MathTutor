If \(1 + 2 + 3 + \dots + n = 666\), then find \(n\).

1

Recognize that the sum of the first \(n\) natural numbers is given by the formula for the sum of an arithmetic series.

2

The formula for the sum of the first \(n\) natural numbers is:

\[

S = \frac{n(n + 1)}{2}

\]

Given \(S = 666\), substitute into the formula:

\[

\frac{n(n + 1)}{2} = 666

\]

3

Multiply both sides by 2 to eliminate the denominator:

\[

n(n + 1) = 1332

\]

Expand and rearrange the equation into standard quadratic form:

\[

n^2 + n - 1332 = 0

\]

4

Factor the quadratic equation:

\[

(n + 37)(n - 36) = 0

\]

This gives the solutions:

\[

n = -37 \quad \text{or} \quad n = 36

\]

5

Since \(n\) must be a natural number, discard \(n = -37\).

\(n = 36\)
