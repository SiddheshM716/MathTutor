Find the sum of \(1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2 + \dots + 16^2\).

1

Use the formula for the sum of squares of the first \(n\) natural numbers: \(\sum_{k=1}^n k^2 = \frac{n(n + 1)(2n + 1)}{6}\).

2

Substitute \(n = 16\) into the formula.

3

Calculate the sum:

\[

\frac{16 \times (16 + 1) \times (2 \times 16 + 1)}{6} = \frac{16 \times 17 \times 33}{6} = 1496.

\]

\(1496\).

Find the sum of \(9^2 + 10^2 + \dots + 21^2\).

1

Express the sum as the difference of two sums:

\[

9^2 + 10^2 + \dots + 21^2 = \left(1^2 + 2^2 + \dots + 21^2\right) - \left(1^2 + 2^2 + \dots + 8^2\right).

\]

2

Use the sum of squares formula for both terms:

\[

\sum_{k=1}^n k^2 = \frac{n(n + 1)(2n + 1)}{6}.

\]

3

Calculate the first sum (\(n = 21\)):

\[

\frac{21 \times 22 \times 43}{6} = 3311.

\]

4

Calculate the second sum (\(n = 8\)):

\[

\frac{8 \times 9 \times 17}{6} = 204.

\]

5

Subtract the second sum from the first:

\[

3311 - 204 = 3107.

\]

\(3107\).
