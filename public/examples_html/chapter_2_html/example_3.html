Show that the square of an odd integer is of the form \(4q + 1\), for some integer \(q\).

1

Let \(x\) be any odd integer. Since any odd integer is one more than an even integer, express \(x\) as \(x = 2k + 1\), where \(k\) is an integer.

2

Square both sides of the equation to find \(x^2\):

\[

x^2 = (2k + 1)^2

\]

3

Expand the squared term:

\[

x^2 = 4k^2 + 4k + 1

\]

4

Factor out 4 from the first two terms:

\[

x^2 = 4k(k + 1) + 1

\]

5

Let \(q = k(k + 1)\), which is an integer since \(k\) is an integer. Substitute \(q\) into the equation:

\[

x^2 = 4q + 1

\]

The square of an odd integer is of the form \(4q + 1\), where \(q\) is an integer.
