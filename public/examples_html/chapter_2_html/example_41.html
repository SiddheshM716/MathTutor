Find the geometric progression whose first term and common ratios are given by:

(i) \( a = -7, r = 6 \)

(ii) \( a = 256, r = 0.5 \)

1

Recall the general form of a Geometric Progression (GP): \( a, ar, ar^2, \ldots \), where \( a \) is the first term and \( r \) is the common ratio.

2

For part (i), given \( a = -7 \) and \( r = 6 \), compute the first three terms using the GP formula.

3

Calculate the terms:

\( a = -7 \)

\( ar = -7 \times 6 = -42 \)

\( ar^2 = -7 \times 6^2 = -252 \)

The required Geometric Progression is \( -7, -42, -252, \ldots \)

Step 1: For part (ii), given \( a = 256 \) and \( r = 0.5 \), compute the first three terms using the GP formula.

Step 2: Calculate the terms:

\( a = 256 \)

\( ar = 256 \times 0.5 = 128 \)

\( ar^2 = 256 \times (0.5)^2 = 64 \)

The required Geometric Progression is \( 256, 128, 64, \ldots \)
