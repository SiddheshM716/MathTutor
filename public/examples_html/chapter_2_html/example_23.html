Check whether the following sequences are in A.P. or not?

(i) \( x + 2, 2x + 3, 3x + 4, \ldots \)

(ii) \( 2, 4, 8, 16, \ldots \)

(iii) \( 3\sqrt{2}, 5\sqrt{2}, 7\sqrt{2}, 9\sqrt{2}, \ldots \)

1

To check if a sequence is in A.P., verify whether the differences between consecutive terms are equal.

2

For sequence (i), calculate \( t_2 - t_1 \) and \( t_3 - t_2 \).

\( t_2 - t_1 = (2x + 3) - (x + 2) = x + 1 \)

\( t_3 - t_2 = (3x + 4) - (2x + 3) = x + 1 \)

3

Since \( t_2 - t_1 = t_3 - t_2 \), the differences are equal.

The sequence \( x + 2, 2x + 3, 3x + 4, \ldots \) is in A.P.

1

For sequence (ii), calculate \( t_2 - t_1 \) and \( t_3 - t_2 \).

\( t_2 - t_1 = 4 - 2 = 2 \)

\( t_3 - t_2 = 8 - 4 = 4 \)

2

Compare the differences.

3

Since \( 2 \neq 4 \), the differences are not equal.

The sequence \( 2, 4, 8, 16, \ldots \) is not in A.P.

1

For sequence (iii), calculate \( t_2 - t_1 \), \( t_3 - t_2 \), and \( t_4 - t_3 \).

\( t_2 - t_1 = 5\sqrt{2} - 3\sqrt{2} = 2\sqrt{2} \)

\( t_3 - t_2 = 7\sqrt{2} - 5\sqrt{2} = 2\sqrt{2} \)

\( t_4 - t_3 = 9\sqrt{2} - 7\sqrt{2} = 2\sqrt{2} \)

2

Compare the differences.

3

Since all differences are equal (\( 2\sqrt{2} \)), the sequence is in A.P.

The sequence \( 3\sqrt{2}, 5\sqrt{2}, 7\sqrt{2}, 9\sqrt{2}, \ldots \) is in A.P.
