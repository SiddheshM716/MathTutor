How many terms of the series \(1 + 5 + 9 + \dots\) must be taken so that their sum is 190?

1

Identify the given values.

First term \(a = 1\), common difference \(d = 5 - 1 = 4\).

We need to find \(n\) such that the sum \(S_n = 190\).

2

Use the formula for the sum of the first \(n\) terms of an arithmetic progression:

\[ S_n = \frac{n}{2} \left[ 2a + (n - 1)d \right] \]

3

Substitute the known values into the formula and solve for \(n\):

\[ \frac{n}{2} \left[ 2 \times 1 + (n - 1) \times 4 \right] = 190 \]

\[ n \left[ 4n - 2 \right] = 380 \]

\[ 2n^2 - n - 190 = 0 \]

Factor the quadratic equation:

\[ (n - 10)(2n + 19) = 0 \]

Since \(n\) must be a positive integer, we discard \(n = -\frac{19}{2}\) and take \(n = 10\).

\(10\) terms must be taken for the sum to be 190.
