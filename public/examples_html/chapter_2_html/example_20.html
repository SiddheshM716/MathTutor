Example 2.20

Find the general term for the following sequences

(i) 3,6,9,...

Solution (i) 3,6,9,...

Here the terms are multiples of 3. So the general term is

an = 3n

(ii) 1/2, 2/3, 3/4,...

We see that the numerator of nth term is n, and the denominator is one more than the numerator. Hence, an = n/(n+1), n ∈ N.

(iii) 5,−25,125,...

The terms of the sequence have + and − sign alternatively and also they are in powers of 5.

So the general term an = (−1)n+15n, n ∈ N.
