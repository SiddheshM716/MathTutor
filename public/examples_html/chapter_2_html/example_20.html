Find the general term for the following sequences:

(i) 3, 6, 9, ...

(ii) 1/2, 2/3, 3/4, ...

(iii) 5, −25, 125, ...

1

For sequence (i) 3, 6, 9, ..., observe that each term is a multiple of 3.

The general term formula for a sequence where each term is a multiple of a constant is \( a_n = kn \).

2

Here, \( k = 3 \), so the general term is \( a_n = 3n \).

(i) \( a_n = 3n \)

---

Step 1: For sequence (ii) 1/2, 2/3, 3/4, ..., notice the pattern in the numerator and denominator.

The numerator of the \( n \)-th term is \( n \), and the denominator is \( n + 1 \).

Step 2: The general term formula is \( a_n = \frac{n}{n + 1} \).

(ii) \( a_n = \frac{n}{n + 1} \)

---

Step 1: For sequence (iii) 5, −25, 125, ..., observe the alternating signs and powers of 5.

The terms can be written as \( 5^1, -5^2, 5^3, \dots \).

Step 2: The general term formula for alternating signs is \( a_n = (-1)^{n+1} \cdot 5^n \).

(iii) \( a_n = (-1)^{n+1} \cdot 5^n \)
