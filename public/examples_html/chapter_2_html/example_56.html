Find the sum of:

(i) \(1^2 + 2^2 + \cdots + 19^2\)

(ii) \(5^2 + 10^2 + 15^2 + \cdots + 105^2\)

(iii) \(15^2 + 16^2 + 17^2 + \cdots + 28^2\)

1

For part (i), use the formula for the sum of squares of the first \(n\) natural numbers:

\[ \sum_{k=1}^n k^2 = \frac{n(n + 1)(2n + 1)}{6} \]

2

Substitute \(n = 19\) into the formula:

\[ 1^2 + 2^2 + \cdots + 19^2 = \frac{19 \times 20 \times 39}{6} \]

3

Calculate the result:

\[ \frac{19 \times 20 \times 39}{6} = 2470 \]

\(2470\)

---

Step 1: For part (ii), factor out \(5^2\) and rewrite the sum:

\[ 5^2 + 10^2 + \cdots + 105^2 = 5^2(1^2 + 2^2 + \cdots + 21^2) \]

Step 2: Apply the sum of squares formula with \(n = 21\):

\[ 25 \times \frac{21 \times 22 \times 43}{6} \]

Step 3: Compute the result:

\[ \frac{25 \times 21 \times 22 \times 43}{6} = 82775 \]

\(82775\)

Step 1: For part (iii), express the sum as the difference of two sums:

\[ 15^2 + 16^2 + \cdots + 28^2 = (1^2 + 2^2 + \cdots + 28^2) - (1^2 + 2^2 + \cdots + 14^2) \]

Step 2: Apply the sum of squares formula for \(n = 28\) and \(n = 14\):

\[ \frac{28 \times 29 \times 57}{6} - \frac{14 \times 15 \times 29}{6} \]

Step 3: Simplify and compute:

\[ 7714 - 1015 = 6699 \]

\(6699\)
