Find the least positive value of \( x \) such that:

\( 67 + x \equiv 1 \ (\text{mod} \ 4) \)

\( 98 \equiv (x + 4) \ (\text{mod} \ 5) \)

1

For part (i), rewrite the congruence equation \( 67 + x \equiv 1 \ (\text{mod} \ 4) \) as \( 67 + x - 1 = 4n \), where \( n \) is an integer.

2

Simplify the equation to \( 66 + x = 4n \). This means \( 66 + x \) must be a multiple of 4.

3

The smallest positive \( x \) that satisfies this is \( x = 2 \), since \( 66 + 2 = 68 \), which is divisible by 4.

Final Answer (i): The least positive value of \( x \) is \( \boxed{2} \).

4

For part (ii), rewrite the congruence equation \( 98 \equiv (x + 4) \ (\text{mod} \ 5) \) as \( 98 - (x + 4) = 5n \), where \( n \) is an integer.

5

Simplify the equation to \( 94 - x = 5n \). This means \( 94 - x \) must be a multiple of 5.

6

The smallest positive \( x \) that satisfies this is \( x = 4 \), since \( 94 - 4 = 90 \), which is divisible by 5.

Final Answer (ii): The least positive value of \( x \) is \( \boxed{4} \).
