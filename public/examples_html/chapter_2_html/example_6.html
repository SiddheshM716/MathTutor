Find the HCF of 396, 504, 636.

1

Find the HCF of the first two numbers, 396 and 504, using Euclid’s division algorithm.

504 = 396 × 1 + 108 (remainder ≠ 0)

396 = 108 × 3 + 72 (remainder ≠ 0)

108 = 72 × 1 + 36 (remainder ≠ 0)

72 = 36 × 2 + 0 (remainder = 0)

The HCF of 396 and 504 is 36.

2

Now, find the HCF of the result (36) and the third number, 636, using Euclid’s division algorithm.

636 = 36 × 17 + 24 (remainder ≠ 0)

36 = 24 × 1 + 12 (remainder ≠ 0)

24 = 12 × 2 + 0 (remainder = 0)

The HCF of 636 and 36 is 12.

The Highest Common Factor (HCF) of 396, 504, and 636 is 12.
