Find the sum of 8 terms of the G.P. 1, -3, 9, -27...

1

Identify the given values from the problem. The first term \( a = 1 \), common ratio \( r = -3 \), and number of terms \( n = 8 \).

2

Recall the formula for the sum of the first \( n \) terms of a geometric progression (G.P.):

\[

S_n = \frac{a(1 - r^n)}{1 - r}

\]

3

Substitute the given values into the formula and compute the sum:

\[

S_8 = \frac{1(1 - (-3)^8)}{1 - (-3)} = \frac{1 - 6561}{4} = \frac{-6560}{4} = -1640

\]

The sum of the first 8 terms is \(\boxed{-1640}\).
