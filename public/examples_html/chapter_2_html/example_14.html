Solve \( 8x \equiv 1 \pmod{11} \).

1

Rewrite the congruence equation \( 8x \equiv 1 \pmod{11} \) in the form \( 8x - 1 = 11k \), where \( k \) is an integer.

2

We need to find integer values of \( k \) such that \( 8x - 1 = 11k \) holds, meaning \( x = \frac{11k + 1}{8} \) must be an integer.

3

Test values of \( k \) to find when \( \frac{11k + 1}{8} \) is an integer. For example:

\[

k = 5 \implies x = \frac{11 \times 5 + 1}{8} = \frac{56}{8} = 7

\]

\[

k = 13 \implies x = \frac{11 \times 13 + 1}{8} = \frac{144}{8} = 18

\]

The solutions are all integers of the form \( x = 7 + 11m \), where \( m \) is a non-negative integer. Examples include \( 7, 18, 29, 40, \ldots \).
