Example 2.14

Solve \( 8x \equiv 1 \pmod{11} \).

Solution: \( 8x \equiv 1 \pmod{11} \) can be written as \( 8x - 1 = 11k \), for some integer \( k \).

When we put \( k = 5, 13, 21, 29, \ldots \), then \( 11k + 1 \) is divisible by 8.

\[ \frac{11 \times 5 + 1}{8} = 7 \]

\[ \frac{11 \times 13 + 1}{8} = 18 \]

The solutions are \( 7, 18, 29, 40, \ldots \).
