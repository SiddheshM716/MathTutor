Find the first five terms of the following sequence.

\( a_1 = 1, a_2 = 1, a_n = \frac{a_{n-2}}{a_{n-1} + 3} \) for \( n > 3, n \in \mathbb{N} \).

1

The first two terms are given as \( a_1 = 1 \) and \( a_2 = 1 \).

2

For the third term \( a_3 \), use the formula \( a_n = \frac{a_{n-2}}{a_{n-1} + 3} \) with \( n = 3 \).

3

Calculate \( a_3 = \frac{a_1}{a_2 + 3} = \frac{1}{1 + 3} = \frac{1}{4} \).

4

For the fourth term \( a_4 \), use the formula with \( n = 4 \).

5

Calculate \( a_4 = \frac{a_2}{a_3 + 3} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{4} + 3} = \frac{1}{\frac{13}{4}} = \frac{4}{13} \).

6

For the fifth term \( a_5 \), use the formula with \( n = 5 \).

7

Calculate \( a_5 = \frac{a_3}{a_4 + 3} = \frac{\frac{1}{4}}{\frac{4}{13} + 3} = \frac{\frac{1}{4}}{\frac{43}{13}} = \frac{13}{172} \).

The first five terms are \( 1, 1, \frac{1}{4}, \frac{4}{13}, \frac{13}{172} \).
