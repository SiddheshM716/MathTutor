Example 2.22

Find the first five terms of the following sequence.

\( a_1 = 1, a_2 = 1, a_n = \frac{a_{n-2}}{a_{n-1} + 3} \) for \( n > 3, n \in \mathbb{N} \).

Solution:

The first two terms of this sequence are given by \( a_1 = 1, a_2 = 1 \). The third term \( a_3 \) depends on the first and second terms.

\( a_3 = \frac{a_1}{a_2 + 3} = \frac{1}{1 + 3} = \frac{1}{4} \)

Similarly, the fourth term \( a_4 \) depends upon \( a_2 \) and \( a_3 \).

\( a_4 = \frac{a_2}{a_3 + 3} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{4} + 3} = \frac{1}{\frac{13}{4}} = \frac{4}{13} \)

In the same way, the fifth term \( a_5 \) can be calculated as

\( a_5 = \frac{a_3}{a_4 + 3} = \frac{\frac{1}{4}}{\frac{4}{13} + 3} = \frac{\frac{1}{4}}{\frac{43}{13}} = \frac{13}{172} \)

Therefore, the first five terms of the sequence are \( 1, 1, \frac{1}{4}, \frac{4}{13}, \frac{13}{172} \).
