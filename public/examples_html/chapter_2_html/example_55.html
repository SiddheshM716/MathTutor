Example 2.55

Find the sum of:

(i) \( 1 + 3 + 5 + \cdots \) to 40 terms

(ii) \( 2 + 4 + 6 + \cdots + 80 \)

(iii) \( 1 + 3 + 5 + \cdots + 55 \)

Solution:

(i) \( 1 + 3 + 5 + \cdots \) to 40 terms = \( 40^2 = 1600 \)

(ii) \( 2 + 4 + 6 + \cdots + 80 = 2(1 + 2 + 3 + \cdots + 40) = 2 \times \frac{40 \times (40 + 1)}{2} = 1640 \)

(iii) \( 1 + 3 + 5 + \cdots + 55 \)

Here the number of terms is not given. Now we have to find the number of terms using the formula, \( n = \frac{(l - a)}{d} + 1 \Rightarrow n = \frac{(55 - 1)}{2} + 1 = 28 \)

Therefore, \( 1 + 3 + 5 + \cdots + 55 = (28)^2 = 784 \)
