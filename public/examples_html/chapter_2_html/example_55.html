Find the sum of:

(i) \( 1 + 3 + 5 + \cdots \) to 40 terms

(ii) \( 2 + 4 + 6 + \cdots + 80 \)

(iii) \( 1 + 3 + 5 + \cdots + 55 \)

1

For part (i), recognize that the series \( 1 + 3 + 5 + \cdots \) is an arithmetic series of the first 40 odd numbers.

The sum of the first \( n \) odd numbers is given by the formula \( n^2 \).

2

Apply the formula with \( n = 40 \).

3

Calculate \( 40^2 = 1600 \).

\( 1600 \)

---

Step 1: For part (ii), observe that the series \( 2 + 4 + 6 + \cdots + 80 \) is an arithmetic series of even numbers.

This can be rewritten as \( 2(1 + 2 + 3 + \cdots + 40) \).

Step 2: Use the formula for the sum of the first \( n \) natural numbers: \( \frac{n(n + 1)}{2} \).

Step 3: Substitute \( n = 40 \) and multiply by 2:

\( 2 \times \frac{40 \times 41}{2} = 1640 \).

\( 1640 \)

---

Step 1: For part (iii), the series \( 1 + 3 + 5 + \cdots + 55 \) is an arithmetic series of odd numbers, but the number of terms is unknown.

Step 2: Find the number of terms \( n \) using the formula for an arithmetic sequence:

\( n = \frac{(l - a)}{d} + 1 \), where \( l = 55 \), \( a = 1 \), and \( d = 2 \).

Step 3: Calculate \( n = \frac{(55 - 1)}{2} + 1 = 28 \).

Now, use the sum formula for the first \( n \) odd numbers: \( n^2 \).

Step 4: Compute \( 28^2 = 784 \).

\( 784 \)
