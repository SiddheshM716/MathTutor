A mother divides ₹207 into three parts such that the amounts are in A.P. and gives it to her three children. The product of the two least amounts that the children had is 4623. Find the amount received by each child.

1

Let the amounts received by the three children be in the form of an arithmetic progression (A.P.), given by \(a - d\), \(a\), \(a + d\).

2

The sum of the amounts is ₹207. Using the formula for the sum of an A.P., we have:

\[

(a - d) + a + (a + d) = 207

\]

3

Simplify the equation:

\[

3a = 207 \Rightarrow a = 69

\]

4

It is given that the product of the two least amounts is 4623. Using the formula for the product:

\[

(a - d)a = 4623

\]

5

Substitute \(a = 69\) into the equation:

\[

(69 - d) \times 69 = 4623

\]

6

Solve for \(d\):

\[

69 - d = \frac{4623}{69} \Rightarrow 69 - d = 67 \Rightarrow d = 2

\]

The amounts given to the three children are ₹67, ₹69, and ₹71.
