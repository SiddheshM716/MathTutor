The 13

th

term of an A.P. is 3 and the sum of first 13 terms is 234. Find the common difference and the sum of first 21 terms.

1

Given the 13

th

term \( t_{13} = 3 \). Using the formula for the \( n \)-th term of an A.P., \( t_n = a + (n-1)d \), we have:

\[ t_{13} = a + 12d = 3 \quad \text{(1)} \]

2

The sum of the first 13 terms \( S_{13} = 234 \). Using the sum formula \( S_n = \frac{n}{2} [2a + (n-1)d] \), we get:

\[ S_{13} = \frac{13}{2} [2a + 12d] = 234 \]

\[ 2a + 12d = 36 \quad \text{(2)} \]

3

Solve equations (1) and (2) simultaneously. From equation (1), \( a = 3 - 12d \). Substitute into equation (2):

\[ 2(3 - 12d) + 12d = 36 \]

\[ 6 - 24d + 12d = 36 \]

\[ -12d = 30 \]

\[ d = -\frac{5}{2} \]

Now substitute \( d = -\frac{5}{2} \) back into equation (1):

\[ a + 12\left(-\frac{5}{2}\right) = 3 \]

\[ a - 30 = 3 \]

\[ a = 33 \]

4

Find the sum of the first 21 terms \( S_{21} \) using the sum formula:

\[ S_{21} = \frac{21}{2} \left[ 2a + 20d \right] \]

\[ S_{21} = \frac{21}{2} \left[ 2(33) + 20\left(-\frac{5}{2}\right) \right] \]

\[ S_{21} = \frac{21}{2} \left[ 66 - 50 \right] \]

\[ S_{21} = \frac{21}{2} \times 16 = 168 \]

The common difference is \( -\frac{5}{2} \), and the sum of the first 21 terms is \( 168 \).
