In an A.P., sum of four consecutive terms is 28 and the sum of their squares is 276. Find the four numbers.

1

Let the four terms be in the form $(a - 3d)$, $(a - d)$, $(a + d)$, and $(a + 3d)$.

2

Sum of the four terms is 28. The formula for the sum is:

$$(a - 3d) + (a - d) + (a + d) + (a + 3d) = 28$$

3

Simplify the equation:

$$4a = 28 \Rightarrow a = 7$$

4

Sum of their squares is 276. The formula is:

$$(a - 3d)^2 + (a - d)^2 + (a + d)^2 + (a + 3d)^2 = 276$$

5

Expand and simplify:

$$4a^2 + 20d^2 = 276$$

Substitute $a = 7$:

$$4(7)^2 + 20d^2 = 276 \Rightarrow 196 + 20d^2 = 276$$

6

Solve for $d$:

$$20d^2 = 80 \Rightarrow d^2 = 4 \Rightarrow d = \pm 2$$

7

Calculate the four numbers for $d = 2$:

$$7 - 3(2) = 1, \quad 7 - 2 = 5, \quad 7 + 2 = 9, \quad 7 + 3(2) = 13$$

The four consecutive terms of the A.P. are $\boxed{1, 5, 9, 13}$.
