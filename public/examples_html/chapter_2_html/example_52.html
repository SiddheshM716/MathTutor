Example 2.52

Find the least positive integer \( n \) such that \( 1 + 6 + 6^2 + \dots + 6^n > 5000 \).

We have to find the least number of terms for which the sum must be greater than 5000.

That is, to find the least value of \( n \) such that \( S_n > 5000 \).

We have, \( S_n = \frac{a(r^n - 1)}{r - 1} = \frac{1(6^n - 1)}{6 - 1} = \frac{6^n - 1}{5} \).

\( S_n > 5000 \Rightarrow \frac{6^n - 1}{5} > 5000 \Rightarrow 6^n - 1 > 25000 \Rightarrow 6^n > 25001 \).

\( 6^5 = 7776 \) and \( 6^6 = 46656 \).

The least positive value of \( n \) is 6 such that \( 1 + 6 + 6^2 + \dots + 6^n > 5000 \).
