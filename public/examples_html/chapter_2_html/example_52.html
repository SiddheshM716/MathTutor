Find the least positive integer \( n \) such that \( 1 + 6 + 6^2 + \dots + 6^n > 5000 \).

1

Recognize that the given series is a geometric series with first term \( a = 1 \) and common ratio \( r = 6 \).

2

Use the formula for the sum of a geometric series:

\[ S_n = \frac{a(r^n - 1)}{r - 1} \]

3

Substitute the known values into the formula:

\[ S_n = \frac{1(6^n - 1)}{6 - 1} = \frac{6^n - 1}{5} \]

4

Set up the inequality \( S_n > 5000 \) and solve for \( n \):

\[ \frac{6^n - 1}{5} > 5000 \]

\[ 6^n - 1 > 25000 \]

\[ 6^n > 25001 \]

5

Calculate powers of 6 to find the smallest \( n \) satisfying the inequality:

\[ 6^5 = 7776 \]

\[ 6^6 = 46656 \]

The least positive integer \( n \) is \( \boxed{6} \).
