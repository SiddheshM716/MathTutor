A person saved money every year, half as much as he could in the previous year. If he had totally saved ₹7875 in 6 years, then how much did he save in the first year?

1

Identify the given information. The total amount saved in 6 years is \( S_6 = 7875 \), and the ratio of savings each year is \( r = \frac{1}{2} \).

2

Use the formula for the sum of a finite geometric series:

\[

S_n = \frac{a(1 - r^n)}{1 - r}

\]

where \( a \) is the first term (savings in the first year), \( r \) is the common ratio, and \( n \) is the number of years.

3

Substitute the known values into the formula:

\[

7875 = \frac{a \left(1 - \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^6\right)}{1 - \frac{1}{2}}

\]

4

Simplify the equation:

\[

7875 = \frac{a \left(1 - \frac{1}{64}\right)}{\frac{1}{2}} = \frac{a \left(\frac{63}{64}\right)}{\frac{1}{2}} = a \left(\frac{63}{32}\right)

\]

5

Solve for \( a \):

\[

a = 7875 \times \frac{32}{63} = 4000

\]

The amount saved in the first year is ₹4000.
