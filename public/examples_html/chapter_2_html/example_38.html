The houses of a street are numbered from 1 to 49. Senthil’s house is numbered such that the sum of numbers of the houses prior to Senthil’s house is equal to the sum of numbers of the houses following Senthil’s house. Find Senthil’s house number?

1

Let Senthil’s house number be \( z \). According to the problem, the sum of the house numbers before Senthil’s house equals the sum of the house numbers after Senthil’s house.

2

Express the given condition mathematically:

\[ 1 + 2 + 3 + \dots + (z - 1) = (z + 1) + (z + 2) + \dots + 49 \]

3

Rewrite the right-hand side using the sum of the first 49 natural numbers minus the sum of the first \( z \) natural numbers:

\[ 1 + 2 + 3 + \dots + (z - 1) = [1 + 2 + 3 + \dots + 49] - [1 + 2 + 3 + \dots + z] \]

4

Apply the formula for the sum of the first \( n \) natural numbers, \( \frac{n(n + 1)}{2} \):

\[ \frac{z(z - 1)}{2} = \frac{49 \times 50}{2} - \frac{z(z + 1)}{2} \]

5

Simplify the equation:

\[ z^2 - z = 2450 - z^2 - z \]

6

Combine like terms and solve for \( z \):

\[ 2z^2 = 2450 \]

\[ z^2 = 1225 \]

\[ z = 35 \]

Senthil’s house number is \( 35 \).
