Find the first term of a G.P. in which \( S_6 = 4095 \) and \( r = 4 \).

1

Identify the given values. The common ratio \( r = 4 \) (which is greater than 1), and the sum of the first 6 terms \( S_6 = 4095 \).

2

Use the formula for the sum of the first \( n \) terms of a G.P. when \( r > 1 \):

\[ S_n = a \frac{r^n - 1}{r - 1} \]

3

Substitute the known values into the formula:

\[ 4095 = a \frac{4^6 - 1}{4 - 1} \]

4

Calculate \( 4^6 \):

\[ 4^6 = 4096 \]

5

Substitute back and simplify:

\[ 4095 = a \frac{4096 - 1}{3} \]

\[ 4095 = a \frac{4095}{3} \]

\[ 4095 = a \times 1365 \]

6

Solve for \( a \):

\[ a = \frac{4095}{1365} \]

\[ a = 3 \]

The first term \( a = 3 \).
