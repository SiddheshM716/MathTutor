Find the sum of all natural numbers between 300 and 600 which are divisible by 7.

1

Identify the sequence of numbers between 300 and 600 divisible by 7.

The numbers are 301, 308, 315, ..., 595. This forms an arithmetic progression (A.P.).

2

Determine the first term (\( a \)), common difference (\( d \)), and last term (\( l \)) of the A.P.

Given:

\( a = 301 \)

\( d = 7 \)

\( l = 595 \)

3

Calculate the number of terms (\( n \)) in the A.P. using the formula:

\[ n = \frac{l - a}{d} + 1 \]

Substituting the values:

\[ n = \frac{595 - 301}{7} + 1 = \frac{294}{7} + 1 = 42 + 1 = 43 \]

4

Compute the sum (\( S_n \)) of the A.P. using the formula:

\[ S_n = \frac{n}{2}(a + l) \]

Substituting the values:

\[ S_n = \frac{43}{2}(301 + 595) = \frac{43}{2} \times 896 = 43 \times 448 = 19264 \]

The sum of all natural numbers between 300 and 600 divisible by 7 is \(\boxed{19264}\).
