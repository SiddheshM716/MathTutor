The general term of a sequence is defined as

\( a_n = \begin{cases}

n(n + 3) & \text{if } n \text{ is odd} \\

n^2 + 1 & \text{if } n \text{ is even}

\end{cases} \)

Find the eleventh and eighteenth terms.

1

Identify whether the term number is odd or even. For \( a_{11} \), 11 is odd. For \( a_{18} \), 18 is even.

2

For odd \( n \), use the formula \( a_n = n(n + 3) \). For even \( n \), use the formula \( a_n = n^2 + 1 \).

3

Calculate \( a_{11} \) using the odd formula:

\[ a_{11} = 11(11 + 3) = 11 \times 14 = 154 \]

4

Calculate \( a_{18} \) using the even formula:

\[ a_{18} = 18^2 + 1 = 324 + 1 = 325 \]

The eleventh term is \( 154 \) and the eighteenth term is \( 325 \).
