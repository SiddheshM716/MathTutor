Find the greatest number that will divide 445 and 572 leaving remainders 4 and 5 respectively.

1

Subtract the remainders from the given numbers to find the numbers that are exactly divisible by the required greatest number.

445 − 4 = 441

572 − 5 = 567

2

The required greatest number is the HCF of 441 and 567. We will use Euclid’s Division Algorithm to find the HCF.

3

Apply Euclid’s Division Algorithm:

567 = 441 × 1 + 126

441 = 126 × 3 + 63

126 = 63 × 2 + 0

Since the remainder is now 0, the HCF is the last non-zero remainder, which is 63.

The greatest number is \boxed{63}.
