In a Geometric progression, the 4

th

term is \(\frac{8}{243}\) and the 7

th

term is \(\frac{64}{729}\). Find the Geometric Progression.

1

Write the expressions for the 4

th

and 7

th

terms using the formula for the \(n\)-th term of a GP, \(t_n = ar^{n-1}\).

For the 4

th

term:

\(t_4 = ar^3 = \frac{8}{243}\) ...(1)

For the 7

th

term:

\(t_7 = ar^6 = \frac{64}{729}\) ...(2)

2

Divide equation (2) by equation (1) to eliminate \(a\) and solve for \(r\).

\(\frac{ar^6}{ar^3} = \frac{\frac{64}{729}}{\frac{8}{243}}\)

Simplify:

\(r^3 = \frac{64}{729} \times \frac{243}{8} = \frac{8}{27}\)

Take the cube root:

\(r = \frac{2}{3}\)

3

Substitute \(r = \frac{2}{3}\) back into equation (1) to find \(a\).

\(a \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 = \frac{8}{243}\)

Calculate \(\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3\):

\(a \cdot \frac{8}{27} = \frac{8}{243}\)

Solve for \(a\):

\(a = \frac{8}{243} \times \frac{27}{8} = \frac{27}{8}\)

The Geometric Progression is \(\frac{27}{8}, \frac{9}{8}, \frac{3}{8}, \dots\)
