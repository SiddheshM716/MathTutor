Find the next three terms of the sequences:

(i) 2, 6, 10, 14,...

(ii) 5, 2, -1, -4,...

(iii) 1, 0.1, 0.01,...

1

For sequence (i) 2, 6, 10, 14,..., observe that each term increases by 4.

2

The next three terms can be found by adding 4 to the last term repeatedly.

3

\( a_1 = 14 + 4 = 18 \)

\( a_2 = 18 + 4 = 22 \)

\( a_3 = 22 + 4 = 26 \)

The next three terms are 18, 22, 26.

Step 1: For sequence (ii) 5, 2, -1, -4,..., observe that each term decreases by 3.

Step 2: The next three terms can be found by subtracting 3 from the last term repeatedly.

Step 3:

\( a_1 = -4 - 3 = -7 \)

\( a_2 = -7 - 3 = -10 \)

\( a_3 = -10 - 3 = -13 \)

The next three terms are -7, -10, -13.

Step 1: For sequence (iii) 1, 0.1, 0.01,..., observe that each term is divided by 10.

Step 2: The next three terms can be found by dividing the last term by 10 repeatedly.

Step 3:

\( a_1 = 0.01 / 10 = 0.001 \)

\( a_2 = 0.001 / 10 = 0.0001 \)

\( a_3 = 0.0001 / 10 = 0.00001 \)

The next three terms are 0.001, 0.0001, 0.00001.
