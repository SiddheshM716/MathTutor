Find the sum to n terms of the series \(5 + 55 + 555 + \dots\)

1

The series is neither arithmetic nor geometric. We can factor out 5 and rewrite the series as:

\[ 5 + 55 + 555 + \dots + n \text{ terms} = 5[1 + 11 + 111 + \dots + n \text{ terms}] \]

2

Multiply and divide by 9 to express the terms in a different form:

\[ = \frac{5}{9}[9 + 99 + 999 + \dots + n \text{ terms}] \]

3

Rewrite each term as a power of 10 minus 1:

\[ = \frac{5}{9}[(10 - 1) + (100 - 1) + (1000 - 1) + \dots + n \text{ terms}] \]

4

Separate the terms into two parts:

\[ = \frac{5}{9}[(10 + 100 + 1000 + \dots + n \text{ terms}) - n] \]

5

The first part is a geometric series. Use the formula for the sum of a geometric series:

\[ 10 + 100 + 1000 + \dots + n \text{ terms} = \frac{10(10^n - 1)}{10 - 1} \]

6

Substitute the geometric series sum back into the expression:

\[ = \frac{5}{9}\left[\frac{10(10^n - 1)}{9} - n\right] \]

The sum to \(n\) terms of the series is:

\[ \frac{5}{9}\left[\frac{10(10^n - 1)}{9} - n\right] \]
