A man starts his journey from Chennai to Delhi by train. He starts at 22.30 hours on Wednesday. If it takes 32 hours of travelling time and assuming that the train is not late, when will he reach Delhi?

1

Note the starting time and travelling time. The journey begins at 22:30 hours on Wednesday and takes 32 hours.

2

Use modulo 24 to calculate the arrival time since the clock cycles every 24 hours. The formula is:

\[ \text{Arrival time} = (\text{Starting time} + \text{Travelling time}) \mod 24 \]

3

Calculate the total time:

\[ 22.30 + 32 = 54.30 \]

Now apply modulo 24:

\[ 54.30 \mod 24 = 6.30 \]

This is because \( 32 = (1 \times 24) + 8 \), so the excess beyond 24 hours is 8 hours.

He will reach Delhi on Friday at 6.30 hours.
