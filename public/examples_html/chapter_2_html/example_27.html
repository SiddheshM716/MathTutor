Determine the general term of an A.P. whose 7

th

term is -1 and 16

th

term is 17.

1

Let the A.P. have first term \( a \) and common difference \( d \). The \( n \)-th term of an A.P. is given by \( t_n = a + (n-1)d \).

2

Given \( t_7 = -1 \) and \( t_{16} = 17 \), we write the equations:

\[

a + 6d = -1 \quad \text{(1)}

\]

\[

a + 15d = 17 \quad \text{(2)}

\]

3

Subtract equation (1) from equation (2):

\[

(a + 15d) - (a + 6d) = 17 - (-1)

\]

\[

9d = 18 \implies d = 2

\]

4

Substitute \( d = 2 \) into equation (1):

\[

a + 6(2) = -1 \implies a + 12 = -1 \implies a = -13

\]

5

The general term \( t_n \) is:

\[

t_n = a + (n-1)d = -13 + (n-1) \times 2 = 2n - 15

\]

The general term of the A.P. is \( \boxed{2n - 15} \).
