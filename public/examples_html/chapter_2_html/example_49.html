Find the sum of the infinite G.P. \( 3 + 1 + \frac{1}{3} + \dots \)

1

Identify the first term (\( a \)) and common ratio (\( r \)) of the geometric progression.

Here, \( a = 3 \) and \( r = \frac{1}{3} \).

2

Use the formula for the sum of an infinite geometric series:

\[ S = \frac{a}{1 - r} \]

3

Substitute the values of \( a \) and \( r \) into the formula and simplify:

\[ S = \frac{3}{1 - \frac{1}{3}} = \frac{3}{\frac{2}{3}} = \frac{9}{2} \]

The sum of the infinite G.P. is \( \frac{9}{2} \).
