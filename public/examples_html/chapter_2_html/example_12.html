Determine the value of \( d \) such that \( 15 \equiv 3 \pmod{d} \).

1

The congruence \( 15 \equiv 3 \pmod{d} \) implies that \( d \) divides the difference \( 15 - 3 \).

2

The formula for congruence is \( a \equiv b \pmod{d} \) if and only if \( d \) divides \( a - b \).

3

Calculate the difference \( 15 - 3 = 12 \). So, \( d \) must be a divisor of 12.

4

The positive divisors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, and 12. Since the remainder (3) must be less than \( d \), we exclude 1, 2, and 3.

The possible values for \( d \) are \( \boxed{4} \), \( \boxed{6} \), and \( \boxed{12} \).
