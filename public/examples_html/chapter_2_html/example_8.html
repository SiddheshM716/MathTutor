Can the number \(6^n\), \(n\) being a natural number, end with the digit 5? Give reason for your answer.

1

Express \(6^n\) in terms of its prime factors.

\(6^n = (2 \times 3)^n = 2^n \times 3^n\).

2

Analyze the parity (even/odd nature) of \(6^n\).

Since \(2\) is a factor of \(6^n\), the number \(6^n\) is always even.

3

Compare with the property of numbers ending with 5.

Any number ending with the digit 5 must be odd, as it is divisible by 5 but not by 2.

No, \(6^n\) cannot end with the digit 5 because it is always even, whereas numbers ending with 5 are odd.
