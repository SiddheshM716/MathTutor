Example 2.39

The sum of first n, 2n and 3n terms of an A.P. are S1, S2, and S3 respectively. Prove that S3 = 3(S2 − S1).

Solution If S1, S2, and S3 are sum of first n, 2n and 3n terms of an A.P. respectively then

S1 = n[2a + (n − 1)d], S2 = 2n[2a + (2n − 1)d], S3 = 3n[2a + (3n − 1)d]

Consider, S2 − S1 = 2n[2a + (2n − 1)d] − n[2a + (n − 1)d]

S2 − S1 = n[[4a + 2(2n − 1)d] − [2a + (n − 1)d]]

S3 − S1 = 3n[2a + (3n − 1)d]

3(S2 − S1) = 3n[2a + (3n − 1)d]

3(S2 − S1) = S3
