The product of three consecutive terms of a Geometric Progression is 343 and their sum is $\frac{91}{3}$. Find the three terms.

1

Let the three consecutive terms of the Geometric Progression be $\frac{a}{r}$, $a$, and $ar$.

2

The product of the terms is given as 343. Using the formula for the product of terms:

\[

\frac{a}{r} \times a \times ar = 343

\]

3

Simplify the equation:

\[

a^3 = 343 \implies a = 7

\]

4

The sum of the terms is given as $\frac{91}{3}$. Using the formula for the sum of terms:

\[

\frac{a}{r} + a + ar = \frac{91}{3}

\]

5

Substitute $a = 7$ into the equation:

\[

\frac{7}{r} + 7 + 7r = \frac{91}{3}

\]

6

Multiply through by $3r$ to eliminate denominators:

\[

21 + 21r + 21r^2 = 91r

\]

7

Rearrange the equation:

\[

21r^2 - 70r + 21 = 0 \implies 3r^2 - 10r + 3 = 0

\]

8

Solve the quadratic equation:

\[

(3r - 1)(r - 3) = 0 \implies r = \frac{1}{3} \text{ or } r = 3

\]

The three terms are either $\frac{7}{3}, 7, 21$ (if $r = 3$) or $21, 7, \frac{7}{3}$ (if $r = \frac{1}{3}$).
