In an A.P. the sum of first n terms is \( \frac{5n^2 + 3n}{2} \). Find the 17

th

term.

1

The 17

th

term (\( t_{17} \)) can be obtained by subtracting the sum of the first 16 terms (\( S_{16} \)) from the sum of the first 17 terms (\( S_{17} \)).

2

First, calculate \( S_{17} \) using the given formula \( S_n = \frac{5n^2 + 3n}{2} \).

3

Substitute \( n = 17 \) into the formula:

\[

S_{17} = \frac{5 \times 17^2 + 3 \times 17}{2} = \frac{1445 + 51}{2} = 748

\]

4

Next, calculate \( S_{16} \) using the same formula.

5

Substitute \( n = 16 \) into the formula:

\[

S_{16} = \frac{5 \times 16^2 + 3 \times 16}{2} = \frac{1280 + 48}{2} = 664

\]

6

Now, find \( t_{17} \) by subtracting \( S_{16} \) from \( S_{17} \):

\[

t_{17} = S_{17} - S_{16} = 748 - 664 = 84

\]

The 17

th

term is \( \boxed{84} \).
