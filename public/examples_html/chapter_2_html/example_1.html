We have 34 cakes. Each box can hold 5 cakes only. How many boxes do we need to pack, and how many cakes are left unpacked?

1

Understand the problem. We need to divide 34 cakes into boxes, each holding 5 cakes, and find the number of full boxes and leftover cakes.

2

Use the division formula:

\[ a = b \times q + r \]

where:

- \( a = 34 \) (total cakes),

- \( b = 5 \) (cakes per box),

- \( q \) (number of boxes),

- \( r \) (leftover cakes).

3

Perform the division:

\[ 34 \div 5 = 6 \) with a remainder of \( 4 \).

This gives:

\[ 34 = 5 \times 6 + 4 \]

We need 6 boxes, and 4 cakes will remain unpacked.
